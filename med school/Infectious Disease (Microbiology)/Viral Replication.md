1) Attach to host cells
2) Enter host cells
3) Genome release
4) mRNA synthesis to make early proteins
	- Non-structural proteins like DNA binding proteins or viral encoded polymerase
5) Genomic replication
	- DNA dependent or RNA dependent DNA polymerase
6) Late protein synthesis
	- Structural proteins and others needed for genome assembly
7) Virion release -- non enveloped through lysis and enveloped through budding
### DNA Virus
- Site of replication = [[nucleus]]
- Converted to mRNA through DNA dependent RNA polymerase II
	- Early genes have DNA binding proteins for genome replication
- mRNA must have 3' polyA tail and 5' methyl cap, introns are removed
- Replication is done through DNA dependent DNA polymerase of host or DNA polymerase of virus
	- **Primer** is needed to continue replication
	
>[!Exception] [[Pox virus]]
>Replication in cytoplasm, not nucleus so has own RNA polymerase and DNA polymerase

### RNA Virus
- Replicate and mRNA synthesis in cytoplasm
- **Exception: orthomyxovirus and retrovirus**
- Make their own RNA dependent RNA polymerase

>[!Clinical]
> DNA polymerase = site of antiviral drugs
> RdRp = potential site for drug development
> Many viruses have [[inclusions]] at replication site where viral proteins and nucleic acids accumulate
### Genetic Variations
- Antigenic Drift
	- Minor mutation
	- Cause antibodies not to recognize -- epidemics
- Reassortment
	- Only viruses with **segmented** genomes
	- Exchange of segments when 2 infect same cell
	- Can give rise to Novel strain
	- Antigenic Shift
		- Causes pandemics
- Recombination
	- 2 viruses related infect cell at same time
	- Genetic material mixes and combines
- Complementation
	- 2 different viruses infect same cell
	- One gives missing function (related to replication) to the other, so protein is supplemented from other virus
	- **NO CHANGE to GENOME**
	- Ex: Hep D virus
- Phenotypic Mixing
	- 2 different viruses share their **surface proteins**
	- Outside proteins don't match inside genome, so mixed particles called pseudoviruses
	
>[!Example] [[Influenza Virus]]
>Hemagglutinin and neuraminidase as surface proteins
>Mutation in protein = antigenic drift -- no longer inhibited
