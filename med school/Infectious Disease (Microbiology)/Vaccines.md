- Allow for B cell and T cell response without infection
- Protection through immune memory
- Allow for [[immunization]], can be passive or active
#### Live Attenuated
- Pathogens modified to be less viral
- Strong **cell-mediated response**
- Slight risk of infection
- If given < 1 month, maternal antibodies may kill pathogens

>[!List] 
>MMR
>Influenza
>Polio (OPV)
>Rotavirus
>[[HHV3|Varicella-Zoster virus]] (chickenpox / shingles)
>Yellow fever
>Oral live typhoid vaccine
>Bacille Calmette Guerin (BCG)
#### Killed
- Pathogen killed but antibodies intact
- Strong humoral response but weaker immune response
- No infection risk

>[!List]
>Hepatitis A
>Influenza
>Japanese encephalitis virus
>Poliovirus (IPV)
>Rabies
>Tick-borne encephalitis
#### [[Conjugated Vaccines]]
#### Oral
- GI mucosal immunity through [[IgA]]
- Oral polio, rotavirus
#### Intramuscular
- Tissue response – [[IgG]]
