- R1 group determines antibacterial activity, b-lactamase release, stability in acid
- R2 determines pharmacokinetics
### Mechanisms
- Bind to PBPs and inhibit transpeptidase cross-linking
- No activity against MRSAs, Listeria, or Enterococci
- More stable to pH changes
### Classification
1) First gen
	- Ex: Cefazolin
	- Strongest [[gram positive]]
	- No MRSA or enterococcal activity
2) Second gen
	- Ex: Cefuroxime
	- Slightly more broad gram negative
	- Use for respiratory tract infections, sinusitis, otitis media
3) Third gen
	- Ex: Ceftriaxone, ceftazidime
	- More effective against gram negative
	- Some cross BBB -- used in meningitis 
4) Fourth gen
	- Ex: Cefepime
	- Most broad spectrum (gram + and - including [[pseudomonas]])
	- Crosses BBB -> use for [[bacterial meningitis]]

- Anti MRSA Cephalosporin (Ceftaroline)
	- **ONLY** b-lactam that works against MRSA
	- **Binds to PBP2a** to overcome resistance
	- Not active against ESBL producing
- Cephalosporin + [[B-Lactamase Inhibitors]] Combination
	- Ceftolozane-tazobactam
	- Ceftazidime-avibactam
	- **Active against ESBL producing bacteria**
### Adverse Effects
- Hypersensitivity
	- [[Anaphylaxis]], rash, edema, nephritis, hemolytic anemia
	- Cross reaction with penicillin (~2%)
- Secondary Reaction
	- [[C. Diff|C. difficile]] associated colitis
- GI Irritation
	- **Minimize this by taking with food**
- Local Site Reaction
- Seizures (high dose and liver issue patients)
