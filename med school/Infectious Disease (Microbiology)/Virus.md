*Not a living cell, just an infectious particle*
- Majority have an affinity to a specific tissue -- tissue tropism
- Host range depends on host attachment sites
- No energy or protein synthesis -- parasite
- No replication through division
#### Components
- RNA or DNA genome
	- Single or double stranded 
		- All DNA is ds except [[parvoviruses]]
		- All RNA is ss except reoviruses
	- Circular, linear, segmented, helical
		- All DNA is linear except papilloma-polyoma virus and hedadnaviral genome (circular)
- Nucleocapsid
	- Protein coat around nuclear material
	- Provides **shape** to virus
- Viral Envelope
	- Presence or absence separates categories
	- Protects genetic material from environment
	- Helps attach virus to cell surface receptor
	- Can have spikes on envelope

>[!Example] GP120 envelope glycoprotein of [[HIV]] helps bind to CD4 cells through coreceptor called CCR5

- Can have presence of specific enzymes
	- Ex: HIV and Hep B have reverse transcriptase
#### Categories
- Type of Genome (DNA vs RNA)
- Envelope presence
	- Nonenveloped
		- Sensitive to heat, acid, drying
		- Released through [[lysic cycle]]
		- Still **infectious** upon drying
		- Survives in GI
		- Antibody response
	- Enveloped
		- Resistant to heat, acid, drying
		- Released through [[Lysogenic Cycle|lysogenic cycle]] and lysic cycle
		- Loses infectious ability upon drying
		- No survival in GI
		- Antibody and complement response
- Shapes
	- Helical (ex: [[rabies]])
	- Icosahedron (almost spherical under microscope)
	- Complex ([[bacteriophages]])
