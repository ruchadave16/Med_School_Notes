### Etiology
*[[Gram negative]]pleomorphic coccobacillus*
- Oxidase positive
- Chocolate agar growth
- Need X factor (heme) and V factor (NAD)
### Pathophysiology
- Capsule (Polyribitol phosphate)
	- Type B = most virulent

### Clinical Features
- Capsulated strains = more virulent
	- Meningitis
	- Epiglottitis
- Noncapsulated strains (NTHi) = less
	- Sinusitis
	- Otitis media
	- Chronic bronchitis
### Diagnosis
- Hib vaccine to prevent
### Treatment
- [[Augmentin]]
- 3rd gen [[Cephalosporins|cephalosporin]]

### Complications
- Short-term
- Long-term
