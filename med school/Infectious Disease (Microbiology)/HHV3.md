#### Mechanism
- Transmission: respiratory droplets (varicella)
- zoster is a reacquired disease that only happens if you had it when a child

- Infection starts in [[oropharynx]]
	- Spreads through blood to liver and other internal organs, and then skin
	- No longer infectious once lesions scab over
- Latent in sensory ganglion, reactivates as zoster
#### Symptoms
- [[Chickenpox]] in children
- [[Shingles]] in adults above 50

#### Diagnosis
- PCR
- Fluorescent antibody test
	- CPE in cell culture test
	- Multinucleated giant cells seen in smear
	- Intranuclear inclusions
#### Treatment
- No antivirals for normal immune system patients
- For immunocomprimized, [[Acyclovir]]
#### Prevention
- Varicella vaccine -- live attenuated
- Zoster -- one live (older), one recombinant (standard today)
	- Contraindication of live vaccines in immune compromised patients
- VZIG (immunoglobulin) post exposure
