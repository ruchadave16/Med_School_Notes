**Main Classes**
- [[Penicillins]]
- [[Cephalosporins]]
- [[Carbapenems]]
- Monobactams -- [[Aztreonam]]
### Common Parts
*Inactivate bacteria [[cell wall]] synthesis by targetting PBPs (penicillin-binding proteins) that are important for [[Peptidoglycan|peptidoglycan]] crosslinking*

- b-Lactam ring
- End up mimicing D-ala D-ala, which is the substrate of PBPs, and **irreversibly bind** to them
	- Cause bacterial lysis

>[!Note] Bacteria in stationary phase are less susceptible to b-lactams

### Resistance Mechanism
1) [[b-Lactamase]] production
	- This enzyme hydrolyzes the b-lactam ring causing antibiotic to be inactivated
	- [[Gram positive]] secrete tons of b-lactamase extracellulary
	- [[Gram negative]] have b-lactamase in periplasmic space and stop drugs before they get to PBPs
		- [[b-lactamase inhibitors]] can restore function of these

2) PBPs alteration
	- ↓ b-lactam affinity causes lower efficacy of drug

3) Impaired penetration through membranes
	- [[Gram negative]] have porin regulation, so entry is regulated 
	- Loss of D2 porin -> resistance to imipenem in [[Pseudomonas aeruginosa]]

4) Efflux pumps
	- [[Gram negative]] have efflux pumps that they use to push b-lactam out of periplasmic space
