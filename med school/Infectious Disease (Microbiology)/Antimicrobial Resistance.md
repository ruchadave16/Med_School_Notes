1) Intrinsic Resistance
	- [[Gram Negative|Gram negative]] intrinsically resistant to [[vancomycin]] because it can't pass outer membrane
	- Anaerobes resistant to [[aminoglycosides]] because they need O2
	- [[Pseudomonas aeruginosa]] has no porins
	- [[Gram Positive|Gram positive]] resistant to [[aztreonam]]
2) Acquired Resistance
	- ↓ entry into pathogen of drug
		- Mutation in porins
		- [[b-lactams]] resistance in [[pseudomonas aeruginosa]] because of loss of OprD porins
	- ↑ efflux of antibiotics
		- [[Tetracyclins]] resistance
	- Enzymatic inactivation of antibiotics
		- [[B-Lactamase|b-lactamases]] enzymes hydrolyze [[penicillins]] and [[cephalosporins]]
		- [[Acetyltransferases]] modify [[chloramphenicol]] to stop it from binding to ribosomes
	- Modification of drug target
		- [[Macrolides]] resistance by ribosomal methylation
	- Alternative metabolic pathways
		- Sulfonamide resistance when bacteria increase folic acid uptake

**Antibiotic pressure drives survival of resistant bacteria**
