### Categories of Therapy 

![[Drawing 2026-02-14 11.08.55.excalidraw]]

- Primary Prophylaxis
	- Prevent infection 
	- Use when pt not infected but at high risk
	- Ex: surgical prophylaxis, HIV prophylaxis
- Preemptive Therapy
	- Prevent disease progression in asymptomatic pts (lab evidence needed)
	- CMV infection in transplant pts
- Empirical Therapy
	- Active infection when causative organism is unknown
	- Principles
		- Selection based on common pathogens
		- Therapy = broad-spectrum
- Definitive Therapy
	- Allows for de-escalation after organism confirmed
- Post-treatment Suppression

**Monotherapy vs Combination Therapy**
- Combination used when monotherapy not sufficient
- Broad spectrum empiric coverage
	- Higher likelihood of covering unknown pathogens in critically ill pts
- Polymicrobial Toxicity
- Prevention of resistance dependence
- ↓ Toxicity
- Synergistic effects
### Selective Toxicity
- Kill pathogen without causing harm to host 

1) Morphological differences -- lack of cell wall
2) Functional differences -- folate synthesis de novo inhibited by [[sulfonamides]]
3) Difference in drug uptake -- activation within microbe to become toxic
4) Variation in target -- ribosome differences, different enzymes

### Measuring Activity
- MIC ([[Minimal Inhibitory Concentration]])
	- Lowest concentration of antibiotic that inhibits visible growth
	- Helps identify if bacteria is **susceptible (S)**, **intermediate (I)**, or **resistant (R)**

- MBC (Minimal Bactericidal Concentration)
	- Lowest concentration that kills 99.9% bacteria
