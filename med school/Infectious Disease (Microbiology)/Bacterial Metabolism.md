*Minimum Requirements = C, N, energy, water, ions*

- Autotroph / lithotroph 
- Heterotroph / organotroph
- Phototroph
- Chemotroph

### Bacterial Catabolism
- Hydrolysis of macromolecules in ECM -> brought through cell membrane into cytoplasm of bacteria -> converted to [[pyruvic acid]]and then to other products

1) Aerobic [[cellular respiration]]
	- [[Glycolysis]]
	- [[Citric Acid Cycle]]
	- [[Oxidative Phosphorylation|ETC]] in cell membrane

2) Anaerobic cellular respiration
	- [[Glycolysis]]
	- Inorganic sources (nitrate -> NH4+, sulfate -> H2S, CO2 -> CH4)
3) Fermentation
	- [[Glycolysis]]
	- Pyruvate -> CO2, alcohols, acids, gases
	- **Low yield: 2-3 ATP / glucose**
	- Common pathways: 
		- [[propionic acid fermentation]] -- [[propionibacterium]]
		- [[butyric acid fermentation]] -- [[Clostridium Botulinum]]

4) [[Pentose Phosphate Pathway]]

### Clinical
- Lactose -- [[MacConkey's Agar]]
- Carb fermentation -- [[Neisseria Gonorrhoea]]
	- [[N. meningitidis]] = glucose + maltose fermentation
	- [[N. gonorrhoeae]] = glucose only
- Indole test: 
	- [[E. Coli]] = indole positive
	- [[Klebsiella Granulomatis]] = indole negative
- TSI agar: H2S production = black precipitate
	- [[Salmonella]] and [[proteus]] = positive
	- [[Shigella]] = negative
- Hippurate hydrolysis
	- Identifies [[Group B streptococcus]] and [[Listeria Monocytogenes]]
- Esculin hydrolysis
	- Identifies [[Group D streptococcus]]


>[!Clinical]
>[[Isoniazid]] is prodrug that must be activated by KatG -> inhibits mycolic acid synthesis
