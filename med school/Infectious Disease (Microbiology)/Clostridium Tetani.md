*[[Gram positive]] rod *
- Anaerobic
- Terminal spores
- "tennis racket" appearance 
- Fine colonies on blood agar, ground glass appearance
#### Mechanism
- Works in [[spinal cord]] on Renshaw cells
	- B portion attaches to motor terminal neurons and is taken in through endocytosis
	- **retrograde** travel -> brainstem
	- Inhibition of inhibitory proteins (GABA and glycine) -> unopposed excitatory response
#### Symptoms
- Muscle spasm and rigidity
- Spastic paralysis
	- Trismus (lockjaw) **Presents First**
	- Risus sardonicus

#### Diagnosis
#### Treatment
- [[Metronidazole]]
- Tetanus immunoglobulin
- Wound care
- Antibiotics
#### Prevention
- Vaccination
