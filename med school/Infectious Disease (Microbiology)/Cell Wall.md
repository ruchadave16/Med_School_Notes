- Made of [[peptidoglycan]], same composition in gram + or - bacteria
- Provides shape to bacteria and mechanical strength, protects from osmotic pressure change
- Target for many antibiotics

Gram Positive [[bacteria]] have thicker peptidoglycan layer while gram negative have [[LPS]] layer

![[Screenshot 2026-02-08 at 2.43.16 PM.png]]
#### Synthesis
1) Cytoplasmic Stage (NAG/NAM synthesis)
	- Inhibition = [[cycloserine]] (Stops conversion of L-Ala to D-Ala) and [[fosfomycin]] (inhibits enol pyruvate transferase)

![[Drawing 2026-02-08 15.20.19.excalidraw]]

2) Membranous Stage (transfer of precursors to membrane)
	- [[Bactoprenol]] gets conjugated with NAM-pp
	- NAG from UDP-NAG also attaches 2nd to bactoprenol
	- Peptidoglycan synthase enzyme flips compound and attaches it to the outside complex
		- [[Vancomycin]] inhibits flipping by clinging onto last 2 Ala
	- Bactoprenol recycled back into membrane by removal of phosphate group
		- Inhibition of this recycling = [[bacitracin]]

3) Extra Cytoplasmic Stage (cross-linking of polymers)
	- Transpeptidase enzyme catalyzes this (penicillin binding protein PBP)
	- [[Beta lactams]] all inhibit this step

>[!Exceptions]
>[[Neisseria]] has lipo-oligosaccharide instead of LPS
>[[Mycobacterium tuberculosis]] is gram positive but has thin peptidoglycan with thick **mycolic acid**
