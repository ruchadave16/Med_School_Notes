**Types**
- None: Arichous
- Single polar: Monotrichous
- Tuft of flagella at one end: Lophotrichous
- 1/2 on both ends: Amphitrichous
- Flagella on all ends: Peritrichous
#### Structure
- Basal body – anchored to cell membrane / wall
	- Hook (curved)
	- Filament (long external component)
- Made from [[flagellin]]
#### Functions
- Motility in liquid environments
	- Clockwise (tumbling)
		- [[Listeria Monocytogenes]] shows tumbling at 25 C
		- Helps change of direction
	- Counterclockwise (smooth movement)
		- Drives + and - chemotaxis
	- Corkscrew (axial filament)
		- Spirochetes like [[Treponema pallidum]]
		- Viscous tissue movement
- Chemotaxis (positive or negative)
- Carry **H antigen** for identification of bacteria

>[!Clinical] Identification
>[[E. coli]] O157:H7
>- H7 tells flagellar antigen
>- Causes hemorrhagic colitis
>- Undercooked beef
>
>[[Salmonella]] uses H antigen patterns
>
>[[Vibrio cholera]] has darting motility in rice-water stool

>[!Clinical] Pathogenesis
>[[Listeria meningitis]]
>- Produces flagella at 25 C → tumbling
>- Non-motile at 37 C
>- This helps approach host cells
>  
> Cystitis
> - [[UPEC]] (Uropathogenic E. coli)
> - Flagella helps ascent from urethra to bladder
