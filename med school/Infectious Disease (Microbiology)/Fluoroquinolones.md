*Synthetic, fluorinated derivatives of nalidixic acid that act against [[gram positive]] and [[gram negative]]*
### Mechanism
- Inhibit[[DNA synthesis]]by targeting **[[topoisomerase II]] (DNA gyrase in [[gram negative]]) and [[topoisomerase IV]] in [[gram positive]]**
- Mammals have different topoisomerase enzymes

| Fluoroquinolone |                           Target | Gram Negative | Gram Positive |
| --------------- | -------------------------------: | ------------- | ------------- |
| Ciprofloxacin   | [[Topoisomerase II\|DNA gyrase]] | Excellent     | Moderate      |
| Levofloxacin    |                             Both | Good          | Good          |
| Moxifloxacin    |             [[Topoisomerase IV]] | Moderate      | Improved      |
| Delafloxacin    |                             Both | Strong        | Strong        |
**Notes**
- Ciprofloxacin and Levofloxacin 
	- Strong gram negative including [[Pseudomonas aeruginosa]]
	- Limited gram (+)
- Moxifloxacin
	- Enhanced gram (+) including [[Streptococcus Pneumonia|Strep Pneumo]] but weaker gram (-)
- Delafloxacin
	- Broad spectrum, balanced between both including [[MRSA]]
### Resistance
Chromosomal mutations and plasmid mediation 
- Mutations
	- To fluoroquinolone binding site on target enzyme so low drug binding
	- Upregulation of efflux pumps
- Plasmid Mediated
	- Qnr proteins protect DNA gyrase
	- Plasmid-encoded modifying enzymes change and inactivate fluoroquinolones
- Cross-Resistance
	- Generally complete resistance to others
	- Exception: Delafloxacin has activity against some resistant gram (+)
### Adverse Effects
- GI disturbance (3-17%) -- nausea, vomiting
- Neuro (1-11%)  -- headache, dizziness, insomnia, anxiety
	- Rare = peripheral neuropathy
- QT prolongation
	- Highest risk with moxifloxacin and levofloxacin
- Cartilage and Joint Toxicity
	- Not recommended for <18 pts
- Tendinitis and Tendon Rupture
	- Rare, most common to Achilles tendon
	- Risk: 
		- >60
		- Corticosteroid use
		- Renal insufficiency
### Drug Interactions
- Chelation with Divalent and Trivalent Cations
	- Separation usage of Ca, Mg, Fn, Al supplements
- QT Prolongation Risk
	- Caution with Class III and Class IA antiarrhythmics
