### Mechanism
- Entry
	- Passive diffusion
	- Active transport
- Target
	- Reversible binding to 30S [[Bacterial Ribosome|bacterial ribosome]]
	- Block incoming aminoacetyl-tRNA from binding to A site
	
![[Screenshot 2026-02-16 at 5.46.08 PM.png]]
### Effects
- Bacteriostatic, broad spectrum (gram + and -)
- More potent against [[gram positive]]
	- Negative resistance due to efflux pumps
- Active against [[intracellular bacteria]]
	- [[Rickettsia]], [[Chlamydia]], [[Mycoplasmae]]
### Resistance
- Efflux Pumps
	- Plasmid encoded have resistance to older tetracyclines, but new are NOT substrates for these
	- Chromosomal encoded have resistance against ALL
- Ribosomal protection proteins
	- Bind to ribosome and displace tetracyclin
	- Resistance to older but NOT newer
- Enzymatic inactivation
### Pharmacokinetics
**Absorption**
- Doxycycline and minocycline = 100% GI absorption
- Older tetracyclines have varied oral absorption
	- Food impairs
- Newer:
	- Tigecycline and eravacycline only parenterally
	- Omadacycline = 35% oral bioavailability
- Absorption impaired by by divalent and trivalent cations
	- Separate intake from dairy

**Distribution**
- All body fluids and tissues except CSF
- Crosses placenta, excreted through breast milk
	- CONTRAINDICATION = pregnancy
- Accumulation in teeth and bone
- Elimination through renal and biliary
	- Doxycycline and tigecycline through non-renal -- NO dose adjustment
### Adverse Effects
- GI: 
	- Nausea, vomiting
	- Direct irritation to gastric mucosa
	- Disruption of gut flora -- diarrhea
- Bone and Teeth
	- Binds to Ca2+ causing deposition in teeth -- discoloration
	- CONTRAINDICATION: pregnancy, children < 8
- Hepatotoxicity
- Renal toxicity
	- Older tetracyclines
- Photosensitivity
	- Pt. wear sunscreen and avoid light
- Vestibular Effects
	- Dizziness, tinnitus at high dosage
- Hypersensitivity reaction rare but possible
