*Inhibit bacterial [[folic acid synthesis]]* (Only bacteria have to do this since humans get from food)
### Mechanism
- Act as analogs of PABA (para-aminobenzoic acid) and competitively inhibit dihydropteroic acid (precursor for folic acid)
	- Absent in humans so its selective to bacteria
	
**Classes**
- Oral absorbable: Sulfisoxazole, Sulfamethoxazole
	- GI absorption
	- Use for systemic infection and UTIs
	- Sulfamethoxazole combined with trimethoprim (TMP-SMX)
- Oral non-absorbable: Sulfasalazine
	- Local action in intestine
	- IBD usage (ulcerative colitis and Crohns disease)
	- Sometimes used for rheumatoid artheritis
- Topical: 
	- Sulfacetamide
		- Eye infections
	- Silver sulfadiazine
		- Infection prevention in **burn patients**
		- Additional properties against gram (+) and (-)
### Resistance
- Intrinsic: some bacteria depend on external folate
- Acquired: mutations or plasmid-mediated gene transfer
	- Overproduction of PABA
	- Production of dihydropteroate synthase with **lower affinity** to sulfonamides
	- ↓ drug permeability
	- Alternative metabolism that bypasses that step
### Uses
- [[Haemophilus Ducreyi]]
- [[Nocardia]] spp.
- [[Klebsiella granulomatis]]
- Some activity against fungi / protozoa

### Adverse Effects
- Hypersensitivity
	- Mild: rash, erythema, photosensitivity
	- Severe: exfoliative dermatitis, Steven-Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis
- Cross-Allergenity
	- Pt. allergic to non-antibiotic sulfonamides can get these 
- Hematologic Disturbance
	- **Blood dyscrasias**
		- Hemolytic anemia, esp patients with [[G6P dehydrogenase deficiency]]
		- Aplastic anemia
		- Granulocytopenia, thrombocytopenia, leukemoid rxn
- Renal toxicity
- Headaches
- GI complaints (nausea, vomiting)
