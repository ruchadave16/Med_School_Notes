#### Action
- Enter through passive diffusion (porins) in [[gram negative]]
	- Active through inner membrane based on O2 presence
	- Inhibited under anaerobic and low pH -> NO effect on anaerobes
	- Enhanced by cell wall agents
- Inhibition of protein synthesis
	- 30S [[bacterial ribosome]] binding
	- Stops formation of initiation complex so misreading
#### Uses
- Streptomycin: first developed, not used today
- Gentamicin: SEVERE infections, topically for infected burns and wounds
- Tobramycin: Useful against [[Pseudomonas aeruginosa]]
- Amikacin: Resistant to aminoglycosidase-inactivating enzymes
#### Administration
- Highly polar so poor GI absorption -- IV/IM
	- Low tissue penetration so low intracellular
- No crossing BBB
- Renal elimination
#### Dosing
**Concentration based killing**
- High concentration means faster and more complete killing
- Once daily administration
#### Adverse Effects
- Ototoxic
	- Vestibular dysfunction: vertigo, ataxia
	- Irreversible hearing loss: Tinnitus, high frequency loss
- Nephrotoxic
	- Accumulation in proximal tubular cells
#### Contraindication
