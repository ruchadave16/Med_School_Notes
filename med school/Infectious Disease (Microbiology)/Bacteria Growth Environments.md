### Oxygen
*In oxygen environment, SOD and catalase break down ROS (stop [[Oxidative Stress|oxidative stress]]*
- [[Obligate anaerobes]]
- Obligate aerobes
	- Enzymes: Superoxide dismutase (SOD) and catalase
	- Ex: [[Pseudomonas aeruginosa]], [[Mycobacterium tuberculosis]], [[Nocardia]]
- Facultative anaerobes
	- Most pathogenic bacteria
	- Pasteur effect: O2 inhibits fermentation
- Microaerophilic Bacteria
	- Low levels of oxygen (2-10%)
	- Ex: [[Campylobacter Jejuni]]
- Capnophilic bacteria
	- Need ↑ CO2 (~10%)
	- Ex: [[Streptococcus Pneumonia|Strep Pneumo]]
- [[Intracellular bacteria]] 
### pH 
*Typically 6.5-7.5*
- [[H. pylori]] need alkaline pH
	- Urease to turn urea -> ammonia
- [[Vibrio cholera]] needs alkaline
### Temperature
*Normally between 35-37 C*
- [[Campylobacter Jejuni]]= 42 C -- [[hemorrhagic colitis]]
- [[Yersinia enterocolitica]] = 25C -- infectious diarrhea
- [[Mycobacterium haemophilum]] = colder at 30-32C -- chronic wound, need iron as cofactor
- [[Pseudomonas aeruginosa]] -- hot tub associated folliculitis
- [[Mycobacterium marinum]] = colder temp, fish tank related wound infection
### Incubation Duration
*Commonly 24-48 hrs*
- [[Mycobacterium tuberculosis]] is slow, 6-8 weeks
- Atypical mycobacteria grow within 7 days
### Nutrition
- Fe++ 
	- Some bacteria have sideropore (iron chelating molecules)
	- [[Enterobactin]] made by [[E. coli]]
	- Mycobactin by [[M. tuberculosis]]
- X Factor / V Factor -- [[Hemophilus Influenza|H. influenza]]
- NaCl > 10% (halophilic bacteria)
	- [[Vibrio cholera]]
- NaCl 6.5%
	- [[Enterococcus]]
