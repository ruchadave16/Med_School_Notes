*Process through which leukocytes exit vasculature → inflammation sites*

Mainly happens in post-capillary venules
- Due to release [[cytokines]]
- Upregulation of E-selectin due to [[IL-1]] and [[TNF]]
- P-selectin is moved to surface by [[histamine]] and [[thrombin]]
- ↑ VCAM ([[CD106]]), ICAM ([[CD54]]), PECAM-1 ([[CD31]])

- Margination: move to side since low blood flow
- Rolling
	- WBCs have carbohydrate (**Sialyl-Lewis X**) ← LAD 2
	- Bind to E-selectin or P-selectin endothelial cells
- Crawling
	- Express integrin ([[CD18]] LAD1) – LFA-1, MAC-1, of VLA4, which binds to **ICAM**-1 or VCAM-1 on endothelial cells
	- Cell becomes flat
- Transmigration
	- Bind **PECAM-1** (CD31) found in between endothelial cells and on WBC
- Migration to inflammation
	- Chemokines draw to site like C5a and [[IL-8]]
