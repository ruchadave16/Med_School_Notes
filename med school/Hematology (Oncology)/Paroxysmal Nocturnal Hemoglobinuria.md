### Pathophysiology
- Deficiency in inhibitors of [[Complement System|complement system]] (DAF or CD59)
- [[Erythrocytes|RBC]] lysis → [[anemia]], [[hemoglobinuria]] → renal failure
- Free plasma hemoglobin → [[thrombosis]]
	- Hemoglobin binds to [[Nitric Oxide]] → NO depletion → ↑ smooth muscle tone
	- Erectile dysfunction, dysphagia

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- Priapism
	- Hemoglobinuria → renal failure
	- [[Thrombosis]]
	- Dysphagia, 
	- Abdominal pain
- Signs
	- Sudden hemolysis at night (awake with abdominal pain and urine discolored from hemolysis)
	- Fatigue and dyspnea ← anemia
	- Abdominal pain ← smooth muscle tension
	- Dysphagia ← smooth muscle tension
	- Thrombosis in unusual locations (portal v., mesenteric v, cerebral v.)
- Variants
