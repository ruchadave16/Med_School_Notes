*Produces insoluble [[fibrin]] clot*

- Assembled on negatively charged phospholipids – surface of activated [[Thrombocytes|platelets]]
- Depends on cofactors: Ca2+ and [[Vitamin K]]

**[[Vitamin K]]** important for Factors **II, VII, IX, X** carboxylation in [[liver]] and for [[Protein C]] and [[Protein S]]

![[Screenshot 2026-01-25 at 9.19.27 PM.png]]
#### In Vivo
- Main initiation = **[[tissue factor]]**
- Tissue factor activates [[Factor VII]] → involves IX and XI → end with [[Factor X]]
- Activated Factor X cleaves prothrombin → [[thrombin]]
- Thrombin cleaves fibrinogen → [[fibrin]]
#### In Vitro
- Provide negative charged surface (intrinsic pathway)
	- Start with activation of Factor XII
	- **Measurement of time = PTT time**
- Provide tissue factor
	- Start with activation of Factor VII
	- **Measurement of time = PT time**

![[Screenshot 2026-01-24 at 2.23.53 PM.png]]

>[!Warning]
>Deficiency in V, VII, VIII, IX, X = moderate to severe bleeding disorder
>Prothrombin (II) = incompatible with life
>XI = mild bleeding
>XII = no increased bleeding tendency

#### Limitations
- Negatively charged phospholipids – only present on active platelets
- [[Thrombin]] – interacts with normal endothelial cells as anticoagulant
- Antithrombin II – inhibitor of coagulation factors
- [[Plasmin]]
