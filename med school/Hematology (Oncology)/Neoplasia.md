*New growth outside normal cell division growth cycle*
#### **Benign** – Remains localized
- Mesenchymal tumor: “cell type” - oma
	- Fibroma = benign tumor of fibroblasts
	- Chondroma = benign tumor of cartilage
- Epithelioid tumor from glandular tissue = “adenoma”
- Epithelioid tumor with finger-like projections = “papilloma”
- Epithelioid tumor with cystic compoment = “cystadenoma”
#### **Malignant** – Metastasis or local invasion
- Solid mesenchymal tumor: sarcoma
	- Fibrosarcoma = malignant tumor of fibroblasts
	- Chondrosarcoma = malignant tumor of cartilage
	- Osteosarcoma = malignant tumor of bone
- Tumor of blood-forming cells: Leukemia / lymphoma
	- Leukemia = WBC tumor
	- Lymphoma = solid tumor of lymphoid tissue
- Epithelioid tumor: Carcinoma
	- Squamous cell carcinoma = malignant tumor like squamous epithelial cells
	- Adenocarcinoma = malignant tumor like glandular tissue
- Mixed tumor: monoclonal origin but can become both epithelial and mesenchymal 
- Teratoma = mixed tumor that can become any cell type of body

Hamartoma – Disorganized masses of cells of the same tissue type
Choristoma – Misplaced cells to wrong location (normal appearing)
#### Characteristics
**Differentiation**
- Well differentiated cells more likely to be benign, poorly differentiated more likely to be malignant (anaplasia)

**Local Invasion + Metastasis**
- Benign = localized
	- Encapsulated = most benign 
- Malignant tumors can invade and destroy adjacent structures
	- Direct seeding into cavities
	- Through lymph – carcinomas
	- Through blood vessels – sarcomas but also carcinomas
	- [[Metalloproteases]] aid with metastasis by **breaking protein like collagen**

#### Clinical Manifestation
- Impingement on nearby structures
- Death of involved tissue
- Nidus of infection

**Cancer cachexia** = hypercatabolic state when muscle mass is lost 
- Extreme weight loss
- Fatigue
- Muscle atrophy
- Anemia
- Anorexia
- Edema
