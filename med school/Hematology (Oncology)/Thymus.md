*Primary organ of [[T Lymphocytes|T cell]] differentiation*

T cells express [[T Cell Receptors|T cell receptors]] here
- Must bind to MHC I and MHC II and NOT to self
#### Role
- Positive Selection: ensures T-cells recognize MHC. Only those with MCH-I or MHC-II get survival signal
	- [[Thymus]] cortex
	- Weak binding = apoptosis
- Negative Selection: ensure they don’t bind to self-antigens. If too strong, [[Apoptosis|apoptosis]] signal
	- [[Thymus]] medulla
	- Excessive binding = apoptosis

#### Composition
- Stroma: [[Reticular cells]] of epithelial origin **
- Parenchyma: [[mesenchyme]] connective tissue origin
	- Capsule + septae
	- Cortex next to capsule where [[T Lymphocytes|T cell]] + selection
	- Medulla with [[Hassall’s Corpuscles]] deep to cortex where [[T Lymphocytes|T cell]] - selection
- Blood-thymic barrier in cortex
- NO afferent lymphatics
- NO filtration of blood or lymph
