- Circulate ~5 days, die unless activated
- Drawn to sites of inflammation
#### Function
**Acute immune response** 
Increase with **bacterial** infection
[[Phagocytosis]] of extracellular agents through [[lysosome]] 
- Granules = [[Lysosome|lysosomes]]
- Kill through [[ROS]] and use myeloperoxidase intracellularly

Motile cells that leave to move to inflammation site (drawn by chemotaxins like [[IL-8]] and C5a)

>[!Warning] Neutrophils do NOT present antigens
#### Structure
**Multilobed, heterochromatic nucleus** – segmented, dark nucleus
**Granules**
1) Azurophilic
	- In all granulocytes
2) Specific granules
	- Ellipsoid, not seen in [[Microscopy|light microscopy]]
	- Contain enzymes (alkaline phosphatase, collagenase, lysozyme, lactoferrin)
	- Fuse with phagosomes → kill pathogens
	- Can be released into ECM
3) Tertiary granules
	- Acid phosphatase, myeloperoxidase
	- Fuse with phagosomes only

#### Development
**Band cells** = immature neutrophils
- Seen in [[bacterial infection]] **Left Shift**
- Infection → higher production of neutrophils → higher release of band cells
- NO segmented nuclei
