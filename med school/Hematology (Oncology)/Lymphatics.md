*System of channels collecting lymph from tissue interstitial spaces and bringing to [[Cardiovascular Anatomy|Cardiovascular system]]*

**No pumps** → Compression of vessels 
##### Function
Protect against infection by housing [[lymphocytes]] and filtering [[lymph]]
Collect tissue fluids, solutes, hormones, plasma proteins to return to bloodstream

>[!Clinical] Failure to return to bloodstream causes [[edema]] because of fluid trapped in tissues

Absorbs [[Adipose|fat]] and large proteins from small intestines to blood stream

>[!Clinical] Lymph flow explains how cancer and disease spread 

##### Components
- [[Lymph]] is similar to plasma with fewer proteins
- [[Lymphocytes]]: circulating components of [[Immune System]]
- [[Lymphatic capillaries]]: found in most tissues that merge into collecting vessels
	- [[Lacteals]] are capillaries in GI tract
- [[Lymphatic Vessels|Lymph vessels]]: networks of thin-walled vessels with 1-way flow (valves) 
- Lymphoid organs: 
	- Primary structures (formation and maturation of [[lymphocytes]])
		- [[Bone marrow]], [[thymus]]
	- Secondary structures (house lymphocytes, location of immune response)
		- Encapsulated: [[spleen]], [[lymph nodes]], [[tonsils]]
		- Diffuse: [[MALT]], [[Peyer's Patches|Peyer's patches]]
	- Tertiary structures
- [[Lymphatic nodules]]
	- [[Chronic Inflammation|Chronic inflammation]] has lymphocytes, plasma cells, APCs all aggregate to make a follicle


>[!Info] Flow: interstitial space → vessles → lymph nodes → trunks → ducts → [[venous angle]] → vein
