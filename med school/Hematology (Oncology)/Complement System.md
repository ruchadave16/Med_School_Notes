*Proteins produced by [[liver]] that circulate in blood stream and bind to pathogens like bacteria*

- Most common = C3, can be converted to [[C3b]] (normally destroyed if not stabilized)
	- Lets off [[C3a]] when it is converted to C3b
	- Binds to Bacterial lipopolysaccharides, fungal cell walls, viral envelopes
- [[C3b]] once stabilized forms [[MAC]] (membrane attack complex) → pores in bacteria

>[!Note] All complement activation include C3 → C3b conversion

![[Drawing 2026-01-19 15.35.15.excalidraw]]
#### Alternative Pathway
- C3 spontaneously converted to C3b (low level)
	- [[C3b]] destroyed unless it binds to amino and hydroxyl group on surface of pathogens
- [[Properdin]] is a protein that binds and stabilizes C3bBb so C3b deposition can happen
- Once the initial [[C3b]] is made, it can bind to complement protein B
	- Complement protein D clips B bound to C3b to form C3bBb (C3 convertase)
	- Can cleave more C3 → C3b
- Rapid accumulation of C3b on surfaces

![[Drawing 2026-01-19 15.05.50.excalidraw]]
- [[Factor H]] ensures alternative pathway doesn’t happen on **host cells**

#### Classical Pathway
- Antibody-antigen complexes ([[Adaptive Immunity|adaptive immunity]])
- Binds to [[C1 Protein]], which cleaves C2 → C2b and C4 → C4b
	- C2b and C4b combine to form C2b4b (C3 convertase)
	- Converts C3 → C3b
	
*“GM makes classic cars”*
- [[IgM]] or IgG [[bind]] to C1
#### [[Lectin Pathway]]
- Mannose binding lectin
#### Inhibition
Both are important to protect [[Erythrocytes|RBCs]]
- Decay accelarating factor (DAF, [[CD55]])
	- Disrupts [[C3b]] attachment
- MAC inhibitory protein ([[CD59]])
	- Disrupts [[MAC]]
