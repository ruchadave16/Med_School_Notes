### Etiology
- Primary causes
- Risk factors

### Pathophysiology
- Deficiency of [[C1 Protein|C1]] inhibitor protein
- No breakdown of [[bradykinin]] so high levels, high vasodilation

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- Episodes of swelling / edema especially in skin, GI, upper airway and tongue, without urticaria
	- Begin in childhood
- Signs
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
	- Low C4 levels because of lack of C1 inhibitor so high consumption of C4
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
	- C1 inhibitor concentrate
- Surgical
- Lifestyle

>[!Warning] CONTRAINDICATION
>- [[ACE Inhibitors]] (these stop bradykinin break down which is already elevated in these patients)
>

>[!Note]
>Epinephrine doesn’t work very well. Often confused for allergic reaction but if no change, that indicates this
### Complications
- Airway swelling can be fatal
