*Genetic disorder caused by [[DNA]] mutations*
- Alterations are heritable
- Mutations impart cancer hallmarks
#### Epidemiology
**Environmental Factors**
- Dominant risk factors
- Diet – obesity = moderately ↑ risk for many cancers
- [[Smoking]] = cancers of mouth, larynx, pharynx, esophagus, pancreas, bladder, and lung
- [[Alcohol]] = cancers of oropharynx, larynx, esophagus, liver
- Reproductive history = estrogen exposure increases risk of breast cancer and endometrium
- Infectious agents
	- EBV 
	- HBV, HCV – hepatocellular carcinoma
	- HHV-8 – kaposi sarcoma
	- HPV (Type 16 and 18) – cervical carcinoma, head and neck cancer,
	- H. pylori – gastric adenocarcinoma and MALT lymphoma
	- HTLV-1 – adult t-cell leukemia/lymphoma
	- Liver fluke (Clonorchis sinensis) – Cholangiocarcinoma
	- Schistosoma haematobium – Squamous cell bladder cancer
- Carcinogens
	- [[Asbestos]] – lung, esophageal, gastric, colon, mesothelioma
	- Benzene – [[acute myeloid leukemia]]
	- Beryllium – lung carcinoma
	- PVC – [[hepatic angiosarcoma]]
	- Tobacco smoke
	- Ionizing radiation
	- UV rays – squamous cell carcinomas and melanomas of skin

**Age**
As age increases, so does chance of cancer

**Acquired Predispositions**
- [[Chronic Inflammation|Chronic inflammation]]
	- Pancreatitis – pancreatic carcinoma
	- Hashimoto thyroiditis – MALT lymphoma
	- Reflux esophagitis – esophageal cancer
	- IBD – colorectal cancer
- Immunodeficiency – viral cancers
- Precursor lesions 

#### Cancer Genes
- [[Oncogenes]] – promote increased cell growth, dominant allele because even one allele can cause cancer
- [[Tumor Suppressor Genes]] – genes normally stop cancer but when both normal alleles are changed, end up causing cancer
- Apoptosis Genes – genes that stop apoptosis are over-expressed in cancer cells

#### Hallmarks
- Self-sufficiency in growth signals
- Insensitivity to inhibitory signals
- Change to cell metabolism
- Apoptosis evasion
- Limitless replication
- Angiogenesis 
- Invasion + metastasis
- Evasion of immune surveilence
