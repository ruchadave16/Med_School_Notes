*”Study of tissues of the body and how tissues are arranged to constitute organs”*

Like “microscopic anatomy” - understanding cellular constituents of human body

Structure-Function relationship - structure dictates function and often change in structure causes dysfunction

[[Tissue Preparation]] and [[Tissue Staining|staining]] to study histology
- [[Immunohistochemistry]] to visualize molecules within tissues

##### [[Microscopy]] in Histology
1) Cellularity: How many cells are in the image? 
	- Look for nuclei
		- Most cells only have 1
		- Non-cancer cells have limited overlap and respect borders
		- Easy to see nuclei as blue, dark-stained round blobs
	- Compare images to show one is hypercellular or hypocellular

2) Size: Can help identify cell type
	- Magnification not always obvious so use something in image that is constant size
	- [[RGC]] are a good choice for comparison
	- Image below, yellow arrow pointing to neutrophil in background of RBC. In both images, about double size of RBC

![[Screenshot 2025-07-12 at 3.04.34 PM.png]]

3) Architecture: Look at cell organization
	- Use nuclei to see this
	- Could be organizing into circles, fascicles

4) Shape
	- Is nuclei round or elongated?
	- Is cytoplasm present in high quantity or less?
