Traditionally [[histology]] requires preparation of tissue slides to visualize with light
- Must be thin for light to pass through

GOAL: preserve tissue structure and distinguish between cells/structures

#### Steps of Tissue Preparation for [[Light Microscopy]]

![[Screenshot 2025-07-10 at 7.36.35 PM.png]]

1) Macroscopic tissue selection: need knowledge of gross anatomy
		*“You’ll only see under the microscope what you select grossly”*
		
2) Fixation
	- Organs placed after removal into fixatives: solutions of stabilizing or cross-linking compounds (create covalent bonds between proteins)
	- Preserves tissue in “living state”, stop degradation
	- Commonly use formalin: 37% formaldehyde
		- Alternative for electron microscopy: glutaraldehyde
	- These react with amine groups to prevent degradation by proteases
	- This step **deactivates** most proteins but also protects from degradation
	
3) Dehydration: Water extracted gradually by bathing in increasing concentrations of ethanol

4) Clearing: 
	- Alcohol needs to be removed for embedding medium to infiltrate tissue
	- Organic compounds replace alcohol but are miscible with wax (**Xylene** common)
	- Removes [[Adipose|fat]] - *fat shows as blank space in microscope*
	
5) Infiltration
	- Tissue put in liquid wax (embedding media)
	- Paraffin very common
	
6) Embedding
	- Block is created when infiltrated media placed in embedding media (liquid), which solidifies around tissue in mold that fits microtome
	
7) Sectioning
	- [[Microtome]] used to cut thin slices (3-10 $\mu$M for light, <1 $\mu$M for electron)
