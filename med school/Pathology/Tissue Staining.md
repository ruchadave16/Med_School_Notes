Thinly sliced tissue is translucent - no contrast so not useful

**Selecting Stain**: Focus on differential selectivity - something that highlights X but not Y

##### Hematoxylin and Eosin (H&E)

![[Screenshot 2025-07-10 at 9.49.39 PM.png]]

Hematoxylin
- **Basic** so stains negatively charged particles (DNA, RNA, glycosaminoglycans)

 >[!note] Anything that is stained by a basic dye is called **basophilic**

- Leaves a blue/purple color
- Mainly stains nucleus, RNA-rich parts of cytoplasm, cartilage matrix

Eosin
- **Acidic** dye that stains positively charged particles (many proteins, mitochondria, granules, collagen)

>[!note] Anything that is stained by acidic dye is **acidophilic** or **eosinophilic**

 - Red/pink color
 - Mainly stains cytoplasm and collagen

##### Periodic-acid Schiff (PAS)
- Stains polysaccharides and other carb-rich tissue purple
	- Mucus (not all, some)
	- Basal laminae
	- Glycogen rich tissue (like liver)
	- Thyroid colloid
	- Pathogenic organisms (ex. Candida)
- Positives stain means **large amount of carbs**
![[Screenshot 2025-07-11 at 3.14.39 PM.png]]

##### Trichrome
- Many types, all contain 3 stains that highlight 3 different aspects of tissue
- Masson’s Trichrome
	- [[Collagen]] - blue/green
	- Muscle cells - red
	- Cytoplasm non-muscle cells - pink/light red
	- Nuclei - black

##### Elastin stain (Verhoef-Van Gieson / VVG)
-  [[Elastic Fibers]] - dark blue/black
- Often used to visualize structure of large arteries, like aorta
