### Causes
- Inadequate removal
- Defect in folding, packaging, or secretion
- Failure to degrade
- Deposition or accumulation of exogenous substance
#### Types
- [[Steatosis]] (Fatty change)
- Cholesterol and Cholesterol Esters
	- Defective catabolism or excessive intake
	- Seen in macrophage or smooth muscle cells in vessels with [[atherosclerosis]]
- Proteins: round, eosinophilic droplets in cytoplasm
	- [[Alpha-1-Antitrypsin Deficiency]]: inclusions in [[hepatocytes]]
	- [[Alzheimers Disease|Alzheimers]]: tau inclusions in [[neurons]]

- Glycogen: in macrophages for [[glycogen storage diseases]]
- Pigments
	- Exogenous
		- Carbon (black, most common)
		- Tattoo ink
	- Endogenous:
		- [[Lipofuscin]] (wear and tear)
		- [[Melanin]]
		- [[Hemosiderin]] **hemosiderin macrophages in alveoli is sign of [[congestive heart failure]]**
		- [[Bilirubin]]
