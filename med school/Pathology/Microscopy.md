##### Light Microscopy
*“focus on light interacting with tissue”*

- **Bright Field:** Normal light passes through specimen and is visualized directly
	- PROS: fast, low cost, many options for processing tissue
	- CONS: limited magnification + resultion
	
- **Fluorescent:** Fluorescent compounds naturally occurring in tissue or introduced as a stain
	- PROS: 
		- higher contrast since fluorescent against dark background
		- multiplex staining limited only by availability of compounds w fluorescence of different wavelengths
	- CONS: slower, more expensive, has to work in dark
		
- **Confocal**: Method to reduce light scatter to increase contrast and resolution
	- Use small point of high-intensity light from laser or pinhole and scan whole slide
	- PROS: higher resolution, better localization, 3D image acquired
	- CONS: slow and expensive
##### Electron Microscopy

![[Screenshot 2025-07-12 at 2.55.10 PM.png]]

Based on electron interactions → since $\lambda$ of electron beams is shorter than light, resolution *significantly higher*

1) Transmission Electron Microscopy (TEM): electron beam passes through specimen
	- Get subcellular details, view organelles
	- **Identify organelles** by EM appearance ** FOR BOARD EXAMS
	![[Screenshot 2025-07-12 at 2.57.56 PM.png]]
	
2) Scanning Electron Microscopy (SEM): electron beam bounces off specimen
	- See surface level details at high resolution with 3D appearance
