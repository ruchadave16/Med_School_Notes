>[!Info] Flow = Serous acini → Centroacinar cell → Intercalated duct → Intralobular duct → Interlobular duct → Pancreatic duct

![[Screenshot 2025-09-08 at 5.43.59 PM.png]]

- Acinus – secretes enzymes
- Ducts – secrete aqueous components
#### Histology
- Serous gland – acinar cells highly polarized w spherical nuclei
- Each drains to intercalated duct (simple squamous or cuboidal)

**Centroacinar Cells**
- Initial cells of intercalated duct, extend into lumen of acinus
- Small, pale stained
- Secrete bicarbonate-rich fluid to make sure enzymes are **inactive** till duodenum

#### Secretion Formation
**Enzymes**
- MOST are inactive (zymogens) and must be activated in GI tract
	- Exception = amylase + pancreatic lipases
- [[Trypsinogen]] → [[Trypsin]]
	- Not active till it reaches intestine
	- Secreted alongside [[trypsin inhibitor]]
- Chymotrypsinogen → Chymotrypsin

*Regulation* (acinar cells)
- Stimulation
	- [[Cholecystokinin|CCK]] from I cells in small intestine 
	- Parasympathetic (Vagus n) → [[Ach]]
- Inhibition
	- [[Somatostatin]] from D cells in intestine
	- Peptide YY
	- Sympathetic innervation


**Aquatic Component**
- [[Isotonic]] to plasma 
	- $[HCO_3^-]$ is higher (secreted) – dependent on flow
	- $H^+$ is generally lower (absorbed)
	- $Cl^-$ dependent on flow
	- $Na^+$ and $K^+$ same as plasma
-  $[HCO_3^-]$ secretion ↑ so more in fluid if flow ↑ 
- $Cl^-$ ↓ if flow ↑ 

*Regulation* (centroacinar cells + ductal cells)
- Stimulation
	- [[Secretin]] – release of water solution of sodium bicarbonate by ductal epithelium
	- [[Cholecystokinin|CCK]]
	- Parasympathetic – [[Ach]]
- Inhibition
	- [[Somatostatin]] from D cells in intestine
	- Peptide YY
	- Sympathetic innervation
