### Definition
↑ presure in portal venous system because ↑ resistance in portal vein

### Etiology
- Primary causes
	- Liver cirrhosis
- Risk factors

### Pathophysiology
- Alternate pathways develop between portal + systemic venous systems
- Enlargement of veins (blood reverses direction)
	- Esophageal varices – L gastric v. with esophageal v (off azygous)
	- Paraumbilical varices – [[Caput Medusae]] (paraumbilica v. with superficial epigastric v.)
	- Rectal varices – Superior rectal v. with middle/inferior rectal v. 
	- Retroperitoneal varices – Secondary retroperitoneal organ (dilated splenic, renal, inferior mesenteric veins)

- Relevant pathways (e.g. immune, hormonal)

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
- Signs
	- [[Caput Medusae]] on anterior abdominal wall because of paraumbilical varices
	- Bleeding hemorrhoids
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
- Surgical
	- Portosystemic shunt – divert blood flow from portal to systemic venous
	- Transjugular intrahepatic portosystemic shunt ([[TIPS]]) – Interventional radiologist puts catheter with unexpanded shunt in R jugular v. and directs through SVC, RA, IVC, hepatic v. into portal v and expands to relieve pressure
- Lifestyle

### Complications
- Short-term
- Long-term
	- Catastrophic upper-GI bleeding because of esophageal varices
	- Rectal varices can cause bleeding hemorrhoids
	- Retroperitoneal varices can cause bleeding
