*Hard, heavily mineralized structures embedded in alveolar ridges of [[mandible]] and [[maxilla]]*

Function: break down food

#### Components
1) Crown = protrudes into [[oral cavity]]
2) Root = embedded in bone of mandible/maxilla
3) Neck = junction of crown and root surrounded by [[gingiva]]

![[Pasted image 20250901162756.png]]

- Central pulp cavity
	- [[Mesenchymal]], vascularized, high stem cell, lined with [[odontoblasts]], high innervation
- Enamel
	- Hard, thin translucent layer
	- Acellular, mineralized tissue covering crown
	- Made by [[ameloblasts]] – come from [[ectoderm]]
- Dentin
	- Mineral and [[Extracellular Matrix|ECM]], made by [[odontoblasts]] – [[neural crest cells]] origin
	- Form most of tooth substance
	- Odontoblasts ensure dentin can repair itself, sensitive to pain
	- Dentinal tubules permeate dentin to provide passage to pulp cavity
- Cementum 
	- Bone-like, avascular tissue 
	- Secreted by [[cementocytes]]
	- Covers dentin, more soft and permeable
- Periodontal ligament
	- Fibrous [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]] anchoring tooth to alveolar socket
	- High turnover
