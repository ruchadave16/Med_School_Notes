##### Boundaries
Starts at rectosigmoid junction (S3), ends at anorectal junction
##### Contents
- Omental appendices disappear
- Teniae coli form outer layer of longitudinal [[smooth muscle]]
##### External Features

##### Internal Features
1) Superior – Peritoneum anterior + lateral, supplied by superior rectal a. ← inferior mesenteric a.
2) Middle – Peritoneum anterior wall, supplied by middle rectal a. ← [[Internal Iliac Artery|internal iliac a.]]
3) Inferior part – Subperitoneal, supplied by middle rectal a.

![[Pasted image 20250920005801.png]]

- Puborectalis m. – sling around anorectal junction
- Transverse rectal folds – shelfs to support fecal matter
##### Blood Supply
Venous
- Rectal venous plexus (connection between portal and systemic)
	- Superior rectal (portal) and middle / inferior rectal (systemic) 

>[!Clinical]
>[[Internal hemorrhoids]] – PAINLESS, dilation of internal rectal venous plexus (drains superior rectal v. into portal vein)
>[[External hemorrhoids]] – PIANFUL, pudendal n., dilation of external rectal venous plexus (drains inferior rectal v. → IVC)
##### Innervation
- Parasympathetic
	- Pelvic splanchnic – pain and distention, peristalsis
- Sympathetic
	- Lumbar splanchnic → inferior hypogastric n. 
- Somatic
	- External anal sphincter ← inferior rectal n.(S2-S4) ← [[Pudendal Nerve|pudendal n.]]
##### Lymphatic Drainage
- Superior rectum – inferior mesenteric lymph node → lumbar lymph nodes
- Middle and inferior and superior anal canal → internal iliac LN
- Inferior [[anal canal]] → superficial inguinal lymph nodes
