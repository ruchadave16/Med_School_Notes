*Posterior 1/3 of palate – mobile*

**Contents**
- Uvula 
- Palatoglossal arch
- Palatopharyngeal arch
- Fauces
- Palatine tonsils

![[Screenshot 2025-10-30 at 11.11.14 PM.png]]

#### Muscles
- Tensor veli palatini – [[Mandibular Nerve|CN V3]] – opens auditory tube, tenses palate
- Levator veli palatini – [[Mandibular Nerve|CN V3]] – elevates palate
- Palatoglossus – [[Vagus Nerve|CN X]] pharyngeal branch – elevates posterior tongue, depresses soft palate
- Palatopharyngeus – [[Vagus Nerve|CN X]] pharyngeal branch – lower soft palate, elevate [[larynx]]
- Musculus uvulae – [[Vagus Nerve|CN X]] pharyngeal branch – shorten uvula

![[Screenshot 2025-10-30 at 11.17.25 PM.png]]
#### Innervation
**Sensory**
- Lesser palatine n. off of [[Maxillary Nerve|CN V2]]
- Archs and palatine tonsil, uvula =  [[Glossopharyngeal Nerve|CN IX]] 

**Motor**
- [[Mandibular Nerve|CN V3]] (mandibular n.) → tensor veli palatini
- [[Vagus Nerve|CN X]] pharyngeal n. to rest

>[!Clinical]
>Lesion to [[Vagus Nerve|CN X]] (pharyngeal n.) can cause dysphagia and deviation of uvula because no soft palate elevation

#### Vasculature
- Lesser palatine a. ← [[Maxillary Artery|maxillary a.]]
