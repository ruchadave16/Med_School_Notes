*Guiding of substances into alimentary canal while protecting airway*

>[!Clinical] Difficulty swallowing = [[Dysphagia]]
>
>Main issue for quality of life

#### Phases
1) [[Chewing]]
	- Starts in mouth under voluntary but switches to involuntary
	- Reflex controlled by [[medulla]] in brain stem
	- Afferent from [[pharynx]] relayed by [[Vagus Nerve|vagus]] and [[Glossopharyngeal Nerve|glossopharyngeal n.]] nerves
2) Pharyngeal Phase
	- Moves food to [[esophagus]]
	1) [[Soft palate]] lifts to block path to [[nasopharynx]]
	2) Esophagus and larynx block opening of trachea
	3) Upper esophageal sphincter relaxes
	4) Peristaltic waves in pharynx push food down into esophagus
3) Movement through [[esophagus]]
	- Controlled by swallow reflex and [[enteric nervous system]]
	- Upper [[esophageal sphincter]] opened + closed by swallowing reflex
	- Primary Wave
		- Swallow reflex – [[Vagus Nerve]]
		- Sequential contractions to move food to stomach – Somatic in upper 1/3, autonomic in lower 2/3 through [[Ach]]
		- Assisted by gravity
	- Secondary Wave
		- [[Enteric nervous system]]
		- Initiated by esophagus distention if food remains after first wave
4) Relaxation of [[stomach]]
	- Lower esophageal sphincter opens when food enters distal end of esophagus
	- [[Vagus Nerve|Vagus]] releases [[VIP]] (vasoactive intestinal peptide) to open sphincter 
	- Stomach relaxes 

>[!Clinical]
>[[GERD]] – stomach contents to into esophagus damaging it
>
>[[Achalasia]] – Lower esophageal sphincter doesn’t relax
