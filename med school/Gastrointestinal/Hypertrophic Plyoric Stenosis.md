### Definition
Opening between [[stomach]] and [[small intestine]] stenosis
### Etiology
- Multifactoral genetic + environmental
- Prevalence (M:F ratio = 4:1)
- More common in Caucasian population (Scandinavian origin)
### Pathophysiology
- Opening thickens due to [[hypertrophy]] of longitudinal and circular muscles
	- Potential [[enteric nervous system]]
	- Food can’t enter [[duodenum]]
- Relevant pathways (e.g. immune, hormonal)

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- Forceful, **projectile, non-bilious vomiting** (0-6 months)
	- Symptoms start 3-5 weeks
	- Poor feeding
- Signs
	- Palpable olive-shaped mass
	- Weight loss
	- Crying without tears
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Surgery

### Complications
- Short-term
- Long-term
	- Untreated can lead to malnourishment and failture to thrive

- Classic buzzwords
- Boards clues
- Prevalence
	- 2-4 in 1000 births
	- More common in males
