*First stop for food – storage unit to control flow into [[duodenum]]*

**[[Chyme]]** – semi-fluid food made by stomach churning + digesting
#### Functions
- Exocrine: Digestive “juices” secreted
- Paracrine: [[Histamine]] — ↑ gastric acid secretion
- Endocrine: [[Gastrin]] – ↑ gastric mobility + acid secretion
#### [[Gastric Secretions]]
#### Gastric Mobility

1) Relaxation
	- Orad region accepts food from [[esophagus]]
	- Vasovagal reflex (afferent and efferent on [[Vagus Nerve|vagus]])
	- [[VIP]] = neurotransmitter to relax
2) Churning
	- Contractions to reduce size of bolus and mix in [[gastric secretions]]
	- Waves in **caudad region**
	- Food pushed to [[small intestine]] but pyloric sphincter contracted so nothing passes
	- [[Parasympathetic Nervous System|Parasympathetic]] increases contractions, [[Sympathetic Nervous System|sympathetic]], [[secretin]] and [[Gastric Inhibitory Peptide]] reduce
	- Contract once every 90 min even if empty (because of [[motilin]])
3) Emptying
	- Pushes chyme into small intestine at controlled rate with neutralization of acid
	- Total emptying = 3 hrs
	- Factors that slow
		- Fats – stimulate [[Cholecystokinin|CCK]] secretion to ensure lipids are digested
		- Acid – lowers pH and stimulates [[enteric nervous system]] which slows
