- $Cl^-$ secreted with $Na^+$ and $H_2O$ following passively
- $HCO_3^-$ secretion coupled with $Cl^-$ absorption in [[villus cells]] of [[ileum]] or epithelial cells of [[Large Intestine|colon]]

$Na^+$ 
- Always absorbed in intestine
- Nutrient-coupled sodium absorption
- Na-H exchangers
- Na-H and Cl-HCO3 exchangers in parallel
- Epithelial Na channels

$K^+$ 
- Small intestine is net absorber

Iron
- Mainly absorbed in [[duodenum]]

$Ca^{2+}$
- Controlled by [[PTH|parathyroid hormone]] and [[Vitamin D]]

>[!Clinical] [[Vitamin D]] Deficiency
>- Can cause low absorption of calcium — [[rickets]] in kids or [[osteomalacia]] in adults

>[!Clinical]
>[[Diarrhea]] – massive fluid loss causing ↓ arterial pressure
>- HCO3 and K+ lost in high amounts causing [[metabolic acidosis]] and hypokalemia
>  
>[[Lactose Intolerance]] – No lactase enzyme means lactose builds up in gut causing osmotic diarrhea
>
>[[Crohn Disease]]
>
>[[Celiac Disease]]

- Fat soluble [[vitamins]]
	- Processed like lipids
- Water Soluble vitamins
	- Most by Na dependent cotransport
	- [[Vitamin B12]] – absorbed in terminal [[ileum]] (needs intrinsic factor), deficiency → anemia
