*Controls all unconscious organs of body, vital for [[homeostasis]]* 

Motor: Involuntary control of [[Smooth Muscle|smooth muscles]], [[Cardiac Muscle|cardiac muscle]], and glands
Sensory: Involuntary sensation from organs

 1) [[Sympathetic Nervous System]]
	- *Fight or flight* – ↑ HR, ↑ BP, ↓ digestion

2) [[Parasympathetic Nervous System]]
	- *Rest and digest* – ↓ HR, ↑ digestion

>[!Clinical] Spinal Cord Injuries
>Can cause loss of sympathetic or parasympathetic control below injury
>- Bradycardia, hypertension, bowel and bladder control loss, temperature regulation
>
>[[Autonomic Dysreflexia]]
##### Neuron Motor Pathways
Preganglionic cell body in CNS → axon synapses on [[ganglia]] in body cavity
Postganglionic cell body in autonomic [[ganglia]] of PNS → axon synapse on organ

**Dual Innervation** – Most organs get parasympathetic and sympathetic (act opposite)
- Resting tone: constant stimulation of parasympathetic fibers

- Preganglionic bodies release [[ACh]], which binds to [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]] on postganglionic neurons
