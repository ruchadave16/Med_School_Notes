(L4-S3) – exits inferior to piriformis m. in greater sciatic foramen
- Enters lateral to ischial tuberosity and runs to biceps femoris long head
Motor to [[posterior thigh]], splits on popliteal fossa

- Tibial n. (L4-S3)
	- Between 2 heads of gastrocnemius, deep to soleus
	- Runs with posterior tibial a. 
	- Motor to posterior leg
	- Sensory through sural n. 
	- Splits at medial malleolus
		- Medial and lateral plantar nn. – foot sole (S2-S3)

>[!Clinical] 
>Tibial n. injury due to knee trauma, [[Baker cyst]], tarsal tunnel
>- Loss of PF and weak inversion
>- Sensory loss on sole of foot
>- Overextension of knee in gait
>
>[[Tarsal Tunnel Syndrome]]
>- Compression of tibial n. behind medial malleolus → pain and numbness in sole of foot 
>- Due to repeated stress, overweight, flat feet

- Common fibular n. (L4-S2) – follows biceps femoris medially to wrap around neck of [[fibula]]
	- Deep fibulary (peroneal) n. 
		- Deep to EDL (extensor digitorum longus)
		- Motor to anterior leg and dorsum of foot
		- Sensory to webbing between 1st and 2nd toe
		- Runs with anterior tibial a. 
	- Superficial fibulary (peroneal) n.
		- Deep to fibularis longus, pierces deep fascia to become superficial 
		- Motor to lateral leg
		- Sensory to lateral lower leg and dorsal foot

>[!Clinical]
>Common fibular n. injury at fibula neck or tight cast
>- Motor loss: DF, eversion, toe extension (foot drop)
>- Sensory loss: lateral lower leg and bottom of foot
>
>Deep fibular n. affected in [[anterior compartment syndrome]]

- Sural n. (S1-S2)
	- Combined branches of common fibulary n. and tibial n.
	- Superficial to gastrocnemius, runs with small saphenous v.
	- Superficial calf (posterior medial sensory)
