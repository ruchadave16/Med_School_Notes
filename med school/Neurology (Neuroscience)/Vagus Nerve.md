*Exits skull through jugular foramen*

**Course**
- Exits skull through jugular foramen
- Runs in [[carotid sheath]] behind [[Common Carotid Artery|common carotid a.]] and internal jugular v. 
- Anterior to 1st part of [[Subclavian Artery|subclavian a.]]
- [[Mediastinum]]
	- Right vagus n. lateral to brachiocephalic trunk, anterior to [[Subclavian Artery|subclavian a.]], anterior to [[esophagus]]
	- Left vagus n. lateral to aortic arch, posterior to [[esophagus]]
	- Both lateral to esophagus before going into [[diaphragm]]
	
**Branches**
Meningeal branch → dura mater
Superior laryngeal n.
- Internal and external branches
Auricular branch → [[external ear]] (auricle and external acoustic meatus) sensory
[[Recurrent Laryngeal Nerve|Recurrent laryngeal n.]] COMES OFF
- R around subclavian a.
- L around aortic arch

**Function**: 
- Visceral Motor (presynaptic parasympathetic): ↓ heart rate, [[bronchoconstriction]], peristalsis of GI tract 
- Somatic Motor: [[larynx]] muscles and superior [[esophagus]]
- Somatic Sensory: pharynx and larynx
- [[Visceral Afferent Fibers|Visceral afferent]] – unconscious sensations back to brain, neck, thorax, most of abdominal cavity

>[!Clinical]
>Damage causes sagging of soft palate, deviation of uvula to normal side, hoarseness of voice
