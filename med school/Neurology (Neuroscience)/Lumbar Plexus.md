>[!Clinical] Common Nerve Root Compression
>- [[Radiculopathy]]: compression of nerve root at spine
>- Herniated disc 
>- Herniated IV disc (L3-L4 disk would compress L4 nerve)
>- REMEMBER – lumbar nerves exit below vertebrae
>![[Screenshot 2025-10-06 at 9.21.22 PM.png]]

-  Ilioinguinal (motor, sensory to scrotum + superior thigh) (L1)
-  Iliohypogastric n. (motor, sensory to hypogastric) (from L1)
	- Enter posterior → medial arcuate line
	- Pierce transversus abdominis m. 
- Genitofemoral n. (L1-L2) – pierces psoas m., inferiorly on anterior surface
	- Genital branch enters [[deep inguinal ring]] and supplies [[cremaster m.]], sensory to skin of labia majora + anterolateral scrotum
	- Femoral branch to skin over [[femoral triangle]]
- Lateral femoral cutaneous n. (L2-L3)
	- Sensory to lateral thigh

>[!Clinical] [[Meralgia Paresthetica Syndrome]]
>Compression of [[Inguinal Ligament|inguinal ligament]] injures lateral femoral cutaneous n., loss of sensation to lateral thigh

- Obturator n. (L2-L4)
	- Through obturator foramen to [[medial thigh]]
	- Sensory to [[medial thigh]]
	- Motor to adductors in [[medial thigh]] (Obturator externus m. adductor brevis, pectineus, adductor brevis, adductor longus, gracilis, adductor magnus.)

>[!Clinical]
>Injury during pelvic surgery, fractures
>- Loss of sensation to medial thigh, ↓ adduction

- [[Femoral Nerve|Femoral n.]](L2-L4)
	- In [[femoral triangle]], lateral to [[Femoral Artery|femoral a.]] 
	- Motor to [[anterior thigh]]
	- Sensory to [[anterior thigh]]
	- Saphenous n. 
		- Branch through [[adductor canal]] NOT adductor hiatus
		- Sensory to medial leg below knee

>[!Clinical]
>Damage to [[Femoral Nerve|femoral n.]] (femoral triangle, anterior femoral head, inguinal hernia)→ inability to extend knee, ↓ patellar reflex, sensory loss to anterior thigh and medial leg
>- Buckling knee
>  
>  Damage to saphenous n. in [[adductor canal]] causes loss of sensation to medial leg
