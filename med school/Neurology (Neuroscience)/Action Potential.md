*Method of transmitting information along nerve or muscle cell*

- Rapid depolarization and repolarization
- All or none event

##### Steps
1) Depolarization from resting potential of -70mV. Happens when stimulus causes slight depolarization and threshold is reached (about 15 mV away from resting)
2) Opening of voltage-gated $Na^+$ channels causes entry of $Na^+$ into cell, which rapidly depolarizes to overshoot above 0 mV
3) Permeability now decreases and and $K^+$ permeability increases, so nerve repolarizes as $K^+$ exits
4) This undershoots resting potential causing hyperpolarization, which returns back to resting

Changes in permeability 
- Opening of gates to $Na^+$ as potential decreases
- Change in potential closes inactivation gates for this ion
- $K^+$ activation delayed 

**Absolute Refractory Period** – time when another action potential can’t take place because $Na^+$ inactivation gates are closed

**Relative Refractory Period** – time when hyperpolarization (can have action potential but only if inward $Na^+$ is more than normal)

**Accomodation** – membrane is slightly depolarized but less than threshold and some $Na^+$ inactivation gates are already closed so inward current cannot cause action potential

##### Nerve Conduction Factors
1) [[Nerve]] diameter → Larger nerve fiber means faster conduction
2) Myelination → increases membrane resistance so current flows down axon interior instead of along membrane
	- Nodes of Ranvier provide low resistance breaks so current jumps (saltatory conduction)
3) Time constants and Length constants

#### Inhibition + Activation
- Inhibition: Hyperpolarization: 
	- K+ channel open and efflux of K+
	- Cl- ions enter cell
- Activation: Depolarization
	- N+ influx, Ca2+ influx
- [[G Protein Coupled Receptors|GPCR]]
	- Gs → adenylyl cyclase → cAMP ↑ → PKA → excitability
	- Gq → phospholipase C → IP3 (Ca2+) DAG → PKC ↑
	- Gi → Presynaptic inhibition (close Ca2+), postsynaptic inhibition (K+ open), adenylyl cyclase inhibition (↓ cAMP)
