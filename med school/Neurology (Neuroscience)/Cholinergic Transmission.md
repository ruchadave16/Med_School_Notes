1) [[Ach]] Synthesis
	- Made in cytoplasm of nerve terminal from [[acetyl CoA]] (from mitochondria) and [[choline]] (from ECF) through enzyme **[[choline acetyltransferase]] (ChAT)**
2) Choline Uptake
	- Choline → terminal by Na-dependent choline transferase (CHT) 
	- RATE LIMITING STEP 
	- Inhibition: **[[hemicholinium]]**
3) ACh Storage
	- ACh moved to [[synaptic]] vesicles through vesicular acetylcholine transporter (VAT)
	- Inhibition: **[[vesamicol]]**
4) ACh Release
	- [[Action Potential|Action potential]] causes opening of volt-gated Ca2+ channels
	- Influx of calcium
	- Ca causes ACh vesicles to fuse with presynaptic membrane
	- ACh released into synaptic cleft
	- Inhibition: **[[Clostridium Botulinum|botulinum]]**
5) ACh action on Receptors
	- Binds to [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]] on postganglionic neuron or [[muscarinic receptors]] on organs
6) ACh degradation
	- [[Acetylcholinesterase]] breaks into choline and acetate
	- Half life: milliseconds

#### Drugs Impacting
- Cholinomimetic Drugs (Enhance Effects)
	- [[Direct Acting Cholinomimetics]]: bind directly to ACh receptors and activate them
	- Indirect: [[Cholinesterase Inhibitors|AchE inhibitors]], ensuring ACh stays in synapse for longer
- [[Anticholinergic Drugs]]

>[!Info] Muscarinic agonists can make [[asthma]] worse
