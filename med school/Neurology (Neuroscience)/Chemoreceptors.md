*Sensory receptors monitoring respiratory blood gases and pH to adjust ventilation*
#### Peripheral Chemoreceptors

![[Pasted image 20250818224115.png]]

- In aortic bodies and carotid bodies
- **Stimulate** ventilation due to
	- Decrease in arterial $P_{O2}$ – Not that sensitive since it has to decrease from 100 to <60 mmHg
	- Increase in arterial $P_{CO2}$ – More sensitive since small change causes increase in ventilation 
	- Decrease in arterial pH – Acid base disturbance stimulates ventilation
	
>[!Equation] $P_{CO2}$ + $H_2O$ → $H_2CO_3$ → $H^+$ + $HCO_3^-$

##### Central Chemoreceptors
- In [[brainstem]]
- Sensitive to change in pH of [[CSF]] and increase ventilation if pH ↓ (nonrespiratory) or $P_{CO2}$ increase
