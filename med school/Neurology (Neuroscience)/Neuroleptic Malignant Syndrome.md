### Etiology
- [[First Generation Antipsychotics]]

### Pathophysiology
- Rapid D2 receptor blockage

### Clinical Features
*Malignant FEVER*
- Symptoms
	- Myoglobinemia
	- Fever
	- Elevated enzymes (CK)
	- Vitals depressed
	- E
	- Lead pipe rigidity
	- Autonomic instability
- Signs
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
	- Immediate cessation of antipsychotic (switch to [[Second Generation Antipsychotics|atypical antipsychotics]])
	- [[Dantrolene]] to reduce muscle rigidity
	- [[Muscarinic Antagonists|Antimuscarinics]]
- Surgical
- Lifestyle
#### Common Confusions
- [[Serotonin Syndrome]]:
	- This has clonus and hyperreflexia, neuroleptic has rigidity and hyporeflexia
