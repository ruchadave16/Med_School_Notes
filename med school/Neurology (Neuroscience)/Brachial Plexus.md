*Rules of 5*
- 5 root combinations (C5-T1) between anterior and middle scalenes
- C5 = most superior
- 5 segments (roots, trunks, divisions, cords, terminal branches)
	- Roots and trunks = supraclavicular (proximal)
	- Divisions = retroclavicular (behind)
	- Cords = infraclavicular (distal)
	- Cords around [[Axillary Artery|axillary a.]]
- 5 terminal branches
	- Median n., radial n., ulnar n., musculocutaneous n., axillary n.

#### Roots (5)
- Ventral rami C5-T1
- Other nerves
	- Dorsal scapular n. (C4-5): Rhomboids, levator scapulae
	- Long thoracic n. (C5-7): Serratus anterior
#### Trunks (3)
- Superior (upper)
- Middle
- Inferior (lower)
- Other nerves
	- Suprascapular n. (C5-6): Supraspinatus, infraspinatus
#### Divisions (6)
- Anterior and posterior for each trunk
#### Cords (3)
- Medial 
- Lateral
- Posterior
- *cords + axillary a. wrapped in axillary sheath*
- Other nerves
	- Lateral
		- Lateral pectoral n. (C5-7) – pectoralis major
	- Posterior
		- Upper subscapular n. (C5-6) – subscapularis
		- Thoracodorsal n. (C6-8) – latissimus dorsi
		- Lower subscapular n. (C5-6) – subscapularis and teres major
	- Medial
		- Medial pectoral n. (C8-T1) – pectoralis major and minor
		- Medial antebrachial cutaneous n. (C8-T1) – skin
		- Medial brachial cutaneous n. (C8-T1) – skin
		
>[!Clinical]
>Location of brachial plexus block = axillary sheath

#### Terminal Branches (5)
- Make an M off of lateral/medial cords
	- Median n. (C5-T1)
		- Motor: Most of anterior [[forearm]] (FCR, palmaris longus, PT, FDS, AbPB, OpB, FPB superficial), lateral intrinsic hand
		- Branch: Anterior interosseous n. (AIN)
			- Between FDP and FPL
			- Motor to lateral 1/2 of FDP, FDL, pronator quadratus
		- Sensory: Anterior lateral hand (3.5)
		- Medial arm → [[cubital fossa]] (medial to brachial a.) → between pronator teres heads → deep of FDS, gives anterior interosseous n. → crosses wrist in [[Carpal Tunnel|carpal tunnel]] → gives off recurrent branch and makes small common digital n. 
	- Ulnar n. (C8-T1)
		- Motor: Anterior [[forearm] (flexor carpi ulnaris, medial 1/2 of flexor digitorum profundus)], medial lumbricals and central intrinsic hand (dorsal and plantar interosseous, adductor pollicis, deep head of flexor pollicis brevis)
		- Sensory: Medial anterior/posterior hand (1.5 fingers)
		- Medial brachium → cross elbow behind medial epicondyle in cubital tunnel → deep to flexor carpi ulnaris → enter wrist in Guyon’s canal
	- Musculocutaneous (C5-C7)
		- Motor to anterior [[arm]] (coracobrachialis, biceps brachii, brachialis)
		- Sensory to lateral [[forearm]] through lateral antebrachial cutaneous n. 
		- Pierces coracobrachialis
- Posterior cord
	- Axillary n. (C5-C6)
		- Motor: Teres minor and deltoid
		- Sensory: Patch over middle deltoid
		- Runs through quadrangular space around surgical neck of [[humerus]]

	- Radial n. (C5-T1)
		- Motor: Posterior brachium (triceps brachii) and posterior [[forearm]] (ECRL, brachioradialis)
		- Branches: 
			- Superficial radial n. – deep to brachioradialis → wrist comes posterior to tendon → across snuff box
				- Sensory to lateral dorsal hand and 2.5 finters 
			- Deep radial n. – from bifurcation to supinator
				-  Motor to supinator and ECRB
			- Posterior interosseous n. (PIN) (C7-8) – continuation of deep radial n. after supinator
				- Motor to ED, EDM, ECU, AbPL, AbPB, EPL, EI
		- Sensory: Posterior brachium, posterior antebrachium, posterior lateral hand (3.5)
		- Through triangular interval → deep to triceps brachii in radial groove → cross elbow deep to brachioradialis → anterior to radial head → divide into superficial and deep branches

>[!Clinical]
>Axillary n. most commonly damaged with fracture of surgical neck of [[humerus]] or [[shoulder dislocation]]
>- Cannot abduct arm past 15
>- No sensation over lateral surface of arm
>
>Musculocutaneous n. damaged due to upper trunk compression or at coracobrachialis
>- Motor: weak flexion of elbow, low biceps reflex
>- No sensory lateral forearm
> 
> Ulnar n. proximal injury 
> - [[Cubital Tunnel Syndrome]] behind medial epicondyle of humerus
> - Motor loss (DIP flexion) 4th and 5th, FCU, hypothenar, interossei, medial lumbrical
> - Radial deviation with wrist flexion
> - Sensory loss palmar and dorsal medial 1/3 hand/fingers
>   
>   Ulnar n. distal injury
> - Wrist = claw hand
>   
>  [[Ulnar Canal Syndrome]]
>  - Compression of ulnar n. near pisiform and hamate
>  - Positive Froment’s test
>   
>   Radial n. proximal injury
>   - Compression in [[Axilla|axilla]] = Saturday night palsy 
>   - Motor loss of elbow extension, wrist drop
>   - Sensory loss posterior arm and forearm, dorsal hand
>   - If injury from humeral fracture, just lose wrist extension and sensory to dorsal hand
>   
>   [[Posterior Osseous Nerve Syndrome]]
>   - posterior osseous n. trapped in supinator or radial head fracture
>   - Loss of finger extension
>
>Superficial Radial n. ([[Wartenberg’s Syndrome]])
>- Injury deep to brachioradialis or tight cast
>- Sensory loss to dorsal radial hand and fingers
> 
> [[Anterior Interosseous Nerve Syndrome]]
> - Injury deep to pronator teres
> - Loss of DIP flexion, thumb flexion
> - No OK sign
>   
>  Median n. proximal injury
>  - [[Pronator Teres Syndrome]] – under bicipital aponeurosis or between pronator teres heads
>  - Motor loss (wrist flexion, thumb movement, finger flexion)
>  - Sensory loss (lateral palm, 3.5 fingers)
>  - Papal sign
>    
>    Median n. distal injury
>  - Wrist laceration or [[Carpal Tunnel Syndrome]]
>  - No opposition of thumb
>  - Sensory loss (lateral palm, 3.5 fingers)
