1) [[Neurotransmitters]] Synthesis
	- Usually in cell body
	- Transported to axon body → terminal, stored in vesicles
2) Storage
	- Packaged into vesicles in terminal
	- Proton pump creates gradient to drive into vesicles
3) Release
	- SNARE proteins and synaptotagmin
	- Docking → priming → fusion → exocytosis
	- **Ca2+ dependent** – action potential opens volt gated Ca channels, Ca2+ influx, vesicle fusion
4) Termination 
	- Enzyme degradation of neurotransmitter like Ach
	- Reuptake by transporters (recycled into vesicles or broken down)
	- Uptake by glia
	- Diffusion away

>[!Warning] Toxins
>- [[Clostridium Botulinum|Botulinum]] (Botox)
>	- Inhibits neurotransmitter release by cleaving SNARE proteins
>	- No fusion with membrane, no release of Ach
>	- Flaccid
>- [[Clostridium Tetani|Tetanus]]
>	- Cleaves fusion so no GABA and glycine release
>	- NO inhibition of motor neurons, spastic paralysis
