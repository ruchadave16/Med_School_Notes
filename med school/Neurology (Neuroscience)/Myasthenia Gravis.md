*Serious Muscle Weakness, flutuating fatiguing*
##### Definition
Autoimmune disease caused by presence of [[Antibodies]] on postsynaptic [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]] of [[skeletal muscle]]

- People have different side effects and manifestations - “snowflake disease”
##### Etiology

##### Pathophysiology
- Autoantibodies reduce ACh [[nicotinic receptors]] on [[muscle end plate]] so less ACh binds 
- Postsynaptic EPP amplitude is reduced so its harder to depolarize the muscle membrane 
##### Clinical Features
- Progressive [[skeletal muscle]] weakness and fatigue, typically at end of day → can lead to death
- 85% present with eye problems: [[Ptosis]] (drooping) of both eyelids after repeated blinking exercises
- [[Diplopia]] → double vision
- Blurred vision
- 15% weakness
	- “Jaw tired” after chewing
- Associated tumors of [[thymus]]

Common aggravating
- Menstrual
##### Diagnosis
- Testing (double vision upon onset vs upon fatigue)
- **[[Edrophonium]]** for diagnosis testing and to identify crisis
	- [[Myasthenia Crisis]]: low AchE inhibitor levels, weakness improves with edrophonium
	- [[Cholinergic Crisis]]: ↑ [[Cholinesterase Inhibitors|AchE inhibitor]] levels, weakness does NOT improve with edrophonium

##### Treatment
- [[Cholinesterases|AchE inhibitors]] ([[Pyridostigmine]] (oral), [[Neostigmine]] (subcutaneous)). These stop AChE from degrading ACh to compensate for less receptors
	- [[Physostigmine]] most commonly used
	- **[[Atropine]]** used to control [[Muscarinic Receptors|muscarinic receptors]] effects
- Immune suppressants
- [[Plasmapheresis]] and intravenous [[immunoglobin]]
- Surgical → If [[thymoma]]

##### Complications / Side Effects
- [[Cholinesterase Inhibitors|AchE inhibitors]] - GI issues, runny nose, muscle spasms, moonface
	- Overactivity due to high ACh
- 15% will experience respiratory failure in lifetime (Myasthenia Gravis Crisis)
	- Air hunger
	- SoB
	- Weak cough
	- Accessory muscles

##### High-Yield Associations
