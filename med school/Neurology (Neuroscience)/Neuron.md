##### Components (Polar Organization)
- Cell body (soma)
	- Most are in CNS 
	- Nucleus – Large, euchromatic with **prominent nucleolus**
	- Cytoplasm – contains [[Nissl bodies]] (rough ER)
	- **[[Chromatolysis]]** = loss of [[Nissl bodies]] after nerve injury
- Axon 
	- Axon hilock – unmyelinated conical extension of soma
	- Terminal arborization
	- [[Synapse]] 
	- [[Axoplasmic Transport]] – bidirectional communication between body + axon
	- [[Wallerian degeneration]] – axon degenerate distal to injury and myelin sheath phagocyted by [[Microglia|microglial cells]] 
- Dendrites

##### Types of Neurons
- Unipolar – [[photoreceptors]]
- Bipolar – 1 axon, 1 dendrite. Senses neurons
- Pseudounipolar – 1 axon into 2 processes. Spinal and CN gangia
- Multipolar – 1 axon and 2+ dendrites

**Functional Neurons**
- Sensory Neurons (peripheral to CNS)
- Motor Neurons (CNS to motor cells) 
- Integrative (important for information processing + communication between sensory and motor)
