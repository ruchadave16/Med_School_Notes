*Cutaneous to lateral face, branch of [[trigeminal nerve]]*

**Path**
- Descends through foramen ovale to enter [[Infratemporal Fossa|infratemporal fossa]]

**Sensory Branches**
1) *Auricotemporal n.* 
	- Sensory to skin anterior to ear (auricle and external acoustic meatus) and skin on external surface of [[Tympanic Membrane|Tympanic membrane]]
	- Roots around middle meningeal a. 
	- Carries PSY fibers from otic ganglion (*tympanic n.* of [[Glossopharyngeal Nerve|CN IX]]) to [[parotid gland]]

2) *Inferior alveolar n.* 
	- Enters mandibular foramen → mandibular canal → innervates mandibular teeth
	- Terminates as *mental n.*

3) *Buccal n.* 
	- Anterior to lingual n. → oral mucosa of cheek

4) *Lingual n.* 
	- Anterior to inferior alveolar n.
	- Sensation (touch temp) to anterior 2/3 of tongue
	- Joined by *chorda tympani n.* (off of [[Facial Nerve|CN VII]]) which carries taste from anterior 2/3 and submandibular and sublingual glands

**Motor Branches** (run with sensory to 8 muscles)
- [[Chewing|Mastication]] muscles: temporalis, masseter, lateral and medial pterygoid
- Mylohyoid, anterior belly of digastric
- Tensor veli palatini
- Tensor tympani m.

*MMATT*


>[!Clinical]
>Damage to this nerve
>- Jaw fracture, infection, tumor in foramen ovale
>- Cannot clench jaw, difficulty chewing
>- Jaw deviates when open to affected side
>- Atrophy of mastication muscles
>- Sensory loss to V3 dermatomal distribution

>[!Clinical]
>Inferior alveolar n. block
>- Dentists to anesthetize mandibular teeth
>- Lingual and buccal also will be affected
>- Incorrect could affect facial n. if it pierces parotid gland
