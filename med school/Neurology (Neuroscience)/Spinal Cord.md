##### Location
- Adults: [[Foramen Magnum]] → L1/L2
- Children: further down
##### Structure
![[Screenshot 2025-08-01 at 10.50.27 PM.png]]

- Spinal cord segment: portion that gives rise to nerve roots forming 1 pair of spinal nerves
- Cervical enlargement (C4-T1): Creates nerves of [[brachial plexus]]
- Lumbosacral enlargement (T11-S1): Creates nerves of [[lumbar plexus]] and [[sacral plexus]]
- Conus medullaris: adult spinal cord terminates at L1/L2
- Caudal equina: Lumbar and sacral nerve roots extend distal to conus medullaris to reach exit 
##### Anatomy
![[Screenshot 2025-07-17 at 8.04.11 PM.png]]

- Gray horns (info in/out of spinal cord)
	- Ventral gray horn → somatic (motor) cell bodies
		- Ventral root → motor axons

>[!Clinical]
> [[Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis]] (Lou Gehrig’s disease)
> - Progressive degeneration of motor neurons in ventral horn

-
	- Lateral gray horn (Intermediolateral cell columns (IMLs)) → autonomic cell bodies
		- Axons go through Ventral Root
		- **Only in T1-L2**
	- Dorsal gray horn (sensory dendrites + interneurons)
		- Dorsal root → axons for neurons in dorsal horn
		- Dorsal root ganglia → cell bodies of sensory axons

- White columns (info to and from [[brain]])
- [[Spinal Nerve]]s: merger of roots → can contain both motor and sensory axons, located proximal to intervertebral foramen
	- Divides into rami
	- Dorsal rami: supply facet (vertebral) joints, deep back muscles
	- Ventral rami: supply body wall and limbs
