##### Definition
Failure of [[Vertebrae|vertebra]] arch fusion to spinous process due to failure of [[neural tube]] closing at [[neurulation]]

##### Etiology
- Related to [[folic acid]] deficiency → Decreased rate of incidence with supplements
- Happens **prior to** knowledge of pregnancy 

##### Pathophysiology
- Spina Bifida Occulta
	- Failure of 2 halves fusing normally at midline
- Spina Bifida Cystica
	- Incomplete closure of [[neural tube]] leads to vertebral arch failure to fuse/form 

##### Clinical Features
- Spina Bifida Occulta
	- Defect at L5 or S1 covered by skin
	- See sacral dimple or tuft of hair above defect
	- 10% of population
	- Neurological symptoms *absent*
	
![[Screenshot 2025-08-11 at 4.47.18 PM.png]]

- Spina Bifida Cystica
	- Protrusion of [[Spinal Meninges|spinal meninges]] and/or [[Spinal Cord|spinal cord]] through vertebra defect
	- [[Meningocele]] – meninges in cyst
	- [[Meningomyelocele]] – meninges AND neural tissue in cyst
	- 1/1000 live births
