*When nerve impulse is converted to chemical signal and then muscle action potential*

##### Components
- Motor end plate – terminal bouton in [[motor fibers]]
- Synaptic cleft – muscle cell depression that receives motor end plate
- Junctional folds – deep invaginations in plasma membrane of muscle cell
- Motor unit – 1 axon and all [[Muscle Tissue|muscle]] fibers it innervates
##### Steps

![[Screenshot 2025-08-07 at 5.00.15 PM.png]]
1) **[[Action Potential|Action potential]]** in presynaptic cell: Depolarization of presynaptic terminal
	- Synthesis and storage of [[ACh]] in presynaptic terminal
	- Choline acetyltransferase is catalyst for ACh formation from [[acetyl CoA]] and choline 
	- ACh stored in vesicles with ATP and [[proteoglycans]] 

![[Screenshot 2025-08-07 at 5.04.04 PM.png]]
2) **Depolarization of presynaptic terminal and $Ca^{2+}$ Uptake**
	- Depolarization of presynaptic opens calcium channels
	- When permeability increases, calcium enters terminal down electrochemical gradient
3) **$Ca^{2+}$ uptake releaces ACh into synaptic cleft**
	- Synaptic vesicles fuse with [[Cell Membrane|plasma membrane]] and empty contents through [[Membrane Transport|exocytosis]]
4) **Diffusion of ACh to postsynaptic membrane and binding to [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]]**
	- [[Nicotinic Receptors|Nicotinic receptor]] is Na/K ion channel
	- ACh binds to $\alpha$ subunits causing change conformationally that increases conductance to Na/K 
5) [[End Plate Potential]] (EPP) 
	- Postsynaptic membrane now depolarizes to a value halfway between Na and K equilibrium (about 0 mV)
	- 1 synaptic vesicle produces miniature end plate potential ([[MEPP]])
	- These sum to make EPP → NOT action potential, just depolarization of muscle end plate
6) **Depolarization of adjacent muscle membrane**
	- Once [[end plate]] depolarizes, this continues with action potential in adjacent muscle tissue
	- This is followed by contraction
7) **ACh Degradation**
	- EPP is transient since ACh is degraded to [[acetyl CoA]] and choline by [[acetylcholinesterase]] (AChE)
	- Half choline taken back into presynaptic through Na/choline cotransport to make new ACh
	- [[Cholinesterase Inhibitors|AchE inhibitors]] block degradation of ACh, causing it to stay in synapse and EPP increases in size
	- [[Hemicholinium]] blocks choline uptake and depletes presynaptic ends of ACh stores

>[!Clinical] [[Myasthenia Gravis]]
>Reduced ACh receptors on [[muscle end plate]] so EPP reduced
