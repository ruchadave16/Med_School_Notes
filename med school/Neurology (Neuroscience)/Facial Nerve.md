*Innervation of [[face]] muscles*

**Functions**
- Somatic motor: facial expression
- Parasympathetic motor: [[Lacrimation]], mucous production in nasal cavity and pharynx, stimulation of [[Submandibular Gland|submandibular gland]] and [[Sublingual Gland|sublingual gland]]
- Sensory: [[taste]] from anterior 2/3 of [[tongue]] and palate
- Somatic sensory: cutaneous to entrance of auditory canal of [[ear]]



![[Screenshot 2025-10-29 at 3.37.32 PM.png]]
#### Other Branches
- N. to stapedius
	- Branches off in facial canal

**DAMAGE BEFORE THIS: Bell’s palsy, face asymmetry, no salivation, loss of taste from anterior 2/3, sensitivity to sound**
#### Terminal Branch: Somatic Motor Innervation
**Location**
- Starts at brainstem in [[pons]], exits through internal acoustic meatus → facial canal → turn and leaves through stylomastoid foramen, passes through [[parotid gland]] WITHOUT innervating
- In facial canal, branch leaves to innervate stapedius

**Branches**
1) Temporal branch
	- Muscles above [[eye]]
2) Zygomatic branch
	- Cheek bone, nose, lower eye muscles
3) Buccal branch
	- Cheek, lower lip muscles
4) Mandibular branch
	- Lower mouth, chin muscles
5) Cervical branch
	- Neck muscles
	
![[Screenshot 2025-10-28 at 10.27.18 PM.png]]

>[!Clinical]
>Damage to facial n. happens near parotid gland
>- Damage to somatic fibers means no facial expressions (ipsilateral), hyperacusis

#### Parasympathetic Motor
**Location**
- Preganglionic bodies in brainstem → synapse on submandibular and [[pterygopalatine ganglion]]
- Submandibular/sublingual
	- Follow CN VII UNTIL chorda tympani branches off and runs through [[middle ear]] across tympanic membrane
	- **DAMAGE BEFORE THIS = Bell’s Palsy, facial asymmetry, low salivation, no taste from anterior 2/3 tongue**
	- Exits skull through petrotympanic fissure
	- Joins lingual n. [[Mandibular Nerve|CN V3]] to go to tongue
	- Synapse in submandibular ganglion → postsynaptic to salivary gland

![[Screenshot 2025-10-29 at 9.32.32 PM.png]]

- [[Nose]]/[[pharynx]]
	- Follow CN VII but at genu of facial canal, exit as greater petrosal n.
	- Runs in greater petrosal canal
	- Merges with deep petrosal n. (sympathetic fibers) to become n. to pterygoid canal in pterygoid canal
	- Synapse in [[pterygopalatine ganglion]] in [[pterygopalatine fossa]] → postsynaptic to mucus glands of nose and pharyx through branches of [[Maxillary Nerve|CN V2]]
- [[Lacrimal gland]]
	- Follows to synapse in [[pterygopalatine ganglion]]
	- Postgangionic parasympathetic → [[Maxillary Nerve|CN V2]] → lacrimal n. ([[Ophthalmic Nerve|CN V1]]) → lacrimal gland
	
![[Screenshot 2025-10-29 at 9.38.04 PM.png]]

**Branches**
- Chorda tympani n. – submandibular ganglion – submandibular and sublingual glands, carries taste from anterior 2/3
- Greater petrosal n. – [[pterygopalatine ganglion]] → mucous glands of nose and pharynx

>[!Clinical]
>Damage to parasympathetic causes dry eye, dry mouth, ↓ nasal secretions

#### Special Sensory
- Cell body in geniculate ganglion
**DAMAGE near geniculate ganglion = face asymmetry, low salivation, loss of taste from anterior 2/3, sensitivity to sound, AND no lacrimation**
- Greater petrosal n. (REVERSE)
	- Leaves palate on [[Maxillary Nerve|CN V2]] → [[pterygopalatine fossa]] on n. to pterygoid canal → greater petrosal n. → facial n.  → brain
- Chorda tympani n. (REVERSE)
	- Leaves tongue on lingual n. → branches onto chorda tympani n. → facial n. → brain
	
>[!Clinical]
>Damage means no taste from these regions

#### Cutaneous Sensory
- Cell bodies in geniculate ganglion
- At stylomastoid foramen, branch → auricle of [[external ear]]
	- Damage here = [[Bell's Palsy]], facial asymmetry

>[!Clinical]
>[[Bell’s Palsy]]
