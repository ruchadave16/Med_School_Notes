#### Secretion
- Release is pulsatile, diurnal 
- Controlled by [[ACTH]]
	- Burst right before waking
	- Stress increases burst amplitude (overrides negative impact)

**[[Negative Feedback]]**
- Cortisol blocks [[CRH]] and indirectly affects hippocampal neurons

#### Function
*Prepare body for stress*
- Stimulation of [[gluconeogenesis]]
	- Catabolic and diabetogenic
	- Protein breakdown in muscle
	- Lipolysis increase, lipogenesis in central fat depots
	- Lowers glucose usage (hyperglycemia) and insulin sensitivity
	- ↑ [[glycogenesis]]
- Anti-inflammatory
	- Synthesis of [[lipocortin]] (inhibits phospholipase A2)
	- Inhibits [[IL-2]] production and [[T Lymphocytes|T-lymphocyte]]
	- Inhibits [[histamine]] release and [[serotonin]] from [[mast cells]] and [[Thrombocytes|platelets]]
- Suppress immune system
	- Inhibits pro-inflammatory [[cytokine]] production
	- Inhibit phospholipase A2 so low [[prostaglandin]] and [[leukotriene]]
- Vascular response (constriction) to [[catecholamines]]
- ↓ [[Ossification|osteogenesis]]
	- ↓ [[collagen]] I synthesis
	- ↓ [[osteoblast]] function (muscle wasting)
	- ↓ Ca absorption → low intestinal absorption and ↑ renal excretion
- ↑ [[Glomerular Filtration]] through [[Blood Flow|vasodilation]] of afferent arterioles
- [[CNS]]
	- ↓ [[REM sleep]]
	- ↑ slow wave sleep
	- ↑ awake
- Can bind to [[Mineralocorticoids|mineralocorticoid]] receptors so Na+ retention and K+ excretion
	- Upregulates Na/K ATPase pumps
- Suppress [[growth hormone]] activity

>[!Warning]
>- Chronic glucocorticoid therapy = ↓ [[ACTH]] secretion, [[atrophy]] of [[adrenal cortex]]
>- MAKE SURE TO TAPER GRADUALLY so no adrenal insufficiency
> - Low immune response
> - CNS impacts can cause depression, lethargy, fatigue
> - Can also cause insomnia, paranoia, euphoria in excess due to ↑ waking
#### Activation Pathway
- Bind to cytoplasmic receptors (GRs) associated with heat shock proteins (Hsp90)
- After binding, substrate-receptor complex dissociates from those proteins → nucleus
- Binds to glucocorticoid response elements (GREs) in [[DNA]], increasing [[transcription]]
	- Activation → ↑ anti-inflammatory genes
	- Repression → ↓ pro-inflammatory genes
