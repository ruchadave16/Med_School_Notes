### Definition
↓ [[insulin]] production or defective [[insulin receptors]] causes ↑ glucose levels

### Etiology
Type I
- Autoimmune disorder

Type II
- Obesity
### Pathophysiology
Type I
- Destruction of beta cells of [[pancreatic islets]] → low insulin secretion
- No glucose gets to cells → liver breaks down lipids into ketoacids → [[Diabetic Ketoacidosis]]
- If glucose isn’t being reabsorbed enough, acts as osmotic diuretic pulling water out for excretion → osmotic diuresis

Type II
- Insulin resistance from over-exposure → downregulation of [[insulin receptors]]
- Secretion from beta cells is normal
### Clinical Features
**Symptoms**
Type I
- Polydispia (constant thirst)
- Polyphagia (constant hunger)
- Polyuria (high urination) 
- Osmotic diuresis

**Signs**
Type I
- ↑ blood glucose
- ↑ [[fatty acids]] and [[ketoacids]]
- ↑ blood [[Amino Acids|amino acid]]
- [[Hyperkalemia]]

Type II
- Similar symptoms but lesser degree

**Variants**
1) Type I
	- Insulin-dependent
	- Affects ppl < 20

2) Type II
	- Non-insulin dependent
	- Most common
	- Usually ppl > 40
	- [[Insulin]] levels can be normal but cells unresponsive

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
	- Type I: [[insulin]] injections
	- Type II:
		- Sulfonylurea drugs – ↑ insulin secretion
		- Biguanide drugs (metformin) – ↑ insulin receptors on tissues
		- SGLT2 inhibitors – ↓ reabsorption of glucose in [[Proximal Convoluted Tubule|proximal convoluted tubule]] so more is excreted
		- DPP-IV inhibitors, thiazolidinedione
- Surgical
- Lifestyle
	- Type II: weight loss and calorie restriction

### Complications
Type I
- [[Diabetic Ketoacidosis]] due to ↑ ketoacid production

Type II
- Persistent hyperglycemia → over glycosylation and breakdown of proteins
