### Etiology
- Overproduction of [[cortisol]] by [[adrenal cortex]]
- Administration of excess [[glucocorticoids]]

>[!Note] Different from [[Cushing Disease]]
>- Cushing syndrome has low ACTH because problem is cortex overproducing cortisol
>- Cushing disease has high ACTH 
### Pathophysiology
- Chronic excess of [[glucocorticoids]] (cortisol)
### Clinical Features
- Round face and central fat redistribution (Supraclavicular fat, buffalo hump)
- Central obesity
- Acne, ↑ fine hair growth
- Metabolic Change
	- Hyperglycemia
	- Increased proteolysis → muscle wasting
	- Increased lipolysis → thin limbs, thin skin
	- Loss of connective tissue → Striae
	-  [[Osteoporosis]]
- Immune Suppression
	- ↓ wound healing
- Hypertension
- Insomnia
- [[Hypokalemia]]
- Growth delay (in children)

### Diagnosis
- Dexamethasone suppression test to differentiate between Cushing Syndrome and Cushing Disease
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Blocking of steroid hormone biosynthesis
	- Ketoconazole
	- Metyrapone
- Surgical
	- Bilateral adrenalectomy + steroid hormone replacement
- Lifestyle
### Complications
- Short-term
- Long-term
