Calcium 1-2% in blood in 3 forms

1) Free iodized calcium (50%)
2) Protein-bound calcium (40-45%)
3) Calcium w anion (5-10%)

>[!Clinical]
>**VERY IMPORTANT** disorders if high or low
>- [[Hypercalcemia]]
>- [[Hypocalcemia]]

#### Causes
- Change to [[Acid-Base Balance]]
- Change to plasma protein concentration
	- Slow, over time
	- If protein conc. ↑, total Ca ↑ 
- Change in anion concentration
	- Changes fraction of Ca complexed with anions
