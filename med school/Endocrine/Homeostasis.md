*”reaching and maintaining a balance”*

This is the process by which the body regulates and maintains internal conditions despite external changes

![[Screenshot 2025-07-12 at 5.00.49 PM.png]]
##### Key Components
1) Setpoint: physiological value around which normal range fluctuates. 
2) Receptor (sensor): detects changes in environment and sends information to control center
3) Control Center: processes changes and sends signals to correct deviation to setpoint ([[hypothalamus]] is common for many processes)
4) Effector (target): carries out response to restore to normalcy

To maintain homeostasis, [[Regulation of Hormone Secretion|hormone secretion is regulated]] by being turned on or off

#### Cell Response
![[Screenshot 2026-01-23 at 3.42.19 PM.png]]

- [[Cell Adaptations]] = reversible responses to changes
- [[Cell Injury]] = when limits of adaptation are crossed, can result in cell death through [[necrosis]] or [[apoptosis]]

>[!example] 
>**Thermoregulation:** sweating and shivering to return body temperature back to normal (negative)
>
>**Blood Glucose Regulation:** Insulin lowers blood glucose by increasing uptake into cells but glucagon raises blood glucose by stimulating release from liver
>
>**[[Calcium Homeostasis]]:** [[PTH]] increases blood $Ca^{2+}$ by stimulating blood resorption, [[calcitonin]] lowers blood $Ca^{2+}$ by inhibiting blood resorption, and vitamin D 


>[!disorders] 
>**[[Diabetes Mellitus]]**: condition where blood glucose regulation is impaired, leading to high blood glucose levels.
>	- can result from insufficient insulin production or insulin resistance
>	
> [[Hyperparathyroidism]]: excess production of PTH leads to high [[blood calcium]]
