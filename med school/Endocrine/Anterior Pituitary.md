*Clusters of glandular cells associated with blood sinusoids secreting 6 main hormones*

- Glandular portion that regulates the target organs
- Not directly innervated by hypothalamus
- Communication through circulatory system 
- Hormone release controlled by releasing/inhibiting hormones from [[hypothalamus]]

![[Screenshot 2025-10-21 at 2.48.38 PM.png]]
#### Compartments
1) Pars distalis
	- Minimal connective tissue, fenestrated capillaries, 
	- 3 types of cells
		- Chromophobes – clear
		- Chromophils – acidophils (red) and basophils (blue)
	- Cells arranged in nests with reticulin network

2) Pars intermedia
	- Poorly developed in humans
	- Has Rathke’s cyst remnants
3) Pars tuberalis
	- Around infundibular stalk 

![[Pasted image 20251108141418.png]]
#### Hormones
**Acidophils**
- Somatotrophs – [[Growth Hormone|GH]] (Growth hormone)
- Lactotrophs – [[Prolactin]]

![[Screenshot 2025-10-19 at 11.09.22 PM.png]]
**Basophils**
- Corticotrophs – [[ACTH]] (Adrenocorticotrophs)
- Thyrotrophs – [[TSH]] (Thyroid stimulating hormone)
- Gonadotrophs – [[FSH]] and [[LH]] (Follicle stimulating hormone and Luteinizing hormone)
![[Screenshot 2025-10-19 at 11.10.11 PM.png]]
#### Embryology
- Oral [[ectoderm]] from roof of primitive mouth
- Migrates to brain forming Rathke’s Pouch

>[!Clinical]
>Normally Rathke’s Pouch disappears but sometimes it can leave tissue behind as colloid-filled cysts
