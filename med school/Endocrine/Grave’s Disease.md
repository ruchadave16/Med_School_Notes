### Etiology
Autoimmune 
### Pathophysiology
Increase in circulating thyroid-stimulating immunoglobulins
- These bind to [[TSH]] receptors in [[Thyroid Gland|thyroid]] follicular cells
- Stimulate release of [[thyroid hormones]] and hypertrophy of gland
### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- **Exophthalmos** = the protrusion of one or both eyes anteriorly out of the orbit ← retro-orbital edema and fat → UNIQUE to Grave’s
	- Pretibial Myxedema = skin lesions ← deposition of [[hyaluronic acid]]
	- Non-pitting edema
- Signs
- Variants

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
	- Drugs to inhibit [[Thyroid Hormones|thyroid hormone]] synthesis
- Surgical
	- Removal of thyroid gland
	- Radioactive ablation of gland with I-131
- Lifestyle

### Complications
Causes [[hyperthyroidism]]
