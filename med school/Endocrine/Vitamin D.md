*2nd regulator of calcium levels after PTH*

**Active form** = 1,25-(OH)2-Cholecalciferol aka 1,25-(OH)2-D3 aka **Calcitriol**
**Storage Form** = 25-(OH)-Vitamin D

- [[Vitamin D2]] (ergocalciferol)
	- Plants, fungi, yeast, fortified foods
#### Synthesis
- [[Vitamin D3]] = precursor (Cholecalciferol) 
	- Get through diet (fish, milk, plants) or converted from 7-Dehydrocholesterol through UV light in [[skin]]
	- Inactive form
- Converted to **25-OH-D3** in [[liver]]
	- Released to blood, inactive
	- Main circulating form
- Activated in [[kidneys]] to [[calcitriol]] WHEN
	- ↑ [[PTH]]
	- ↓ calcium
	- ↓ phosphate

#### Actions
- Increase [[blood calcium]] and phosphate
- Intestines: ↑ absorption of calcium and phosphate
- Kidneys: ↑ absorption of calcium and phosphate
- Bone: [[Osteoclast]] activity ↑, and also [[bone mineralization]] ↑ 
#### Toxicity
- Hypercalcemia
- Hypercalciuria
- Stupor
- Loss of appetite

>[!Clinical]
>Deficiency
>- [[Rickets]] in children
>	- epiphyseal widening and metaphyseal cupping/fraying on Xray
>	- pathological bow legs
>	- beadlike costochondral junctions
>	- craniotabes
>- [[Osteomalacia]] in adults
>	- osteopenia and pseudofractures in xray
>	- bendy and soft bones
>- Hypocalcemic tetany


>[!Clinical]
>[[Vitamin D Resistance]]
>- Kidney can’t produce 1,25-dihydroxycholecalciferol
>- Supplements don’t help
>- Can be caused by chronic kidney disease OR absence of 1$\alpha$-hydroxylase
