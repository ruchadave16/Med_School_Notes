*Hormones containing [[iodine]] that are lipophilic*

- [[Triiodothyronine]] (T3)
- Tetraiodothyronine, or [[thyroxine]] (T4)

These are bound to serum proteins in blood (99% – [[thyroxine-binding protein]] (TBG) ← liver)
- **ONLY free hormones are active**
- TBG provides reserve
#### Actions
Increase metabolic activities in tissues
- Receptors in nuclei of cells, >> affinity for t3

![[Screenshot 2025-10-22 at 9.29.40 PM.png]]

- ↑ Basic metabolic rate
	- ↑ Oxygen consumption
	- ↑ body temp
- Metabolism
	- ↑ glucose absorption in GI tract
	- ↑ [[gluconeogenesis]], lipolysis, proteolysis
	- net muscle loss
- CV and Resp
	- ↑ [[cardiac output]] and ventilation
- Growth
	- ↑ [[ossification]]
- CNS
#### Synthesis
- Come from [[thyroglobulin]]

1) Iodine Organification: [[Tyrosine]] on thryoglobulin are iodinated
	- MIT if one iodine
	- DIT if 2 iodine

![[Screenshot 2025-10-22 at 8.53.12 PM.png]]

	- Stays iodinated until stimulation of thyroid gland to secrete hormones

2) Coupling: Iodinated residues come closer and get coupled
	- MIT + DIT = [[triiodothyronine]] (T3)
	- DIT + DIT = [[thyroxine]] (T4)
	
![[Screenshot 2025-10-22 at 8.57.22 PM.png]]

	- Thyroxine is made at a MUCH faster rate than T3

3) Endocytosis 
	- When [[thyroid gland]] is stimulated, colloid containing thyroglobulin (with T3, T4, MIT, DIT) endocytosed

4) Hydrolysis
	- Thyroglobulin droplets combine with [[lysosome]] membranes → break peptide bonds to release T3, T4, DIT, MIT
	- T3 and T4 are secreted
	- MIT and DIT stay and are deiodinated 

#### Conversion
- **T3 is 3x as potent as T4** but made slower
- 5’-iodinase converts T4 → T3
#### Secretion
- T3 and T4 transported to capillaries → systemic circulation

#### Inactivation
- T4 can be deactivated by removing iodine from inner ring making reverse T3
- T3 and rT3 → DIT
- T4 and T3 can also → glucuronide conjugates
