*Molecules produced by [[Endocrine System|endocrine]] cells in response to a stimuli*

- Target cells have receptors that bind to these hormones and initiate responses
- Neurocrine: when neurons release hormones into blood
#### Classes
1) [[Steroid Hormones]]
	- Hydrophobic, lipid soluble
	- Directly cross into cell and bind to intracellular
	- NOT stored in cell
	- Made from [[cholesterol]]

2) [[Peptide Hormones]]
	- Very diverse, hydrophilic

3) [[Amine Hormones]]
	- Made from [[tyrosine]]
	- [[Catecholamines]] (hydrophilic)
	- [[Thyroid Hormones]] (lipophilic)

#### [[Regulation of Hormone Secretion]]
#### Patterns of Secretion
- Time based – cyclic
- Stimulus based – pulsatile based on stimulus

#### Patterns of Action
- Only acts on cell with receptor of hormone
- Peptides and catecholamines → cell surface receptors 
	- Signal cascade
- Steroid and thyroid → intracellular
	- Mainly through gene [[transcription]]

#### Peripheral Transformation
- Some formed from precursors after secretion

>[!Example]
>- [[Thyroxine]] – converted outside thyroid to triiodothyronine (active)
>- [[Testosterone]] – converted to dihydrotestosterone and even estrogen

#### Hormone Degradation
- Most through filtering in [[kidneys]]
- Inactivation through enzyme in blood or extracellular spaces
- Can be completely metabolized
