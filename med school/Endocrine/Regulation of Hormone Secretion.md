Can be achieved by neural mechanisms or by feedback mechanisms 

- Neural mechanisms through secretion of [[catecholamines]] → pre-ganglionic sympathetic nerves synapse onto [[adrenal medulla]] and secrete them when stimulated

- Feedback mechanisms are MUCH more common

1) [[Negative Feedback]]: some [[Hormones|hormone]] action inhibits further secretion of that hormone

>[!example] If blood glucose rises, [[insulin]] is released to reduce blood glucose


2) [[Positive Feedback]] *(less common)*: some hormone action increases secretion of hormone

>[!example] [[Birth|Childbirth]] - [[oxytocin]] during childbirth increases contractions so delivery happens

	- Usually leads to a change in body status, not return to homeostasis
