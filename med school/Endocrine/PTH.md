*Hormone that maintains normal serum $Ca^{2+}$ levels*

- Single chain polypeptide of 84 AA
- Made on [[Ribosome|ribosomes]] as preproPTH with 115 AA
- Processed in [[Golgi Apparatus|Golgi body]] into secretory granules
#### Synthesis
- Chief cells of [[Parathyroid Gland|parathyroid glands]]
#### Functions
![[Screenshot 2025-10-22 at 11.44.18 PM.png]]
**[[Bone]]**
- Increase bone reabsorption, bringing $Ca^{2+}$ and phosphate to ECF ([[Calcium Homeostasis|calcium homeostasis]])
- Initially PTH receptor on osteoblasts → increases bone deposition
- 2nd action on osteoclasts through RANKL

**[[Kidneys]]**
- Increase Ca reabsorption in [[Distal Convoluted Tubules|distal convoluted tubule]] (generally only 1-2% excreted but PTH can reduce this even more)
- Decrease phosphate reabsorption in [[Proximal Tubular Transport|proximal tubular transport]] – more free ionized Ca
- Increases 1,25-dihydroxycholecalciferol production (to activate [[Vitamin D]])

**[[Small Intestine]]**
- *Indirectly* stimulates Ca reabsorption in small intestine through Vitamin D activation in kidneys
#### Secretion
- Released when plasma $Ca^{2+}$ ↓ and other way around
- Chronic change in $Ca^{2+}$ can cause long term
	- Chronic hypocalcemia → secondary hyperparathyroidism (↑ synthesis and storage of PTH, hyperplasai of parathyroid)
	- Chronic hypercalcemia → (↓ synthesis and storage of PTH, )

![[Screenshot 2025-10-22 at 11.27.04 PM.png]]

- Magnesium has similar effect but lesser degree (↓ Mg = ↑ PTH)

>[!Warning] Exception
>Severe [[hypomagnesemia]] inhibits PTH (seen with alcoholism) 

- 1,25-dihydroxycholecalciferol inhibits PTH secretion and storage
