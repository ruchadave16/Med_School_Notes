[[Epinephrine]] and [[norepinephrine]]
#### Synthesis
- [[Tyrosine]] is precursor (transported to nonadrenergic terminals through Na-dependent carrier)
	- Tyrosine → [[DOPA]] (tyrosine hydroxylase, **rate limiting step**)
	- [[DOPA]] → [[dopamine]] (DOPA-decarboxylase)
	- [[Dopamine]] → [[norepinephrine]] (dopamine $\beta$-hydroxylase, in vesicles)
	- [[Norepinephrine]] → [[epinephrine]] (phenylethanolamine N-methyltransferase, mainly happens in [[Adrenal Medulla|adrenal medulla]])
- [[Cortisol]] needed to make epinephrine from norepinephrine
- [[ACTH]] stimulates creation of norepinephrine
#### Release
- [[Dopamine]] transported into vesicles by VMAT (vesicular monoamine transporter)
	- Inhibition: [[reserpine]]
- Release depends on Ca2+ when action potential causes exocytosis
	- Inhibition: [[bretylium]], [[guanetidine]]
- Binds to [[adrenergic receptors]]
- Reuptake by norepinephrine transporter (NET)
	- Inhibition: [[cocaine]], [[Pharmacology/Tricyclic Antidepressants|Tricyclic Antidepressants]]  
#### Regulation
- Secreted by [[Sympathetic Nervous System|sympathetic]] innervation
- Metabolism
	- Monoamine Oxidase ([[MAO]]): breaks down in nerve terminal
	- Catechol-O-Methyltransferase (COMT): breaks down in synaptic cleft
		- Found in adrenal medulla and peripheral cells
#### Function
- Stress response – fight or flight
- ↑ BP
- ↑ breathing rate
- ↑ heart rate
- ↑ metabolic rate
- ↑ blood glucose
