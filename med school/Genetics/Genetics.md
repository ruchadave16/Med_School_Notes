Autosomal Inheritance: Genetic traits passed from parents to offsprings through autosomes

Pleiotrophy – Phenomenon when 1 gene affects multiple phenotypes

- Penetrance: Number of individuals with genotype that express the phenotype
- Expressivity: Degree to which genotype is seen in phenotype

[[Codominance]] – 2 alleles seen equally in organism (both seen) (see black and white spots)
Incomplete dominance – neither masks the other, see blend (black and white – see gray)

Anticipation: genetic disorders seen more early as they’re passed down generations

Uniparental Disomy: 2 copies from 1 parent, none from other parent 
Mosaicism: 2+ genetically different sets of cells

Allelic Heterogeneity: Different alleles at same locus show same phenotype
Locus Heterogeneity: Mutation at diff loci cause same pathology

Sex-*limited* trait – Only seen in 1 sex
Sex-*influenced* trait – Happens in both but seen more in one over another
- Related to hormone level differences

Multifactoral Inheritance – 1+ genes combine with environmental factors to cause condition
- Sometimes frequency of disease doesn’t follow bell shaped distribution. Here, symptoms absent until threshold exceeded

[[Population Genetics]] – Forces that transmit and select genetic variants

Polygenic Inheritance – When inheritence depends on multiple genes
- Each gene provides incremental change to phenotype
- Some are more or less impactful

Gene Mapping: Used to know location of gene on chromosome 

Linkage Disequilibrium: Non-random association of alleles at different loci showing that some genes are inherited together (linked)
