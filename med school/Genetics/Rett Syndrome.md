### Etiology
- Mutation in MECP2 gene on X chromosome

### Pathophysiology
- Synapse formation is not regulated → low plasticity
- Progressive neurodevelopmental abnormalities

### Clinical Features
- Progressive loss of acquired skills
- Hand wringing
- Loss of speech and motor
- Seizure and scoliosis

![[Pasted image 20251209211527.png]]

### Diagnosis
- Labs
	- Genetic testing for MECP2 mutation
- EEG to monitor seizures and rule out other causes
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Symptomatic treatment of seizures, breathing issues, motor
- PT and occupational therapy
- Communication devices

### Complications
- Short-term
- Long-term
