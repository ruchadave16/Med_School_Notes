Because men only have 1 X chromosome, they will express all mutations
- Woman can show WT, htereozygous, or homozygous

**X Recessive**
- [[Hemophilia A]]
- [[OTC Deficiency]]
- [[Duchenne Muscular Dystrophy]]
	- Mutation of DMD gene that makes [[dystrophin]]
	- Weakness progresses, loss of muscle
	- 20x HIGHER creatine kinase 
	- 8-10% heterozygous females show symptoms
		- Skewed X inactivation (DMD/WT) ← only express mutant
		- Uniparental isodisomy (DMD/DMD) ← both copies from mother
		- New germline mutation 

**X Dominant**
- [[Hypophosphatemic Rickets]]
	- Affects men and women
	- Mutation in PHEX gene (phosphate regulating endopeptidase)
	- Does NOT skip generations
	- Defect in renal phosphate resorption
- [[Rett Syndrome]]
	- Normally sporatic mutation
	- Males
		- Neonatal – encephalopathy
	- Females (early normal)
		- Later: slow growth, high mortality, cardiac arrest
	- 2/3 patients have mutation in MeCP2 
		- This gene binds to metylated DNA for gene silencing
		- Defect: Genes that should be silenced aren’t
	- 1/3 have mutation in CDKL5
		- CDKL5 kinase phosphorylates and activates MeCP2
