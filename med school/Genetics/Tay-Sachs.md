### Etiology
- Autosomal recessive 
	- Deficiency of hexosaminidase A
	- Incidence = 1:360,000
- Risk factors
	- Ashkenazi Jewish descent: Incidence = 1:3,600
### Pathophysiology
- Loss of [[lysosome]] enzyme: hexosaminidase A
- Accumulation of GM2

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- Accumulation of GM2 in retina
	- Neurological symptoms: degeneration, seizures, blindness, muscle weakness
- Signs
	- “Startle response”, seen between 3-6 months of age
	- “Cherry red” macula
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
None
### Complications
Death by 2 yrs
