### Etiology
- Genetic: 45 XO
- No Barr bodies
- Frequency: 1 / 5000 female births
### Pathophysiology
- Normally most genes on the second X are silenced, but 15% remain active
- In Turner, don’t produce enough of the 15% of genes that are normally NOT silenced on the extra X
### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- Learning disability
- Signs
	- Neck webbing, broad chest, wide nipples, gonadal dysgenesis
	- Puffy feet
	- Short stature ← haploinsuﬃciency of SHOX gene
- Variants
