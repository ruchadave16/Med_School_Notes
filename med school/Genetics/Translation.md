- On [[Ribosome|ribosomes]]

1) Initiation 
2) Elongation
3) Termination
#### Initiation

- $tRNAi^{Met}$ binds eIF2-GTP with initiation factors (eIF3)
	- Bind to small (40S) ribosomal
- Initiation factors scan 5’ onwards (use ATP)
- Find AUG by binding to met-tRNA anticodon
- eIF4F binds 
	- 60S joins with 40S
	- Uses GTP

**Polysome**: Once 1 starts moving, another ribosome can bind to empty 5’
#### Regulation
- Kinases activate eIF (eukaryotic initiation factors)

[[Insulin]] – eIF4E activation
- Normally inactive, bound to inhibitor (4E-BP)
- Phosphorylation releases eIF4E and activates it

Normally eIF2 inactive – Heme kinase phosphorylation
- [[Heme]] blocks this to increase translation of globin for [[hemoglobin]] 
#### Elongation
- Initially $tRNAi^{Met}$ in P site
	- Binds to aminoacyl-tRNA activated by eEF1A-GTP at A site
- **Peptidyl transferase** forms bond between methionine at P site and AA at A site
	- Frees $tRNAi^{Met}$ and ribosome moves 1 down
- tRNA at E, new peptidy-tRNA at A site
	- [[GTP]] used releasing these
- A site can accept new tRNA
#### Termination
- Release Factors
	- RF-1: UAA or UAG
	- RF-2: UAA or UGA
- Peptidyl transferase moves to water instead of new AA

*Next Steps*
- Folding with help of chaperone proteins

- Methionine might be removed
- Potential cleavage to active fragments 
- Modification of amino acids:  Phosphorylation, Glycosylation, Fatty acid acylation, Acetylation, Carboxylation, Methylation, Hydroxylation, ADP-Ribosylation, Ubiquitination

>[!Clinical]
>[[Diphtheria]] – bacteria *Corynebacterium diphtheriae*
>- No protein synthesis
>- ADP-ribose transfered from NAD+ → diphthamide
>  
>  [[Shiga Toxin]] and [[Veratoxin]]
>- Enterohemorrhagic *E coli* infections
>- Inactive 28S rRNA in 60S ribosomes
>	- Shiga has RNA glycosylase that removes adenine from 28S
>	- No binding of aminoacyl-tRNA
>- No protein synthesis

>[!Clinical] [[Antibiotics]]
>- [[Streptomycin]] – Binds to 30S, stops initiation complex
>- [[Tetracycline]] – Binds to 30S, stops binding of aminoacyl-tRNA to A site
>- [[Chloramphenicol]] – Binds to 50S, stops peptidyltransferase
>- [[Erythromycin]] – Binds to 50S, stops translocation
>- [[Puramycin]] – Analog of aminoacyl-tRNA, stops BOTH eukaryotic and prokaryotic translation
