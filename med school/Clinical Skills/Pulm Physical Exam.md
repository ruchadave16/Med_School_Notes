#### Important Notes
- Neurovasculature in inferior groove of ribs so place needles on superior side of rib
	- Decompression of thoracic [[pneumothorax]] – 2nd intercostal
	- [[Chest tube]] insertion: 4th intercostal space (midaxillary at triangle of safety)
	- Lower margin of a well-placed endotracheal tube on a chest x-ray: 4th rib
- R main bronchus is wider and shorter so [[aspiration pneumonia]] more common in R middle + lower lobes
- Irritation of [[pleuritis]] causes sharp pain (knifelike) that increases because of inhalation

#### Physical Exam
##### Thorax Inspection
- Rate, rhythm, depth of breathing – walk around and back in 1 breathing cycle
- Delayed expiration – COPD

##### Peripheral Inspection
- Nail beds (cyanosis) 
	- Check clubbing
- [[Lips]] (cyanosis)
- Cap Refill – same finger on both hands

*No signs of respiratory distress, tachypnea, cyanosis, clubbing, or use of accessory muscles for respiration*

##### Thorax Palpation
- **3 for 3 circling motion**
- Lower gown
- Locations (1 + 4) – ask to lift breast tissue and then lift gown back up
- Ask for pain / tenderness

- Open back
- Chest Expansion
	- Hand along 10th ribs
	- Ask patient to exhale and inhale completely
- Tactile Fremitus
	- Ask to hug self
	- Along scapular like (1 + 2, 2 + 3, 3 + 4) say blue balloons
	- ↓ or absence = thick chest wall, obstructed bronchus, COPD, pleural effusion, fibrosis, infiltrating tumor
	- Asymmetric = ↑ chance of unilateral pleural effusion, pneumothorax, neoplasm
	- ↑ fremitus = unilateral pneumonia
- TEST CVA Tenderness now between spine + 12th rib
*No generalized tenderness of thorax. Expansion and fremitus are bilaterally symmetrical*


##### Percussion
- Keep hands crossed
- Go down in ladder 1, 2, 3, 4
- Resonance = normal
- Dullness = fluid or tissue
- Hyperresonance = hyperinflated lungs (COPD, pneumothorax, chronic bronchitis)
- Tympanic = gastric air bubble (large pneumothorax)

*Lung fields resonant + symmetric to percussion bilaterally*

##### Diaphragmatic Excursion
- Generally do bilaterally
- Exhale completely and hold
- Start at base of scapula, percuss to find lower level of lungs
- Inhale completely 
- Find new base (should be 3-5 cm below)
- Very high amount = pleural effusion or elevated hemidiaphragm

##### Auscultation
- Breath in and out with open mouth every time stethascope touches, move in ladder
- 1 + 4 anterior, 1, 2, 3, 4 posteriorly (DRAPING FRONT AFTER)
- WHOLE breath cycle
- ↓ Sounds
	- ↓ airflow or low sound transmission
	- If bronchovesicular sounds at unexpected locations, fluid filled lung or consolidated tissue

- Crackles
	- Fine – abnormal lung tissue
	- Coarse – COPD, asthma, bronchiectasis, pneumonia, heart failure
- Wheezes
	- In sever asthma, silent lung – **EMERGENCY**
- Rhonchi
- Stridor
	- Narrowing of upper respiratory tract, loudest at neck 
- Pleural friction rub

##### Transmitted Voice Sounds (Signs of [[Pneumonia]])
- E-A egophony
	- If sounds like A, egophony present
	- Consolidation of tissue
- Whispered pectoriloquy
	- Whisper 1-2-3
	- If loud, present
