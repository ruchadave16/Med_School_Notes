#### Health History
- Concerning symptoms
	- Chest pain
	- Palpitations
	- SOB
	- Swelling (edema)
	- Fainting (syncope)
- [[Chest Pain]]
	- Location, exertion, types of activities, intensity, radiation, symptoms associated, nocturnal occurrence, relieving factors
	- Baseline activity
	- Atypical (Women >65 → upper back, neck, jaw pain, SOB, nausea, vomiting, PND, fainting)
	- Diagnoses LIFE THREATENING (Angina pectoris, MI, dissecting aortic aneurysm, pulmonary embolus)
- [[Palpitations]]
	- Assess – ask patient to tap rhythm
	- Serious dysrhythmias *do not* cause palpitations generally
- [[SOB]]
	- Quantify start, change over time, occurrence
	- Orthopnea – dyspnea when supine, okay when sitting up
	- Paroxysmal Nocturnal Dyspnea – nighttime dyspnea waking up from sleep
		- 1-2 hr after falling asleep
		- Associated wheezing / coughing?
- Swelling ([[Edema]])
	- Location, timing, setting, associated symptoms
	- Interstitial tissue can absorb 5L + before edema shows so rapid weight gain before edema shows
- [[Fainting]]


- [[PAD]]
	- Risk factors ≥ 50, smoking, diabetes, hypertension, ↑ [[cholesterol]], African American descent, CAD
	- Pain/Cramping in legs with exertion (only 10% have this)
		- Relieves in 10 min
	- Atypical leg pain (can carry on but still pain w exertion)
	- Warning Signs: fatigue, aching, numbness, or pain, limiting walking; erectile dysfunction; poorly healing wounds on legs/feet; rest pain in lower leg/foot (changes when standing/supine); abdominal pain after meals (food fear/weight loss); first-degree relative with AAA.
	- Buttock/hip (aortoiliac), thigh (femoral/aortoiliac), upper calf (superficial femoral), lower calf (popliteal), foot (tibeal/peroneal)
	- Hair loss over anterior [[tibia]] because of ↓ arterial perfusion
	- Acute back pain (thrombosis)
	- Pain by eating (mesenteric ischemia)
- [[PVD]] (Peripheral vascular disease)
	- DVT
	- Pain or swelling in calf/leg. Upper extremity DVT: arm discomfort, pain, paresthesias, weakness.
	- Well’s clinical score OR geneva score
#### Physical Exam
- JVP
	- 30 degree table, have patient lie down and perform on right side
	- Turn head away from area and try to find internal jugular vein
	- Measure to top, add 5 cm
	- Elevated if >3cm above sternal angle

*Jugular Venous Pulse is X cm above sternal angle with head of exam table at 30 degrees without jugular venous distention*

![[Screenshot 2025-09-21 at 12.35.49 PM.png]]

- Carotid Arteries
	- Should only feel 1, symmetric brisk pulsation
		- ONLY one side at a time
		- If buzzing sensation, thrills – turbulent flow
	- Auscultate (patient STOP breathing)
		- Bruits indicate atherosclerotic luminal stenosis

- Other arteries
	- Brachial
	- Radial
		- In Raynaud, wrist pulses normal but episodes of demarcated pallor of fingers
	- Ulnar
	- Femoral 
		- Have patient put foot on table and then put knee out to side
		- Then have patient pull drape and gown up to inguinal ligament area
		- Go 3-4 widths down from ASIS and press deep in
		- If absent, LR > 6 for PAD
		- Exaggerated = femoral aneurysm
	- Popliteal
		- Foot on table still
		- Both hands in crease
		- Exaggerated = popliteal aneurysm
	- Posterior Tibial
		- Compare temperature – Asymmetric LR PAD > 6
		- Poikilothermia seen in PVD
	- Dorsalis Pedis
		- Absent but present popliteal = LR PAD > 14

*__ pulses with brisk and symmetric upstroke without thrills*

- Allen’s test of collateral circulation
	- Block radial + ulnar, let each go individually
- Edema test
	- Push down on anterior tibia + pedis for a few seconds and see how long it takes to come up
	- 1+ = < 15 sec
	- 2+ 30 sec
	- 3+ 60 sec
	- 4+ 2-3 min
	- Unilateral = DVT, lymphedema
	- Bilateral = heart failure, cirrhosis, nephrotic syndrome

- Palpate 4 valves (still hand) for thrills
	- Palpable S2 = pulmonary hypertension

*No obvious pretibial or pedal edema. No palpable thrills*

PMI Palpation
- Find PMI with flat hand, move pads to apex
- Dextrocardia if on R side of body
- PMI > 2.5 cm – LV [[hypertrophy]]
- In COPD, PMI may be in xiphoid area

Auscultation of Precordium
- Auscultate each of 4 valves

>[!Murmurs]
>Time (Systolic between S1 and S2) or Diastolic
>- Palpate carotid to help
>- Diastolic murmur – valvular heart disease
>- Aortic murmur radiates to neck, mitral often to [[axilla]]
>Shape
>- Crescendo, decrescendo, crescendo-decrescendo, plateau

*No carotid bruits. PMI non-displaced. On ausculation, heart has RRR without murmurs, gallops, or rubs (MGR)*
