## Head
#### Anatomy and Physiology Pearls
- Superficial Temporal Artery: Upwards just in front of ear, palpable here
#### Physical Exam
- Note hair quality, distribution, texture, loss
	- **Fine hair found in [[hyperthyroidism]], course in [[hypothyroidism]]**
- Part hair 3 places and look for scaliness ([[seborrheic dermatitis]], [[psoriasis]]), lumps ([[pilar cysts]]), nevi ([[melanoma]])
- Palpate skull front → back
	- Note size, contour, deformities, depressions, lumps, tenderness
	- **Enlarged skull could indicate [[hydrocephalus]] or [[Paget disease of bone]]**
- Note face structure, asymmetry, involuntary movements, edema, masses
- Skin (assess color, texture, hair distribution, lesions)

*Normocephalic, atraumatic with no tenderness or changes with scalp, skull, or hair*

## [[Eye|Eyes]]

#### Health History Pearls
- Vision Changes
	- Refractive errors: close work → [[hyperopia]] or [[presbyopia]] and distance → [[myopia]]
	- Blurred vision:
		- Unilateral, sudden, painless: vitreous hemorrhage, macular degeneration, retinal detachment, retinal vein occlusion, central retinal artery occlusion
		- Unilateral, sudden, painful: Cause in cornea + anterior chamber – **Immediate referral**
		- Gradual bilateral: cataracts, glaucoma, macular degeneration
	- Location of visual loss:
		- Central: nuclear cataract and macular degeneration
		- Peripehral: advanced open-angle glaucoma
	- Specks / Scotomas:
		- Moving: vitreous floaters
		- Fixed defects: lesion in retina, visual pathway, brain
		- Flashing lights with new floaters: traction on retina with detachment of vitreous body from retina
- Eye Pain, redness, tearing
	- Red painless: subconjuctival hemorrhage and episcleritis
	- Red eye with gritty sensation: viral conjuctivitis and dry eye
	- Red painful: corneal abrasion, foreign body, corneal ulcer, acute angle closure glaucoma, herpes keratitis, fungal keratitis, hyphema, uveitis
- Diplopia
	- Side by side images or vertical?
	- Persist with 1 eye closed? Which eye affected?
	- Cause coudl be lesion in brainstem or cerebellu, or weakness/paralysis in extraocular muscles
	- **One eye suggests problem with ocular surface, cornea, lens, macula

#### Physical Exam
- Visual Acuity
	- Distant with Snellen Chart 20 ft away
	- Near Visual with bent arm chart. Cover 1 eye at a time with glasses on. See smallest line where half plus correct (left to right for eye 1 and right to left for eye 2)
	- Numerator = equivalent distance, denominator = normal eye
- Peripheral Vision: 
	- Standing, eye level, come close
	- If they close left, close right and bring fists in upper left and lower right in peripheral vision. Tell them to look at me and hold up different numbers
	- Do this for 4 quadrants for both eyes
	- **If left eye repeatedly can’t see fingers, left homonymous hemianopsia**
	
	*Near Visual Acuity = 20/20 both eyes, uncorrected. Visual fields are full and intact to confrontation*

- Can do color vision to rule out damage to optic nerve (red-green deficit / red desaturation)
- Pupillary Light Response (no glasses)
	- Dim light, tell them to look at nose, 2x on each eye
	- Direct response (pupil should constrict with light)
	- Indirect response (opposite should constrict)
- Near Response
	- Normal lighting, hold finger 10 cm from patient
	- Focus on distance then finger → pupils should constrict (2x 1 for each eye)
- Extraocular Muscles
	- Finger 36cm from eyes. Head still and follow with eyes
	- H shape and then converge at nose 
	- Check for symmetry
	- Lid lag: eyes from up to down show rim of sclera above eris (seen in hyperthyroidism)

*Pupils are equal, round, and reactive to light & accommodation (PERRLA) both directly and consensually. Extraocular muscles intact (EOMI)*

- Outer structures of eyes
	- Eye position (protrusion, abnormalities deviation)
	- Eyebrows (fullness, hair, scaliness)
	- Eyelids
		- Red inflamed lid margins → blepharitis with crusting
		- Failure of eyelids to close (lagophthalmos) → corneas exposed DANGEROUS get opthalmology
	- Lactrimal Apparatus
		- Excessive tearing (increased production or impaired drainage) or dryness could be secretion problem (Sjogren syndrome)
	- Conjuctiva + Sclera
		- Depress lower lid and look at color, nodules (Look L, R, U)
		- Yellow suggests **jaundice**
		- Turn upper lid upside down to inspect conjunctiva
	- Cornea + Lens
		- Dim lights, turn light to 80% brightness
		- Corneal Light Reflection
			- Stand 2 feet in front
			- Shine light and ask to look in it
			- Reflection should be symmetrical
		- Slit Lamp from side across cornea (x2)
			- Look for opacities, abrasions, anterior chamber, iris, pupil

*Palpebral conjunctiva without pallor. Bulbar conjunctiva without injection, erythema, or exudate. No scleral icterus. Corneas clear with no epithelial defects. Anterior chamber is clear without hypopyon or hyphema*

- Ophthalmoscope (adjust focus, remove glasses), darken room
	- Hold in right hand for right eye, left hand for left eye 
	- Light to medium round beam, 80% brightness
	- Tell patient to look at point 3/4 way up wall, other hand on patient’s shoulder
	- Identify red reflex → **absence suggests opacity of lens (cataract) or vitreous, or detached retina, mass, retinoblastoma**
	- Hold in view and move inwards FINGER OUT. Follow blood vessel in to find optic disc
	- Enlarged cup: **chronic open-angle glaucoma**
	- Papilledema (disc is pink, vessels visible, cup not visible)

*Sharp disc margins evidence of papilledema. Retina without obvious A-V nicking, hemorrhages, exudates*
#### Health Promotion Pearls
- Vision Impairment: Comprehensive screening of all adults regularly
- [[Glaucoma]] Screening: Retinal exam shows pallor + increasing size of optic cup 
	- Risk = > 65, African American descent, diabetes, myopia, ocular hypertension
- UV-Related injuries
	- sunscreen on face/eyelids + sunglasses

## [[Ear|Ears]]
#### Health History Pearls
- Ear Related: Hearing loss (uni/bilateral? sudden/gradual onset? symptoms associated)
	- Sudden onset sensorineural hearing loss means ENT referral
	- Distinguish conductive vs sensorineural loss
	- Impact of noise (sensorineural have difficulty hearing in noisy but conductive find noisy helpful)
	- Medications (aminoglycosides, chemotherapeutic agents for permanent, NSAIDs for temporary) and exposure to loud noise
- Earache + Discharge
	- Symptoms: fever, sore throat, cough, URI – ear infection?
	- Location** external suggests otitis externa but deeper suggests otitis media
	- Discharge character: yellow/green = acute otitis externa or media with perforation
- Ringing in ears:
	- If [[tinnitus]] + fluctuating hearing loss and vertigo, [[Meniere disease]]
- Dizziness / [[Vertigo]]:
	- Ask them to describe how they feel WITHOUT saying dizzy
	- Vertigo: room is spinning, rotary → problem in inner ear, peripheral CN VIII lesion, or central pathways
	- [[Peripheral Vertigo]]: Benign positional vertigo, labyrinthitis, vestibular neuritis, Meniere disease
	- [[Central Vertigo]]: Ataxia, diplopia, dysarthria signal central neurological causes
	- Presyncope: passing out sense
	- Dysequilibrium: unsteadiness

#### Physical Exam
- Finger rub 6 inches behind → ask to close eyes and tell which ear
- Older patients have high frequency hearing loss
- Weber Tuning Fork (512 Hz)
	- Place on head or mid-forehead, where hear sound best
	- Normal: equal in both/midline
	- Unilateral conductive hearing loss: lateralized to impaired ear
	- Unilateral sensorineural hearing loss: lateralized to good ear
- Rinne Tuning Fork
	- Traditional: vibrate place on mastoid, ask when they don’t hear and bring to ear 
	- Ask which was louder → **air should be more than bone**
	- Quick: Place on both and ask whats more louder
	- If bone >, conductive hearing loss

*Hearing intact bilaterally to finger rub at 6 inches. AC > BC. Hearing symmetrical without lateralization*

- Look at skin for deformities, masses, lesions
- Press on tragus, mastoid, tug test
	- **Movement of auricle + tragus painful in acute otitis externa but NOT in otitis media**
	- **Tenderness behind ear in otitis media + mastoiditis**. 
- Otoscope exam:
	- Go just a bit past the ear hairs
	- Pull auricle up and back and a bit away
	- Manipulate device NOT ear and use fingers
	- Seeing tymphanic membrane, ask to blow out and should see movement at cone of light
	- Acute otitis externa (canal swollen, narrow, moist, or tender)

*No lesions, deformities, or tenderness of helix, antihelix, tragus, or lobule of ear. External auditory canal is patent with no edema or erythema. Tympanic membrane translucent, pearly gray with bony landmarks visible. Light reflex anterior-inferior and TM mobile to Valsalva*

#### Health Promotion Pearls
- [[Hearing loss]] screening: 
- Top cause = age related (high frequency loss)
- Next = Hazardous noise levels

## [[Nose]]

#### Health History Pearls
- Nosebleed:
	- Source and distinguish from coughing up/vomiting blood
	- Trauma, inflammation, drying/crusting of nasal mucosa, tumor, foreign body
	- Recurrent problem? Ask about recurrent bleeding/bruising elsewhere, meds (anticoagulants, NSAIDs), vascular malformations, coagulopathies
- Nose discharge
	- Sneezing, watery eyes, throat discomfort, itching
	- Itching → allergic cause
	- Drug induced: topical decongestants / intranasal cocaine
	- Acute bacterial sinusitis: Not likely unless URI past 7 days 
	- Unilateral: deviated septum, nasal polyp, foreign body, granulomatous disease, carcinoma

#### Physical Exam
- Surface: check for deformities, symmetry
	- Tenderness of tip suggests local infection
- Ask patient to close 1 side and breathe in and out
- Palpate frontal and maxillary sinuses
	- Tenderness with face pressure, nasal discharge, and smell >7 days = **[[acute bacterial rhinosinusitis]]**
- Otoscope:
	- Place on OUTSIDE tip AWAY, tip head back
	- Look at color: 
		- Viral [[rhinitis]]: red and swollen mucosa
		- Allergic [[rhinitis]]: pale, bluish, red mucosa
	- Septum: Check for any ulcers or polyps
*Mucus is clear, not purulent. Mucosa pink and healthy with no lesions. Turbinates are not erythematous or boggy. No septal perforation, deviation, or inflammation. Maxillary and frontal sinuses are non-tender to palpation*
## Throat

#### Health History Pearls
- Sore throat
	- Associated with acute URI
	- Sore tongue: local lesions, systemic illness
	- **If strep, rapid antigen testing or throat culture**
- Bleeding / Swollen gums
	- [[Gingivitis]] but ask about local lesions and general bleeding/bruising
- Hoarseness:
	- Ask about allergies (environmental), acid reflux, smoking, alcohol, irritant inhalation, voice overuse
	- **If > 2 weeks, get laryngoscopy** → reflux, vocal cord nodules, hypothyroid, head + neck cnacer, neurological disorders
- Bad Breath
	- Poor oral hygiene, [[tobacco]], plaque retention
	- Respiratory common causes

#### Physical Exam
- Oral Cavity with Light and Tongue Depressor
- Lips and oral mucosa
	- Color, moisture, ulcers, cracking
	- Check for pallor or central cyanosis from anemia
	- Oral mucosa: discolorations, ulcers, white patches, nodules
	- If any, palpate with and note thickening or infiltration → malignancy
	- Denture stomatitis
	- Thrush: cream or bluish white patches on tongue
	- Koplik spots (early measles sign) – small white specks like salt
	- Petechiae (trauma) small red spots 
	- [[Leukoplakia]]: thick white patch from chewing tobacco → oral cancer or HPV
	- Kaposi sarcoma in [[AIDS]]: deep purple lesions
- Gums + Teeth
	- Red gums suggests **gingivitis**, black line might be **lead poisoning**
- Roof + Floor of Mouth: 
	- **Floor of mouth common place for carcinoma that should be biopsied**
- Tongue
	- Ask patient to stick out and move it side to side (check for symmetry) **asymmetric could be lesion of CN XII**
	- Sides and undersurface of tongue have cancer often
		- Men > 50, smokers, chewing tobacco, alcohol
		- Persistent nodule (red or white) is suspect and should be biopsied
- [[Pharynx]]
	- Ask to say ‘ah’ (soft palette should rise)
		- If CN X paralysis, it fails to rise 
		- Press firmly on midpoint of arched tongue
		- **asymmetric tonsils with symptoms could be pathology (lymphoma?)**
#### Health Promotion Pearls
- Oral Health
	- Daily hygiene 
	- Dental care annually
	- Avoid tobacco limit alcohol
	- Dry mouth: drink more water and chew sugarless gum
- [[Oral Cancer]]
	- Risks: Tobacco + alcohol, HPV 
	- Referral of any suspicious oral mucosal lesions for biopsy

*Breath is not malodorous. Good dentition. No erythema, exudate, ulcerations, lesions, or growths of lips, gums, tongue, buccal mucosa, pharynx, tonsils, or hard and soft palates. Uvula is midline without deviation, palate elevates symmetrically*
## Neck
#### Health History Pearls
- Neck Mass/Lump
	- Ask onset, changes, pain, associated symptoms (discharge, dysphagia, dyspnea)
	- **Persistant neck mass in adult > 40 → suspected malignancy**
	- [[Pharyngitis]] accompanied by tender, enlarged lymph nodes

- Thyroid Mass, Nodule, Goiter
	- Ask about function: Temperature intolerance, sweating, changes to skin tecture, palpitations, weight change

![[Screenshot 2025-08-16 at 11.46.41 PM.png]]

#### Physical Exam
- Move pads of index + middle fingers in circles to assess lymph nodes of neck
	- Note change in size, shape, delimitation (should be able to roll in 2 directions), mobility, consistency, any tenderness
	- **Small, mobile, discrete, nontneder nodes are common**
	- **Tender nodes = inflammation**
	- **IF ENLARGED, non-tender, hard, or fixed → could be malignant**
	- Supraclavicular enlargement, especially left = metastasis from thoracic or abdominal malignancy
- [[Trachea]]: inspect for deviation from midline, place finger on 1 side and note space between it and SCM → symmetry?
	- Masses can cause deviation to 1 side (could be thorax condition)
	- Auscultation: breaths over trachea stridor → severe subglottic or tracheal obstruction **EMERGENCY**
- Thyroid: tip head back, can use tangential lighting to look at area below cricoid cartilage
- Tell Patient to swallow (check that all parts rise then fall)
	- Goiter = enlargement of thyroid to be double size
- Palpate thyroid: flex neck forward and locate thyroid isthmus, tell patient to swallow 
	- Palpate left and right by having patient turn to that side
	- Check size, shape, consistency, nodules / tenderness
	- Soft in [[Grave’s Disease]] and can be nodular
	- Firm in [[Hashimoto thyroditis]] and malignancy
- If enlarged, listen to see bruit
	- Localized bruit in hyperthyroidism from Graves disease or toxic multinodular goiter
*No significant anterior or posterior cervical, supraclavicular, or infraclavicular lymphadenopathy. Trachea is midline without deviation. Thyroid is not enlarged. No palpable thyroid nodules*
#### Health Promotion Pearls
- [[Thyroid cancer]] incidence has tripled but mortality stable
	- Nodules > 2cm, firm, fixed are concerning – Ultrasound
	- USPSTF: Recommend against screening of thyroid cancer
