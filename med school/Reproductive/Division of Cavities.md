#### Septum Transversum
![[Attachments/Excalidraw/Drawing 2025-09-03 16.53.01.excalidraw.md]]
- Comes from [[mesoderm]]
- Forms ventral [[Mesentery Formation|mesentery]] and parts of diaphragm
- Initially cranial to intraembryonic coelom, but folds inferiorly near heart when [[embryo folding]] happens
	- Pinches off of intraembryonic coelom
	- Pericardial cavity cranially
	- [[Peritoneal cavity]] caudally
	- Pinched area = pericardioperitoneal canals (in circle) , each is lateral to foregut and dorsal to septum transversum

#### Separation of Pericardial and Pleural Cavities
Week 5
- Lung buds are dorsal, off of [[foregut]] (of GI tube)
- Heart is ventral

![[Pasted Image 20250903212509_542.png]]
- Pleuropericardial folds from lateral part of embryo near lung buds (pericardioperitoneal canal)
	- Currently, one fold is superior to lung bud – separates pericardioperitoneal canal (which is pleural cavity) and pericardial cavity
	- Other (pleuroperitoneal fold) is inferior separating pleural and peritoneal cavity
	- Contain common cardinal v. and [[Phrenic Nerve|phrenic n.]] 
	- As lung grows, fold is thinner and pushed forward (enclose heart – fibrous pericardium)

![[Attachments/Excalidraw/Drawing 2025-09-03 21.22.27.excalidraw.md]]

Week 7 
- Pleuropericardial folds fuse with mesentery ventral to esophagus form primordial mediastinum
	- This separates pericardial and pleural cavities
- Outer layer → thoracic wall 
- Inner layer → fibrous pericardium
