##### Blood Supply
- Branches of internal pudendal a. ← [[Internal Iliac Artery|internal iliac a.]] ← common iliac a.
	- Dorsal arteries (paired)
	- Deep arteries run through center of corpus cavernosa

Venous
- Cavernous → deep dorsal vein → prostatic venous plexus
- Superficial dorsal v. → superficial external pudendal v.

Arteriovenous shunts open between deep arteries and superficial veins, branches of deep are closed ← flaccid
Arteriovenous shunts close and deep arteries push blood into lacunae of erectile tissue ← erect
##### Innervation
- Parasympathetic (S2-S4) – pelvic splanchnic ([[erection]]) – relax smooth muscle in trabercula, ↑ blood flow to cavernous spaces
- Sympathetic (T11-L2) – thoracolumbar – pelvic plexus through hypogastric n. ([[ejaculation]]) – ↓ blood flow into cavernous spaces so back to flaccid
##### Lymphatic Drainage
- Skin – superficial inguinal node
- Glans – deep inguinal + external iliac nodes
- Spongy urethra – internal iliac nodes
##### Embryology
- Forms from genital tubercle + urethral groove
- Urethral groove comes forward and fuses midline
- Endodermal lining of groove becomes [[urethra]]

>[!Clinical]
>[[Hypospadias]] – incomplete fusion of urethral groove causes abnormal openings at ventral part of penis
>[[Epispadias]] – Urethral meatus found on dorsal part of penis
>- Associated with exstrophy of bladder
