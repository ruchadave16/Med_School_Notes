Lesser sac – space between pancreas and stomach and spleen
- Posterior stomach grows more and liver moves to right so stomach + mesogastrium turns
- Dorsal moves left → more curvature of stomach
- 6-8 weeks – spleen and pancreas grow
- Stomach pushes liver to right and spleen back to left
- Tail of pancreas pushed retroperitoneal
- Remnants of dorsal mesogastrium
	- Gastrosplenic lig. (spleen → stomach)
	- Splenorenal lig. (spleen → posterior wall peritoneum) (lienorenal lig.)

Omental Foramen (epiploic foramen, foramen of Winslow)
- Opening between lesser sac and peritoneal cavity – posterior to lesser omentum

#### Greater Omentum
- [[Lipoprotein]] formation + keeps GI contents at higher temperature for physiological processes
- Formed from dorsal mesogastrium
- When stomach grows and rotates, mesogastrium pulled with it
- Lesser sac continues between layers of greater omentum in fetus (disappears in adult)

#### Mesoduodenum
- Posterior wall to duodenum
- Rotation + growth of stomach makes duodenum and pancreas get pushed retroperitoneally
- Both layers disappear completely
