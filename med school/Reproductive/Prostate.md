##### Boundaries
- Surrounds prostatic [[urethra]] inferior to [[urinary bladder]] and [[levator ani]]
- Separated from rectum by rectoprostatic fascia
##### Contents
- Prostatic ducts – open into prostatic sinuses with prostatic fluid (citric acid – nutrients and acid phosphatase)
##### External Features

##### Internal Features
4 lobes
1) Anterior (in front of urethra)
2) Median (between urethra and ejaculatory duct)
3) Posterior (behind urethra)
4) Lateral (to sides of urethra)

Has tubuloalveolar glands which empty into urethra

>[!Clinical]
>[[Benign Prostatic Hyperplasia]] – Median lobe enlargement, which compresses urethra and blocks urination
>- Common in transition zone (histological)
>- Common in men > 50 years

**Histology**
- Tall cuboid → pseudostratified columnar epithelium
- Prostatic concretions in lumen – [[Glycoproteins|glycoprotein]] spheres

**Histological Zones**
- Peripheral zone
	- Location of glands, common site of inflammation + cancer
	- [[Prostate Specific Antigen]] (PSA) to measure [[Glycoproteins|glycoprotein]] for tumor
- Central zone
	- Submucosal glands
- Transition zone
	- Mucous glands, around urethra

##### Innervation
- Sympathetic:
	- Inferior hypogastric n.
- Parasympathetic
	- Pelvic splanchnic (S2-S4)
##### Lymphatic Drainage
- Internal iliac LN
- Secondarily to sacral LN
>[!Clinical]
