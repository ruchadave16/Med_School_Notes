*Formation of 3 germ layers from epiblast*

>[!Clinical] Critical step for establishing orientation

**Primitive Streak** = longitudinal midline groove in [[epiblast]] on caudal end
- Formed by epiblast cells migrating medially
- Caudal end cells called primitive node with opening: primitive pit
- Defines axes

![[Drawing 2025-08-06 17.38.05.excalidraw]]

>[!Clinical] 
>[[Teratoma]]:  Most common neonatal tumor
>- Overgrowth of undifferentiated epiblast cells staying at primitive streak 
>- Cells of primitive streak are undifferentiated, so anything can be in tumor
>- More common in females
>[[Situs Inversus]]: Ciliary dyskinesia means organs are mirror-image of normal
>- Can happen in 1 or all organs


[[Endoderm]] Formation:
- [[Epiblast]] cells move downwards through primitive streak towards [[hypoblast]] and cranially
- Invade and displace hypoblast
- On ventral surface

![[Screenshot 2025-08-06 at 5.44.39 PM.png]]

[[Mesoderm]] Formation:
- Migrating [[epiblast]] cells fill space between epiblast and [[embryonic endoderm]]

![[Screenshot 2025-08-06 at 5.45.21 PM.png]]

[[Ectoderm]] Formation: 
- Cells remaining in epiblast are ectoderm
- On dorsal surface

![[Screenshot 2025-08-06 at 2.10.27 PM.png]]

**Oropharyngeal Membrane** 
- *Cranial* area where endoderm and ectoderm attach with no mesoderm → future mouth

**Cloacal Membrane**
- *Caudal* area where endoderm and ectoderm attach with no mesoderm → future anus and urogenital
