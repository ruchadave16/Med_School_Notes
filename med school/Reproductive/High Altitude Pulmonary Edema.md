### Etiology
- Rapid ascent > 4500m
- **Most common cause of death in people who ascend rapidly**

### Pathophysiology
- ↓ PP of O2 → vasoconstriction
- Hypoxic vasoconstriction → ↑ pulmonary arterial and capillary pressure → pulmonary hypertension
- Inflammatory response → increase in extravascular fluid → pulmonary edema

### Clinical Features
- Onset 2-4 days post arrival
- Cough – nonproductive but can have pink, frothy sputum
- **SOB**
- Weakness
- Chest tightness
- **Crackles, wheezing**
- Cyanosis
- Tachypnea / tachycardia
- **Headache**
- **Elevated pulse 100-120 bpm**

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
	- Chest xray – patchy infiltrates
	- ECG – elevated pulm pressure, normal LV function
- Criteria

### Treatment
- DESCEND immediately
- Oxygen therapy

- Short-term
- Long-term
