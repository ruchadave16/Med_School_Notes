#### External Features
[[Broad Ligament]]
- Double layer of peritoneum folding over uterus
- Encloses uterine tube, vessels, round ligament, ovarian ligament
	- Mesometrium – over uterus
	- Mesosalpinx – suspends uterine tube
	- Mesovarium – anterior ovary to posterior broad ligament
	
![[Pasted image 20250920013543.png]]

[[Round Ligament]]
- To anterolateral uterus, remnant of lower gubernaculum
- Goes through [[Inguinal Canal]] and exits through [[Superficial Inguinal Ring|superficial inguinal ring]]

[[Ovarian Ligament]]
- Ovary → uterus below uterine tube within broad ligament
- Remnant of upper gubernaculum

[[Suspensory Ligament of Ovary]]
- Ovary to lateral pelvic walls
- Contains ovarian vessels, nerves, lympahtics

[[Cardinal Ligament]]
- Lateral cervix and vagina to lateral walls
- Contains **uterine artery and vein**
- Ureter is posterior “Water under the bridge”

>[!Clinical]
>[[Hysterectomy]] – need to be careful not to damage ureter

Uterosacral Ligaments
- Posterior uterus → middle of sacrum

Vesicouterine pouch – between bladder and uterus
Rectouterine pouch – between rectum and uterus

>[!Clinical] [[Culdocentesis]] is when needle passed through posterior vaginal wall to get peritoneal fluid
#### Layers
1) [[Endometrium]]
	- Simple columnar [[Epithelial Tissue|epithelium]] with ciliated and secretory cells + stroma
	- 2 layers:
		- Stratum functionalis – spinal arteries, shed off during [[menstruation]], lighter stained
		- Stratum basalis – radial + straight arteries, stable during menstruation, darker stained

2) Myometrium
	- [[Smooth muscle]], blood from arcuate arteries

3) Perimetrium 
	- Connective tissue layer outside

#### Blood Supply
- Uterine aa. from [[Internal Iliac Artery|internal iliac a.]] (in [[cardinal ligament]]) – superficial
- Internal pudendal a (inferior)
- Ovarian aa. from aorta (in [[Suspensory Ligament of Ovary]])

- Vaginal venous plexus → internal iliac v
#### Innervation
- Dual sympathetic + parasympathetic for contractions and blood flow
#### Lymphatic Drainage
- Cervis → obturator LN → external iliac LN
- Uterus → superficial or external iliac LN
