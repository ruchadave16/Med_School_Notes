1) Foramen cecum forms – small invagination between 1st and 2nd [[pharyngeal arches]]
2) Thyroid diverticulum forms – [[endoderm]] outgrowth from 2nd pharyngeal arch
	- Forms follicular cells

![[Drawing 2025-10-31 22.45.35.excalidraw]]
3) Diverticulum migrates caudally in front of hyoid
	- Connects to pharynx through thyroglossal duct

![[Drawing 2025-10-31 22.49.21.excalidraw]]

4) Tip bifurcates into 2 lobes and isthmus
	- Eventually thyroglossal duct goes away
	- Opening of duct = foramen cecum of tongue

>[!Clinical]
>- Persistent thyroglossal duct: ectopic tissue doesn’t degenerate
>- Pyramidal lobe: remnant of duct but functional tissue
>	- Airway access issue

5) Parafollicular cells from 4th pharyngeal pouch tissue

>[!Clinical]
>[[Thyroglossal Cyst]] – dilation along path of thyroglossal duct
>- Failure of degeneration = cause
>- See swelling on midline, superficial structure but moves when swallowing
>- Treatment = surgical removal
>
