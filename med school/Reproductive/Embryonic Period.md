##### Embryoblast → Bilaminar Embryonic Disc (Week 2)
- [[Embryo]] starts to orient dorsal and ventral
- Embryoblast (Inner cell mass of [[blastocyst]]) cells make 2 layers
	- **Epiblast** – Dorsal
		- Cells form embryo with cavity that becomes amniotic cavity
	- **Hypoblast** – Ventral, contact with blastocyst cavity
		- Cells to line blastocyst cavity = Exocoelomic (Heuser’s) membrane
		- Blastocyte cavity becomes primitive yolk sac → important for [[hematopoiesis]], degrades by birth

##### Bilaminar Embryonic Disc → Trilaminar Embryo (Week 3)
- 3 germ layers [[ectoderm]], [[mesoderm]], [[endoderm]]
- [[Gastrulation]]: Formation of 3 layers (trilaminar germ disc) from [[epiblast]]
##### [[Notochord]] Formation (Day 17-30)
- Epiblast cells from primitive node invaginate towards hypoblast and migrate cranially
- Form chord of cells running down midline
- Cells detach from [[endoderm]] and become [[notochord]] → in [[Mesoderm]]
- In adults, this is found in center of IV disc
- Drives development of neural

##### [[Endoderm]] Differentiation (Week 3-4)
- Ventral layer, forms most innermost layers
- Epithelial lining of organs, portions of [[liver]], [[pancreas]], [[Thyroid Gland|thyroid]], [[Parathyroid Gland|parathyroid]]
##### Organogenesis (Week 4-8)
- Week 4 = [[Embryo Folding]]
- Yolk sac degenerates by week 7
- [[Pharyngeal Arches]]
- [[Lung Buds Formation]] 
- [[Division of Cavities]] – [[Intraembryonic Coelom|intraembryonic coelom]] is one continuous space so separates into pericardial, pleural, and peritoneal cavities through 2 partitions: diaphragm and pleuropericardial membrane
- [[Diaphragm Formation]]
- [[Mesentery Formation]]
- Week 5 onwards kidneys – [[Urogenital Development]]

>[!Clinical]
>[[Agenesis]] = absent organ because absent primordial tissue
>[[Aplasia]] = absent organ despite present primordial tissue
>[[Hypoplasia]]: Incomplete organ development
