**Hindfoot** – Talus + [[calcaneus]]
**Midfoot** – Navicular, cuboid, cuneiforms
**Forefoot** – 5 metatarsals, 14 phalanges

>[!Clinical]
>5th metatarsal most commonly fractured
>
>[[Jones fracture]] – proximal 5th MT, sometimes 4th
>
>[[Avulsion fracture]] of 5th MT because of pull from fibularis brevis

#### Archs
- Medial longitudinal arch
- Lateral longitudinal arch
- Transverse arch
- Support by plantar aponeurosis → [[plantar fasciitis]] → due to overstretching

![[Screenshot 2025-10-05 at 3.14.47 PM.png]]
#### Articulations
- Transverse tarsal joint
	- Calcaneus + cuboid
	- Talus + navicular
	- Connects hindfoot to midfoot
- Tarsometarsal joint
	- Medial cuneiform + 1st MT
	- Intermediate cuneiform + 2nd MT
	- Lateral cuneiform + 3rd MT
	- Cuboid + 4th and 5th MT
	- Connects midfoot to forefoot
- Metatarsophalangeal joint

>[!Clinical]
>[[Lisfranc Injury]] – disruption of ligamentous structure + fracture 
>-  Dislocation of tarsometatarsal at medial cuneiform and 2nd MT

#### Muscles
- 4 layers of muscles on plantar surface
	- Deepest: great toe muscle with **sesamoid** bones embedded in tendon

>[!Clinical]
>Sesamoid bones can dislocate
>- [[Hallux valgus]] – lateral deviation of great toe

- Dorsum has anterior tendons + 2 muscles
#### Ligaments
- Short plantar ligament (Plantar calcaneocuboid ligament)
	- Calcaneus → cuboid
	- Medial to long plantar ligament
- Long plantar ligament
	- Calcaneus → cuboid + metatarsal base
	- Supports lateral longitudinal arch
- Plantar calcaneonavicular ligament (spring ligament)
	- Sustentaculum tali → navicular tuberosity
	- Supports head of tali + medial longitudinal arch

>[!Clinical]
>[[Pes Planus]] – flat feet
>- Flexible (lose arch during weight bearing)
>- Rigid (always lose arch)
#### Innervation
- Medial and lateral plantar branches of tibial n. → intrinsic muscles
- Sural n. → skin of posterolateral [[leg]] + lateral [[foot]]
- Tibial n
	- Medial calcaneal branches → skin over heel
	- Medial plantar n → plantar surface of medial 3.5 and sole area
	- Lateral plantar n → plantar surface of lateral 1.5 and sole area

#### Vasculature
- Posterior tibial a.
	- Into medial and lateral plantar aa. 
	- Medial plantar a. is smaller and then plantar digital a. 
	- Lateral plantar a. → deep plantar arch (anastomosis with deep plantar a.)
- Anterior tibial a. 
	- Ends as dorsalis pedis a. → lateral tarsal a. and arcuate a. 
	- Dorsalis pedis ends bifurcating into deep plantar a.
