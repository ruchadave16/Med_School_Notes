#### External Oblique Muscle
- Most superficial
- Inferior margin = [[Inguinal ligament]]
- Fibers follow external intercostal (hands in pocket)
- Action: Compress abdominal viscera
	- 1 side *contralaterally* rotates vertebral column
	- Both sides flex vertebral column
- Innervation: Anterior (Ventral) rami of thoraco-abdominal (T7-T11) and subcostal (T12) n. 

#### Internal Oblique Muscle
- Intermediate layer
- Aponeurosis splits to encase [[rectus abdominis m.]]
- Lowest fibers merge onto spermatic cord and form [[cremaster m]]. 
- Direction (opposite of external oblique, same as internal intercostal)
- Action: Compresses abdominal viscera
	- One side *ipsilaterally* rotates vertebral column
	- Both sides flex vertebral column
- Innervation: Anterior (ventral) rami of thoraco-abdominal (T6-T12) and L1 nn.

#### Transversus Abdominis Muscle
- Innermost layer
- Direction = horizontal 
- Action: Compresses abdominal viscera
- Innervation: Anterior (ventral) rami of thoraco-abdominal (T6-T12) and L1 nn.
