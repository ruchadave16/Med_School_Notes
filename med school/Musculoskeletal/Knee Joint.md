*Bi-condyloid [[Synovial Joints|Synovial joint]]*

#### Articulations
- Femorotibial – 2 round femoral condyles contact with 2 flat tibial condyles
- Medial is larger and extends more distally
- Patellofemoral joint – [[patella]] articulates w femoral condyles at different spots

**Epicondyles** allow attachment of collateral ligaments

#### Joint Capsule
- Thin, weak, incomplete
- Attaches superiorly to femoral condyle, inferiorly to tibial condyles

#### Blood Supply
- Genicular aa. ← popliteal a. (anastomosis around this joint)

#### Innervation
- Branches of sciatic, femoral, and obturator nn. 
- Bursa
	- Suprapatellar bursa – synovial capsule continues between quadriceps tendon and femur

>[!Clinical]
> - [[Prepatellar bursitis]] – Inflammation of prepatellar bursa due to repeated trauma from kneeling
>- [[Popliteal Cyst]] – Fluid collection in gastrocnemius-semimembranosus bursa

#### Meniscus
- [[Fibrocartilage]] triangular pads on tibial plateau increase surface and help with knee movement
- Medial meniscus – more commonly torn *Green*
- Lateral meniscus – freely movable *Blue*

![[Screenshot 2025-10-04 at 11.55.09 PM.png]]

>[!Clinical] [[Meniscus Tears]]
>Due to abnormal twisting of knee
>”Knee gave away”
>- Test with [[McMurray test]]
>- – pain and popping with internal rotation = lateral 
>- – pain and popping with external rotation = medial 

#### Ligaments
- Extracapsular Ligaments
	- Medial (tibial) collateral ligament (MCL)
		- Broad and flat
		- Medial femoral epicondyle → proximal tibia
		- Attaches to medial meniscus 
		- Resists valgus stress (abduction of tibia)
	- Lateral (fibular) collateral ligament (LCL)
		- Thin, cordlike
		- Lateral femoral epicondyle → head of fibula
		- Resists varus stress (adduction)

- Intracapsular Ligaments
	- Anterior cruciate ligament (ACL)
		- Anterior tibial plateau → lateral femoral condyle
		- Injured with hyperextension or fixed leg with rotation
		- Test with anterior draw test or Lachman test
	- Posterior cruciate ligament (PCL)
		- Posterior tibial plateau → medial femoral condyle 
		- Injured with flexion
		- Test with posterior draw test

>[!Clinical]
>- Unhappy triad: Injury to MCL, medial meniscus, ACL → blow to lateral knee
>- Genu valgum: “knock knee”, tibia deviated laterally
>- Genu varum: “bow legged”, tibia deviated medially
