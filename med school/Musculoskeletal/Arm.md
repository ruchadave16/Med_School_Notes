#### Muscles
**Anterior** (Flexor)
- Innervation: musculocutaneous n. (C5-7)
- Biceps brachii
	- Crosses elbow and shoulder joints
	- Long head = lateral, runs in bicipital groove – flexion of shoulder
	- Short head = medial
	- Both contribute to flexion and supination of elbow
	- PA: supraglenoid tubercle of [[scapula]] (LH), coracoid process (SH)
	- DA = [[radius]] (bicipital aponeurosis)
- Coracobrachialis
	- Crosses [[Glenohumeral Joint|shoulder joint]] medially
	- Adduction and flexion of shoulder
	- Pierced by musculocutaneous n.
	- PA: coracoid process
- Brachialis
	- Forearm flexor, arm flexor
	- Deep to biceps brachii

**Posterior** (Extensor)
- Innervation: radial n. (C5-T1)
- Triceps brachii
	- [[Forearm]] extensor
	- Arm extension at shoulder
	- 3 heads (long crosses shoulder, lateral and medial)
	- PA: infraglenoid tubercle of [[scapula]]
	- DA = [[ulna]] olecranon process
- Anconeus: small near elbow
#### Region
[[Cubital Fossa]]
#### Innervation
- Musculocutaneous n. (C5-7)
	- Anterior compartment of arm
	- Terminal branch of [[brachial plexus]], pierces coracobrachialis, distally between biceps brachii and brachialis
	- Comes out lateral to biceps as **lateral cutaneous nerve** of [[forearm]]
	- Injury = weak flexion and supination at elbow, no sensation of [[forearm]]
- Radial n. (C5-T1)
	- In radial groove of posterior [[humerus]] with deep brachial a. 
	- Supplies muscles of posterior arm and [[forearm]]
	- Injury = wrist drop, forearm extensor paralyzed

*Pass through nerves*
- Median n. lateral to brachial a. to supply [[forearm]] and [[hand]]
- Ulnar n. medial to brachial a., NOT through cubital fossa
