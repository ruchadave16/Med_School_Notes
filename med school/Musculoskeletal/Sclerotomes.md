4th week of [[Embryonic Period|embryonic period]], medial [[mesenchymal]] cells of [[Somites|somites]] are called **sclerotomes**

Form along craniocaudal axis of embryo
- Migrate to [[notochord]] and [[neural tube]] 
- Form [[cartilage]] of [[Vertebral Column|vertebral column]] and [[ribs]] → axial skeleton and flat bones of [[cranium]] 

Nerves split sclerotome to attach to [[myotome]] and [[dermatome]]

![[Screenshot 2025-08-11 at 4.41.28 PM.png]]

**Vertebral Body** – Forms around [[notochord]]
- Fusion of caudal end of one sclerotome with cranial end of another

**[[IV Disc]]** 
- Annulus Fibrosus = unfused parts of sclerotome mesenchyme
- Nucleus Pulposus = forms when [[notochord]] regresses 

**Vertebral Arch** 
- Forms around [[neural tube]]
- Sclerotome cells move dorsally to neural tube and fuse with contralateral sclerotome

>[!Clinical] Failure to do this results in [[Spina Bifida]]

- **[[Hox gene]]** expression determines variations in vertebra
