12 pairs
##### Types
1) True (vertebrosternal) Ribs: attach directly to [[sternum]]
	- Ribs 1-7
	- Anterior ends connect to sternum by costal cartilage
2) False (vertebrochondral) Ribs: indirectly attach to [[sternum]]
	- Ribs 8-10
	- Costal cartilage merges w costal cartilage above
3) Floating (vertebral, free) Ribs: don’t connect to sternum
	- Ribs 11-12

##### Features
- **Head**: articulation with vertebral bodies
- **Tubercule**: articulation with transverse process
- **Shaft**: main body of rib
- **Costal angle**: portion of ribs turn anterolaterally 

>[!Note] Most likely place for rib fracture = costal angle

- **Costal groove**: near inferior border containing intercostal neurovascular bundle
- **Costal cartilage**: cartilaginous extension of anterior part of ribs
- **Costal margin**: costal [[cartilage]] of ribs 8, 9, 10 merge with superior, forming border
- **Intercostal space**: space between ribs, numbered by rib above

>[!Example] Abnormalities
> 1st rib: wider and broader, more horizontal, 2 transverse grooves on superior part for subclavian V/A
> 	- Separated by scalene tubercule (where anterior scalene muscle attaches)
> 2nd rib: tuberosity for serratus anterior → rough area on upper surface
> 11 and 12 rib: short tubercule / costal cartilage
