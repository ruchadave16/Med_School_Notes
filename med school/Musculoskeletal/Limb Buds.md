*Mesenchyme from somatic layer of lateral plate [[mesoderm]] that extend outward with overlying ectoderm*
- Regulated by [[Hox gene]]


- Week 4: Forms limb buds as small ridge
- Week 5: Limb buds visible
- Week 6: Hand and foot plates
- Week 8: Fingers and toes visible

Apical ectodermal ridge = thickened border of [[ectoderm]]
- Inductive influence on mesenchyme
- Area near AER undifferentiated, proximal areas into muscles and bones
- AER also drives cell death → forms fingers

Bones:
- Endochondral [[ossification]] – EXCEPT clavicle
- Mesenchyme condenses (week 5)
- Ossification (week 8)

Muscles:
- From myotome of somites (paraxial skeleton)
- Hypaxial divide into dorsal and ventral muscle mass

>[!Clinical] Congenital Malformation of Limbs
>
>Muscle Agenesis 
>- [[Poland Syndrome]] – no pectoral muscle, ipsilateral breast hypoplasia, absence of 2-4 ribs
>- [[Prune Belly Syndrome]] – absence of abdominal wall muscles
>
>Limb Malrotation (rare)
>- Generally happened because of [[thalidomide]] consumption during pregnancy
>- Caused amelia (absence of limbs) and meromelia (partial absence of limb)\
>  
>  [[Sirenomelia]] – fused lower limbs due to environmental or genetic factors, commonly diabetes mellitus
>  
>  [[Clubfoot]] – foot twisted out of position
>  
>  [[Brachydactyly]] – short digits
> 
>[[Syndactyly]] – fused digits, commonly due to limb malrotation (inherited)
>
>[[Ectrodactyly]] – missing midline finger/toes due to damage to AER
>
>[[Polydactyly]] – extra digits, common, genetic/environmental
