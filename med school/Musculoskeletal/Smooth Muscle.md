##### Features
- No striations because of no sacromeres
- Dense packing, central nuclei

Slow and **involuntary** contraction

![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 5.11.57 PM.png]]
- Longitudinal section looks wavy, tapered nuclei
- Cross-sections look rounded/polygonal
- Hard to differentiate from dense [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]]
	- These usually have plumper, more open nuclei
	- Contained in muscle cell, not squished between [[Collagen|collagen]]
	- Individual fibers appear in connective 
	- Look for blood vessels and other features in connective

**Function**
- Produce motility
- Maintain tension

##### Types 
1) Multiunit Smooth Muscle
	- Iris, ciliary muscle of lens, vans deferens
	- Behave as separate motor units
	- Little to no electric coupling
	- Densely innervated, 
2) Unitary Smooth Muscle
	- Most common → uterus, GI tract, ureter, bladder
	- Spontaneously active
	- High electric coupling so coordinated contraction
3) Vascular Smooth Muscle
	- Properties of both

##### Embryology
- Walls of gut + gut derivatives 
	- Lateral plate [[mesoderm]] → visceral (splanchnic) mesoderm → smooth muscle
- Blood vessels 
	- Lateral plate [[Mesoderm|mesoderm]] → somatic mesoderm → blood vessels
	- [[Neural crest]] cells

>[[Sphincter]] and [[dilator]] muscles of pupil from [[Ectoderm]]
>[[Mammary gland]] and [[sweat gland]] tissue from ectoderm
