*17 muscles making up forearm crossing or attaching to [[elbow joint]]*
#### [[Posterior Forearm]]
- Radial n. 
- PA: lateral epicondyle or posterior radius/ulna
- Extensors of wrist and fingers or thumb movement
#### [[Anterior Forearm]]
- Median n., ulnar n., anterior interosseous n. 
- PA: medial epicondyle or anterior radius/ulna
- Flexors of wrist and fingers

#### Vasculature
Brachial a. → Ulnar and radial in [[cubital fossa]] (anastomosis around elbow)

**Anterior**
- Radial a. 
	- Deep to brachioradialis 
	- Radial pulse – lateral to tendon of flexor carpi radialis
	- Forms anastomosis around wrist
	- Wraps around wrist through [[Anatomical Snuffbox|anatomical snuffbox]]
- Ulnar a. 
	- Deep to flexor carpi ulnaris 
	- Found lateral to FCU at wrist
	- Enters [[hand]] through Guyon’s canal
- Common interosseous a. (proximal branch)
	- Anterior interosseous a. – anterior side of interosseous membrane, supplies deep forearm
	- Posterior interosseous a. – pierces interosseous septum to [[posterior forearm]]

Veins
- Deep follow arteries
- Cephalic v. – starts lateral side of thumb, up lateral forearm and arm, runs in [[Deltopectoral Triangle|deltopectoral triangle]]’s groove, drains to axillary v., visible in distal forearm
- Basilic v. – starts medial side, up medial forearm and arm, drains to brachial v.
- Medial antebrachial v. – middle of anterior forearm, drains to median cubital v. 

#### Innervation
- Radial n. (C5-T1) – brachioradialis and extensor carpi radialis longus
	- Superficial radial n – cutaneous
	- Deep radial n. – motor, pierces supinator and becomes *posterior interosseous n.*

- Ulnar n. (C8-T1) – travels between heads of flexor carpi ulnaris, enters wrist superficial to flexor retinaculum
	- Innervates flexor carpi ulnaris and medial half of flexor digitorum profundus

- Median n. (C5-T1) – deep to bicipital aponeurosis, medial to brachial a. in [[cubital fossa]]
	- Between heads of pronator teres, deep to flexor digitorum superficialis
	- Enters wrist deep to flexor retinaculum in [[carpal tunnel]]
	- Motor to forearm 

	- Branch: Anterior interosseous n. (C8-T1)
		- Between flexor pollicis longus and flexor digitorum profundus
		- Just motor, no sensory
		- Doesn’t enter hand
	
>[!Clinical] Papal Sign
>- Median nerve injury = paralysis of thenar eminence and first 2 lumbricals
>- No lateral hand sensation
>- No fine control of 2 and 3 digits
>- No opposition of thumb
