##### Types of Contractions
- Isomeric contractions: Length held constant
	- Length is fixed and muscle contracts → tension measured
	- No shortening
- Isotonic contractions: Load held constant
	- Muscle is contracted and shortening is measured

##### Length Tension Relationship 
Used to measure tension from isomeric contractions 
1) Passive tension: stretching muscle to different lengths
2) Total tension: muscle stimulated to contract at different lengths
3) Active tension: total tension - passive tension
	- Active force from contraction of muscle → explained by cross-bridge cycle model
	- Force **proportional** to # of cross-bridges 
	- Max tension if max overlap between thick and thin filaments

##### Length Force Relationship 
Measure force generated by isometric contractions at different lengths 
- Passive force: measured before stimulation of muscle
- Total force: when muscle is stimulated (active + passive)
