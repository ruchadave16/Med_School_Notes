##### Boundaries
- [[Diaphragm]] superiorly
- [[Superior pelvic aperture]] inferiorly
##### Contents

##### External Features
**4 quadrants**
- Divided horizontally by transumbilical plane and vertically by medial plane
- RUQ, RLQ, LUQ, LLQ

**Abdominal Regions**
- 9 regions partitioned by right and left midclavicular and transverse (subcostal and intertubercular) planes
- Epigastric (E), Umbilical (U), Pubic (P) in center
- Right and left hypochondriac (RH and LH), right and left lumbar (RL and LL), and right and left inguinal (RI and LI)

![[Pasted image 20250904222033.png]]

- [[Umbilicus]] – Vertebral L3-L4 level, innervated by T10
- Linea alba – vertical midline raphe from xiphoid process to pubic symphysis
- Linea semilunaris – 2 curved crooves marking lateral border of rectus abdominis m.
- [[Inguinal ligament]] – from [[anterior superior iliac spine]] of ilium to pubic tubercule
	- Comes from aponeurosis of external oblique m.
- McBurney’s Point – Location of [[appendix]] in RLQ (on line between ASIS and umbilicus, about 1/3 close to ASIS)

>[!Clinical] Surgical Incisions
>Most are midline in rectus sheath, between rectus muscles
>Exception
>- Pelvic surgeries – horizontal suprapubic incision
>- Liver surgery – RUQ, subcostal incision
>- Some splenectomies / foregut – LUQ subcostal incision

[[Rectus sheath]] surrounding muscles
- Peritoneal folds cover ligaments towards umbilicus
	- Medial umbilical fold – remnant of urachus in middle, bilateral are remnants of umbilical aa. 
	- Lateral umbilical folds cover inferior epigastric vessels

##### Blood Supply
- [[Superior epigastric artery]] – Continuation of internal thoracic a. 
	- Enters posterior rectus sheath
	- Supplies superior rectus abdominis m. 
- [[Inferior epigastric artery]] – Comes from external iliac a.
	- Superiorly in transversalis fascia to enter rectus sheath under arcuate line
	- Anastamose with superior epigastric at umbilical
- Deep circumflex iliac a. – Comes from external iliac a.
	- Deep aspect of abdominal wall parallel to [[inguinal ligament]]
- Superficial circumflex a. – From [[Femoral Artery|femoral a.]]
	- Runs in subcutaneous tissue with [[inguinal ligament]]
- Superficial epigastric a. – From [[Femoral Artery|femoral a.]]
	- Runs in superficial tissue to umbilicus

![[Pasted image 20250904224723.png]]

Deep veins parallel arteries of same name
- Subcutaneous plexus drains into internal thoracic v. superiorly and laterally / inferiorly to to superior and inferior epigastric v. 
- Cutaneous veins around umbilicus connect to hepatic portal vein ** 

>[!Clinical] [[Caput Medusae]]
>- With liver cirrhosis, hepatic portal can be blocked causing ↑ portal hypertension
>- Vessels at this connection of cutaneous with hepatic can be shunts into caval system
>- This can cause engorgement of superficial abdominal veins
##### Innervation
Thoraco-abdominal nerves – Anterior rami of T7-T11
Subcostal Nerve – Anterior rami of T12
Iliohypogastric and Ilioinguinal Nerves – terminal branches of L1 anterior rami

[[Dermatome|Dermatomes]]
- T7-T9 = skin above umbilicus
- T10 = **umbilicus**
- T11-L1 = skin below umbilicus
- L1 = Along ASIS + inguinal ligament

Nerves run behind posterior wall of rectus sheath and between internal and transversalis muscles in anterolateral muscles
##### Lymphatic Drainage
- Deep vessels with deep veins and drain to external iliac, common iliac, and R and L lumbar lymph nodes
- Superficial follow subcutaneous veins
	- Superior to trans-umbilical plain drain to axillary or maybe parasternal
	- Inferior to superficial inguinal nodes
