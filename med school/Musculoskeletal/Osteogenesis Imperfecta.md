### Etiology
- Dominant mutation that changes structure of triple helix of Type I [[collagen]]
- Mutation of $\alpha_1 COL1A1$ in Chr 17 OR $\alpha_2 COL1A2$ in Chr 7 makes malformed collagen helix
	- Different loci but same disease

**Type I** (mild)
**Type II** (most severe) – dominant negative
**Type III**
**Type IV** (least severe) – haploinsufficient

### Pathophysiology
- 2 $\alpha$ chains by gene Chr 17 and one by Chr 7
### Clinical Features
- Type I
	- Bone fragility (fractures in childhood)
	- Normal stature, normal teeth, low deformities
	- Blue sclerae
	- Hearing loss sometimes
- Type II
	- Congenital bone deformities and fractures
	- Lethal in perinatal because of respiratory insufficiency
- Type III
	- Severe form with short stature ([[kyphosis]])
	- Growth failure and fractures
	- Abnormal teeth
	- Possible hearing loss
- Type IV
	- Mild bone fragility
	- Abnormal teeth
	- Normal sclera
	- Possible hearing loss
