##### External Features
- Pelvic inlet: defined by pelvic brim (sacral promontory, arcuate line of ilium, pectineal line, pubic tubercule)
- Pelvic outlet: defined by pubic arch, ischial tuberosities, tip of coccyx

>[!Clinical] Obstetric conjugate line – minimum AP diameter (sacral promontory → pubic symphysis) → If this is too small, problem for vaginal delivery

- Interspinous diameter: ML distance between ischial spines, smallest diameter for vaginal delivery
![[Pasted image 20250920144452.png]]
- Greater pelvis (false) – pelvic inlet and plane of iliac crest
- Lesser pelvis (true) – pelvic inlet and pelvic outlet
- [[Perineum]] under [[pelvic floor]]

>[!Clinical] [[Pelvic Fractures]]

- Urogenital Hiatus – opening for urethra and vagina
- Orifice of Anal canal – opening for anus
##### Internal Features

**Ligaments**
- Sacrotuberuous ligament – sacrum to ischial tuberosity
- Sacrospinous ligament – sacrum to ischial spine
These both form greater and lesser sciatic foramen

**Walls**

- Piriformis covers anteror sacrum and SI joint – exits greater sciatic foramen

##### Innervation
Somatic
- [[Sacral plexus]]

Parasympathetic
- Pelvic splanchnic nerves (S2-S4) - preganglionic
- Pelvis – through L/R [[inferior hypogastric plexus]]
- Hindgut – through hypogastric plexus + inferior mesenteric plexus

Sympathetic
- Sacral sympathetic trunks
	- ONLY gray rami communicants
	- Form ganglion impar near coccyx
	- Presynaptic in trunk, postsynaptic enter sacral/coccygeal nerves to innervate lower extremity
- Superior hypogastric plexus
	- At bifurcation of aorta
	- From thoracic/lumbar splanchnic nerves
- L/R hypogastric n. – superior hypogastric plexus → [[inferior hypogastric plexus]]

Visceral Afferent (Sensory)
- Pelvic pain line: inferior border of peritoneum
	- If it touches peritoneum, travels with sympathetic
	- If deep in peritoneum, parasympathetic
	- EXCEPTION (Anus) – below pectinate line = somatic of inferior rectal branch of [[Pudendal Nerve|pudendal n.]]]
