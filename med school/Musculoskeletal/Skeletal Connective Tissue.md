Skeletal muscle has 3 [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]] layers
1) Epimysium: external sheath of dense connective tissue (E)
	- Continuous with [[tendon]]s
	- Forms septa that carry nerves, blood vessels, and lymphatics to each fascicle

2) Perimysium: thinner layer that covers [[muscle fascicle]] (P)
	- Defines specific functional unit
	- Penetrated by nerves and vessels

3) Endomysium: delicate layer surrounding each muscle fiber (cell) (En)
	- Contains [[Reticular Fibers|reticular fibers]] and scattered [[fibroblasts]]
	- Contains nerve fibers and capillary system
