#### Fascia
- Superficial fascia
	- Loose connective tissue + adipose
	- At knee, this connects to deep fascia
- Deep fascia
	- Dense connective tissue (continuation of Scarpa’s fascia)
	- Fascia Lata: Deep fascia of thigh
		- Connected to inguinal ligament, iliac crest, sacrum
		- Intermuscular femoral septae – attaches to femur and divides thigh into anterior, posterior, and medial parts
- Iliotibial band (IT band)
	- Lateral thickening of fascia lata
	- Attaches to iliac tubercle and anteriolateral tibial tubercle
- Saphenous hiatus
	- Opening in deep fascia over [[femoral triangle]]
	- [[Great Saphenous Vein|Great saphenous v.]] superifical to deep through hiatus to drain into femoral v. 
- Crural fascia
	- Deep fascia around leg
	- Continuation of fascia lata of thigh
	- Compression stocking for venous return → high inflammation = [[compartment syndrome]]
	- Divides into compartments: interosseous membrane (between [[tibia]] and [[fibula]]), anterior and posterior intermuscular septum (attach to fibula), and transverse intermuscular septum (deep and superficial posterior compartments)

#### Lymphatics
- Deep inguinal LN
	- Around [[Femoral Artery|femoral a.]] in [[femoral triangle]]
	- Drains superficial inguinal LN and popliteal LN, deep thigh
	- Goes to external iliac LN
- Superficial inguinal LN
	- Around [[saphenous hiatus]] in [[femoral triangle]]
	- Drains scrotum, penis skin, vaginal orafice, vagina, skin below umbilicus, anal canal below pectinate line, superficial thigh skin, superior posterolateral and anterior leg
- Popliteal LN
	- Behind knee
	- Drains leg and foot
	- Goes to deep inguinal
	
>[!Info] Superficial inguinal → deep inguinal → external iliac → common iliac → lumbar LN → [[lumbar trunk]]
