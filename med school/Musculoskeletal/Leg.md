#### Muscles
**Anterior**
- Tibialis anterior – main dorsiflexor of foot/ankle
	- 1st MA: DA

>[!Clinical] [[Medial Tibial Stress Syndrome]] 
>- overuse / repeated-load injury
>- traction periostitis by anterior or posterior

- Extensor digitorum longus (EDL)
- Extensor hallucis longus (EHL)
- Peroneus tertius
- 2 extensor retinaculum between malleoli

>[!Clinical] [[Chronic exertional compartment syndrome]]
>- Tight fascia/compartments → inflammation and ↑ pressure in compartments
>- Nerve/vessel compression, avascular necrosis, pain
>- Fasciotomy 
>- 5 P’s: Pain, pallor, pulselessness, paresthesia, paralysis

**Lateral**
- Peroneus longus superficial to peroneus brevis
	- Behind lateral malleolus
	- Peroneus longus tendon crosses sole of foot → medial foot
		- DA: 1st MT
	- Peroneus brevis inserts in 5th metatarsal tuberosity
	- Held down by 2 fibular retinacula

![[Drawing 2025-10-06 00.45.07.excalidraw]]

**Posterior**
- Transverse intermuscular septum separates into superficial and deep
- Superficial
	- Gastrocnemius
		- PA: Superior to femoral condyles
		- DA: calcaneus tuberosity
	- Plantaris
		- Superior to lateral femoral condyle
		- DA: calcaneus tuberosity
	- Soleus (sup → deep)
		- DA: Calcaneus tuberosity
	- Triceps surae: gastrocnemius + soleus → form achilles tendon
	- **Ankle-jerk reflex**

![[Drawing 2025-10-06 00.20.00.excalidraw]]

- Deep
	- Flexor digitorum longus (FDL), flexor hallucis longus (FHL), tibialis posterior (TP) – navicular tuberosity DA
	- Behind medial malleolus in tarsal tunnel
	- Tom, Dick, and Nervous Harry = order from malleolus → calcaneal tendon (TP, FDL, post. tibial a/v, FHL)

#### Vasculature
- Popliteal a/v → anterior tibial a/v and posterior tibial a/v
- Anterior tibial a. → dorsalis pedis a. 
- Posterior tibial a. → medial and lateral plantar a./v, fibular a. 

- Small saphenous v. – runs with sural n. 
- [[Great Saphenous Vein|Great saphenous v.]] – superficial on medial leg
