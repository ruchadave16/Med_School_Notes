*Muscles on [[thorax]] or [[Back|back]] with distal attachment to [[scapula]] or [[humerus]]*
- Innervation by [[Brachial Plexus|brachial plexus]]

![[Pasted image 20251009004041.png]]

#### Posterior Muscles
Scapula / arm movement

1) Trapezius
	- 3 parts: Upper, middle, lower
		- Upper: scapula elevation, upwards rotation
		- Middle: scapula retraction (PA: acromion)
		- Lower: scapula depression, upwards rotation (PA: spine of scapula)
	- Innervation = accessory n. ([[Accessory Nerve|CN XI]])
	- Damage causes weakness in shoulder elevation

2) Levator scapulae
	- Deep muscle in upper back/neck
	- Scapula elevation, downwards rotation
	- PA: superior angle of [[scapula]]

3) Rhomboid major and minor
	- Deep to trapezius
	- Work with middle trapezius to retract [[scapula]]
	- Downards rotation of scapula
	- PA: medial border of [[scapula]]

4) Latissimus dorsi
	- Makes posterior axillary fold
	- Attach to anterior humerus
	- Innervation: Thoracodorsal n. (C6-8)
	- Extension, adduction, and internal rotation of shoulder
	- DA: near intertubercular sulcus of [[humerus]]

![[Drawing 2025-10-12 13.52.18.excalidraw]]

5) Teres Major
	- Makes posterior wall of axilla
	- Adduction and internal rotation of shoulder
![[Screenshot 2025-10-12 at 4.35.42 PM.png]]
#### Anterior Muscles
1) Pectoralis Major
	- 2 parts: Clavicular head + sternocostal head
	- Makes anterior axillary fold
	- Tightness = rounded shoulders
	- Internal rotation, flexion and adduction of shoulder
	- DA: near intertubercular sulcus of [[humerus]]

2) Pectoralis minor
	- Deep to pectoralis major
	- Attaches to coracoid process
	- Tightness = rounded shoulders
	- Scapula depression
	- PA: coracoid process of [[scapula]]

3) Serratus anterior
	- Side of ribs
	- Attach on medial border of scapula
	- Stabilizes and moves [[scapula]] (upwards rotation, protraction)
	- Innervation: long thoracic n. (C5-7)
	- PA: medial border of [[scapula]]

![[Screenshot 2025-10-12 at 4.34.14 PM.png]]

>[!Clinical]
>Damage to long thoracic n. causes paralysis of serratus anterior – winged scapula, no abduction of arm past 90

4) Deltoid
	- Covers shoulder
	- Works with supraspinatus to abduct shoulder (continues motion)
	- 3 portions (anterior, middle, posterior)
		- Anterior = internal rotation and flexion of shoulder 
		- Middle (PA: acromion)
		- Posterior = external rotation and extension of shoulder (PA: spine of scapula)
	- DA: deltoid tuberosity of [[humerus]]
	- Motor: axillary n. (C5-6)
	- Sensory: Small patch = axillary n. (C5-6)
