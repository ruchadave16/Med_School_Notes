**Primary curvature**: present at birth → thoracic and sacral
**Secondary curvature**: postnatally → cervical and lumbar

Vertebral canal (foramen) goes from base of [[skull]] to coccyx, houses [[spinal cord]], [[spinal meninges]], internal vertebral ligaments, vascular elements
- Intervertebral foramen transmit spinal nerves + blood vessels laterally

>[!Clinical] Abnormal Vertebral Curvatures
>[[Kyphosis]] “hunchback” - Exaggerated thoracic curvature, results from [[osteoporosis]] of anterior thoracic spine
>
>[[Lordosis]] “swayback” - Exaggerated lumbar curvature, can be temporary
>
>[[Scoliosis]] - Abnormal lateral curvature, 80% are idiopathic
