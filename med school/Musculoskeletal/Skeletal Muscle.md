##### Features
- Long, multinucleate cells, behave as one unit, made of [[Myofibril|myofibrils]]
- Striated
- Peripheral nuclei
- Surrounded by [[sarcoplasmic reticulum]] (SR) and invaginated by [[T Tubules|transverse tubules]] (T tubules)

**Voluntary** and quick [[Skeletal Muscle Contractions|contractions]]
##### Components 
**[[Myofibril]]** – thick and thin filaments longitudinally into [[sacromere]]s
**[[Sacromere]]** – Z-line to Z-line
Surrounded by 3 layers of [[Skeletal Connective Tissue|connective tissue]]


![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 4.20.07 PM.png]]
##### Energy System
[[Excitation-Contraction Coupling]] converts potential to tension

[[ATP]] reserves are only 5-6 seconds through [[phosphocreatine system]]
##### Appearance 
**Longitudinal**
- Multi-nucleate cells, nuclei (N) at periphery
- Very long, highly eosinophilic with **striations**
- [[Fibroblasts|Fibroblast]] nuclei (F) seen between fibers

![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 4.29.00 PM.png]]

**Cross-Sectional**
- Highly eosinophilic but can’t see striations
- Individual fibers can be seen (MF) with [[Skeletal Connective Tissue|skeletal connective tissue]] (perimysium – separates fascicles])

![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 4.30.49 PM.png]]

##### Types of Skeletal Muscle
Can be separated by rate of contraction (fast vs slow), pathway for ATP synthesis (oxidative phosphorylation vs glycolysis)

Metabolic difference based on → capillary density, # mitochondria, glycogen and myoglobin levels

##### Embryology
Almost **all** from [[Mesoderm|mesoderm]]

 >[!Warning] Exceptions
 >[[Myoepithelial cells]], [[sphincter]] and [[dilator]] muscles of pupil
 
- Ventrolateral cells of [[somites]] become [[Myotome|myotomes]], remaining become [[Dermatome|dermatomes]]
- Occipital somites form head musculature 
	- Innervated by [[cranial nerves]] 
	- Some are part of [[pharyngeal arches]]
