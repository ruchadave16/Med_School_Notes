*Modified cardiac cells that generate waves of depolarization to stimulate contractions*

Found in sub[[Endocardium|endocardial]] layer and adjacent [[myocardium]]

#### Nodes
1) Sinoatrial (SA) Node: At [[Right Atrium|RA]] near [[SVC]] entrance 
	- Pacemaker
	- Initiates depolarization that spreads through atria and to AV node

2) Atrioventricular (AV) Node: [[Right Atrium|RA]] beneath [[endocardium]] at junction of [[Right Ventricle|RV]]
	- Impulse goes through fibrous skeleton to ventricles through AV Bundle (of His)

3) Atrioventricular Bundle (of His)
	- Tissue conducting from AV to IV septum
	- AV bundle splits into **[[Purkinje Fibers]]** 
#### Cycle
1) Late diastole: 
	- Both sets of chambers relaxed, ventricles fill passively

2) Atrial systole:
	- Atrial contraction forces small amount of blood into ventricles
	- Mainly filling of atria

		*S1 sound – lub – systole starts*

3) Isovolumetric ventricular contraction:
	- 1st part of ventricular contraction where AV close but blood not pushed yet

4) Ventricular ejection
	- ventricular P > artery P so semilunar open and blood ejected

		*S2 sound – dub – diastole starts*

5) Isovolumetric ventricular relaxation
	- P in ventricles falls and blood falls into semilunar cusps, closing valves

#### Phases on [[ECG]]
Phase 1: Last part of filling ventricle due to atrial contraction ← P-wave of ECG
- Most of atrial filling is in earlier part of diastole

Phase 2: Mitral closes, ventricular contraction ← S1
- Aortic closed so volume constant in ventricles **end diastole volume**

Phase 3: Vent. pressure > atrial pressure so aortic valve opens
- Rapid ejection of blood to aorta
- C wave in atrial pressure curve since there is a slight pressure back of mitral valve towards atrium

Phase 4: Contraction ending so slower ejection
- T wave shows ventricular repolarization
- dAtrial pressure has V wave because atrium filling and P rises since mitral is closed

Phase 5:
- Vent. pressure < aortic pressure so aortic valve closes ← S2
- Vent. Pressure > atrial pressure so mitral closed
- Volume still same **end systolic volume**

Phase 6:
- Vent. pressure < atrial so mitral opens
- Ventricle fills rapidly (NO atrial contraction)

Phase 7: 
- Slower filling that is passive
