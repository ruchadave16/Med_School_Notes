##### Features
- Striated, single cells
- Elongated, branched cells with intercalated discs
- *Central nuclei*
- Less organized than skeletal muscles
- Numerous, large [[mitochondria]] and [[glycogen]] stores

**Involuntary** and rhythmic contractions
- [[Intercalated discs]] (in arrows) linking cells

![[Pasted image 20251108135605.png]]

##### Contraction Methods
Similar to [[Skeletal Muscle Contractions]]
- Myosin and actin, [[tropomyosin]] inhibition, Ca regulation
- Same [[Sacromere|sacromere]]s
- Same cross-bridge method

Differences:
- Intrinsic and spontaneous contraction

##### Cardiac [[Sarcoplasmic Reticulum]]
- Same functions but less developed and irregular
	- Does NOT separate myofilaments into myofibrils here
- T-Tubules at Z-Line here 
- **Diad** NOT triad

##### Embryology
Visceral (sphlanchnic) [[Mesoderm|mesoderm]] surrounding endothelial heart tube
