- Arterial thrombus: occlusive, has ↑ [[fibrin]] and [[Thrombocytes|platelets]]
	- Common location = coronary and cerebral arteries
- Venous thrombus: more [[Erythrocytes|RBCs]] and less platelets
	- **90% happen in veins of lover extremities – [[Deep Vein Thrombosis|DVT]]**
- Mural thrombus: in chamber of heart
- Vegetation: thrombus on heart valve – can be infected ([[infective endocarditis]]) or sterile ([[nonbacterial thrombotic endocarditis]])

**Fate**
- Propagation – growth
- [[Embolism]] – dislodge and travel 
- Dissolution – common with newer thrombi through fibrinolysis
	- [[tPA]] is agent effective for treating clots but only when given in first few hours
- Organization – effort to convert into fibrous lump if dissolution doesn’t work
	- Ingrowth of endothelial cells, smooth muscles, fibroblasts
#### Histology
Thrombi have laminations (stripes) called **lines of Zahn**
- Pale stripes = platelets + fibrin
- Dark stripes = RBC deposition
