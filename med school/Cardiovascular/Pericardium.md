*Double-walled sac with **serous fluid***

>[!Clinical] [[Pericardial Effusion]]: fluid collection from pericardial capillaries in pericardial cavity

1) Fibrous pericardium
	- Tough, dense [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]]
	- Continuous with tunica adventitia of blood vessels
	- Protects [[Cardiovascular/Heart]]
	- Attached to [[diaphragm]], [[sternum]], and great vessels

2) Serous pericardium
	- Continuous sheet folded over
	- Parietal – lines fibrous pericardium
	- Visceral ([[epicardium]]) – covers heart

Pericardial space in between serous layers with serous fluid to ↓ friction

#### Sinuses
- Visceral + parietal serous pericardium continuous make sinuses
	 1) Transverse pericardial sinus
		 - Behind [[ascending aorta]] and [[pulmonary trunk]] 
		 - Anterior to [[SVC]]
	 2) Oblique pericardial sinus
		 - Behind [[atria]] where [[SVC]], [[IVC]], and pulmonary vv. enter heart

#### Neurovasculature
- Anterior to root of lung, in pericardium
- Blood supply = pericardiacophrenic a. (off internal thoracic a.)
- Innervation = [[Phrenic Nerve|phrenic n.]]

>[!Clinical] [[Cardiac Tamponade]] = Compression on [[Cardiovascular/Heart]] in pericardial sac
