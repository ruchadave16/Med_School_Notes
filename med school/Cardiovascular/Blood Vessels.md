#### Layers
1) Tunica Intimia – Innermost layer
	- Simple squamous endothelium with [[basal lamina]]
	- Subendothelial layer – loose connective tissue 
	- Can have internal elastic lamina – [[Elastic Fibers|elastic fibers]] with pores for diffusion

2) Tunica Media – middle layer
	- Layers of [[smooth muscle]] cells 
	- [[Elastic fibers]], [[Collagen]] III, [[proteoglycans]] ← made by **smooth muscle cells**
	- Large arteries have thin external elastic lamina separating from outermost 
	- Prevents over dilation

3) Tunica Adventitia – outermost
	- Continuous with surrounding connective tissue
	- Longitudinal [[Fibroblasts|fibroblast]] and connective tissue, some elastic fibers, collagen I
	- Resists elongation

4) Vasa Vasorum + Nervi Vasorum
	- In adventitia of large arteries 
	- Vasa Vasorum – System of vessels that give blood to vascular walls
	- Nervi Vasorum – [[Autonomic Nervous System|Autonomic]] nerves that contract [[Smooth Muscle|smooth muscles]] in walls

#### Types of Blood Vessels

1) [[Artery]]: More smooth muscle and elastic tissue
2) Arteriole
3) Capillary: Site of [[Transcapillary Fluid Exchange|exchange]] of gas and nutrients through diffusion of lipid-soluble, bulk flow of water and lipid-insoluble, and vesicle transport of large molecules
	- Small endothelial tubes network
	- Lowest blood flow velocity
	- Highest cross-sectional area
4) Venule
5) [[Vein]]: Blood towards the heart

#### Endothelial Cells
**Functions**
- Selective permeability barrier
- Non-thrombogenic barrier (ensure blood is liquid)
- Modulate blood flow + vascular resistance
- Cell growth regulation
- Immune response regulation
- [[Extracellular Matrix|ECM]] maintanence
- [[Lipoprotein]] + cholesterol metabolism
