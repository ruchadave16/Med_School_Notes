*Abnormal accumulation of excess fluid within tissues*

- Subcutaneous edema 
	- Dependent → influenced by gravity
	- Pitting
- Periorbital edema
	- In eyelids, sign of renal disease
	- In children, consider hypoalbuminemia from nephrotic syndrome
- Pulmonary edema
	- Associated with CHF
- Cerebral edema
	- 

**Inflammatory Edema**
- [[Acute Inflammation]] → vascular permeability → fluid and intravascular proteins lost
- Results in protein-rich fluid mix called exudate

![[Screenshot 2026-01-24 at 5.11.06 PM.png]]

**Non-Inflammatory Edema**
- Vessel wall intact so intravascular proteins maintained but hydrostatic / oncotic pressure causes loss of fluid
- Results in protein-poor fluid called transudate

![[Screenshot 2026-01-24 at 5.12.43 PM.png]]
#### Mechanisms
- ↑ [[Hydrostatic Pressure|hydrostatic pressure]]
	- From any disorders that impede on **venous** return 
	
>[!Example] [[Congestive Heart Failure]]
>- Pulmonary venous congestion → ↑ pulmonary hydrostatic pressure → transudative fluid out of circulation into lungs → [[pleural effusion]] 

- ↓ [[Oncotic Pressure|oncotic pressure]]
	- Inadequate [[albumin]] synthesis or loss of it

>[!Example]
> [[Cirrhosis]] – Albumin is made in liver 
> Protein malnutrition
> Protein losing glomerulopathies

- Lymphatic obstruction
	- Impaired reabsorption of interstitial fluid = [[lymphedema]]
- Sodium retention
	- ↑ Na+ retention means ↑ water retention so intravascular fluid volume increases and oncotic pressure ↓ 
	- Seen in renal diseases and [[Congestive Heart Failure|CHF]]
- Inflammation
	- Triggers release of cellular mediators that widen inter-endothelial spaces making space for fluid and proteins to escape
