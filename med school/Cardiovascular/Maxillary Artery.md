*One of 2 terminal ends of [[External Carotid Artery|external carotid a.]]*

- Develops from 1st [[Pharyngeal Arches|pharyngeal arch]]

![[Screenshot 2025-11-01 at 4.49.12 PM.png]]

3 main parts
1) Mandibular portion
	- Deep auricular a.  – TMJ, external acoustic meatus, tympanic membrane
	- Anterior tympanic a. – TMG, tympanic membrane, tympanic cavity
	- **Middle meningeal a.** – through foramen spinosum → [[Dura Mater]]
	- **Inferior alveolar a.** – mandibular foramen to lower [[teeth]] and gums
	
>[!Clinical] Rupture of middle meningeal a. causes epidural hematoma

2) Pterygoid portion
	- Between 2 heads of lateral pterygoid, to muscles of mastication
	- Deep temporal a., pterygoid a, masseteric a., buccal a.

3) Pterygoidpalatine portion
	- Through pterygomaxillary fissure → pterygopalatine fossa
	- Posterior superior alveolar a. – uppar molar and gums, maxillary sinus
	- **Infraorbital a.** – through infraorbital fissure through foramen to lower eyelid, upper lip, cheek, anterior maxillary teeth
	- **Descending palatine a.** – hard palate, gums, soft palate, palatine tonsils
	- A. to pterygoid canal – [[Pharyngotympanic Tube|auditory tube]]
	- Pharyngeal branch – nasopharynx
	- **Sphenopalatine a.** – terminal branch to nasal cavity and septum

>[!Clinical] Sphenopalatine a. is main cause of severe posterior nosebleeds
