Phase 4: [[Resting Potential|Resting potential]] is when $K^+$ conductance is high and sodium gates have *not* opened
	→ Close to -90mV
Phase 0: Depolarization because of sodium voltage gated channels opening
Phase 1: Repolarization because sodium channels go inactive and potassium goes out
Phase 2: Plateau because calcium voltage gated open and calcium influx balances out the potassium efflux
Phase 3: Repolarization because calcium channels close (time dependent) and potassium efflux continued to bring the polarization back
