### Definition
Disease of elastic arteries hardening

### Etiology
##### Predisposing Factors
- Dyslipidemia (> 3:1 LDL:HDL ratio)
- Hyperglycemia of diabetes
- Hypertension
- Toxins from smoking

### Pathophysiology
- Damaged or dysfunctional endothelial cells → oxidize LDLs in [[Blood Vessels|tunica intima]]
- This causes adhesion and entry of macrophages to eliminate LDL
- Lipid-filled macrophages (foam cells) accumulate forming fatty streaks
- When worsening, form fibro-fatty plaques (atheromas)

- In elastic arteries, this can destroy walls and cause bulging, eventually causing [[aneurysm]]
- In muscular arteries, can occlude blood flow causing [[ischemic heart disease]]
