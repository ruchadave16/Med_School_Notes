*Blood supply to [[myocardium]]*

2 branches (RCA and LCA)

1) Right Coronary Artery (RCA)
	- Opens to R. coronary sinus
	- Off [[aorta]] in r. coronary sulcus
	- Branches supply [[Right Atrium|RA]]. [[Right Ventricle|RV]], Conus of [[Right Ventricle|RV]]
	- **Sinoatrial (SA) nodal a.** → supplys SA node and pulmonary trunk
		- *60% from RCA, 40% from LCx*
	- **Right marginal a.** → inferior border of [[heart]] to supply [[right ventricle]]
	- RCA wraps around heart at crux to turn into **posterior intraventricular a., or posterior descending a. (PDA)**
		- In posterior interventricular sulcus
		- Branch to AV node
		- Supplies R/L posterior ventricular walls + posterior 1/3 interventricular septum
		- Gives septal branches

**Cardiac Dominance** – Determines which vessel gives rize to PDA
	RCA dominant in most ppl, LCA in 15%, co-dominance in 18%

2) Left Coronary Artery (LCA)
	- Artery off of [[aorta]] behind pulmonary trunk
	- **Anterior interventricular a., or left anterior descending (LAD)**
		- In anterior interventricular groove to apex
		- Supplies anterior surface of L ventricle and anterior 2/3 IV septum
		- **Lateral diagonal a.** → supplies L anterior ventricular wall
		- Septal branches → supply 2/3 IV septum
		- [[Anastomose]]s with PDA
	- **Circumflex branch of LCA (LCx)**
		- Follows coronary groove to back
		- Ends at crux, can anastomose with RCA
		- Supplies [[Left Atrium|LA]] and [[Left Ventricle|LV]]
		- **Left (obtuse) marginal a.** → Left border of heart, Left ventricle
