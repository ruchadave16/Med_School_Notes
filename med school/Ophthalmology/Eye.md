#### External Features
- [[Eyelids]]: binding orbits
- Orbital fascia: around [[Eyeball|eyeballs]] and muscles
- Orbital fat
- Conjuctiva: mucous membrane lining eyelids 
- Conjuctival fornices: space deep to eyelids

#### Inner Components of Eye
- Anterior Segment (front of lens)
	- Aqueous humor
	- Anterior chamber – [[Cornea]] → [[Iris]]
	- Posterior chamber – [[Iris]], [[Ciliary Body]], anterior lens
	- Canal of Schlemm – [[Cornea]] → iris angle
		- Location of aqueous humor drainage

![[Screenshot 2025-11-08 at 2.15.38 PM.png]]

>[!Info] FLOW: ciliary body stroma → ciliary process → posterior chamber → anterior chamber through iris → canal of Schlemm → venous system

![[Pasted image 20251027222159.png]]

- [[Lens]]
- Posterior Segment (behind lens)
	- Vitreous humor
	- Vitreous chamber
	- Vitreous body – refractile gel filling cavity
		- Maintains lens position
		- Supports neural retina
	- [[Retina]]
	- Optic disc
#### Bony Landmarks
- Orbital margin: around orbital opening
- Superior wall: orbital part of frontal bone
- Medial wall: orbital plate of ethmoid bone
- Inferior wall: maxilla, parts of zygomatic and palatine bone
- Lateral wall: frontal process of zygomatic bone, greater wing of [[sphenoid]]
- Apex: optic canal and superior orbital fissure

#### Vasculature
![[Screenshot 2025-10-26 at 11.33.25 PM.png]]

- Ophthalmic a. 
	- [[Internal Carotid Artery|Internal carotid a.]] 1st part
	- Main blood supply
	- Goes through optic foramen

- Central retinal a. 
	- Branch of ophthalmic a. inferior to optic n.
	- Pierces optic n. sheath, runs in nerve to eyeball
	- Comes out at optic disc → internal part of [[Retina]]
	- Terminal branches are only supply to [[Retina]]

 - Superior and inferior ophthalmic v. through SOF to cavernous sinus
 - Central retinal v. → cavernous sinus
#### Innervation
- [[Optic Nerve|Optic n.]] CN II
	- Sensory input from [[Retina]]
	- Leaves through optic canal
	- Has optic sheath (cranial meninges and subarachnoid space)

- [[Oculomotor n.]] (CN III)
	- Superior and inferior divisions
	- Superior to superior rectus and levator palpebrae superioris
	- Inferior to medial rectus, inferior rectus, inferior oblique
		- Fibers to ciliary ganglion

- [[Trochlear n.]] (CN IV)
	- Superior oblique
	- Anterior brainstem

- [[Abducens n.]] (CN VI)
	- Through SOF to lateral rectus

- [[Ophthalmic Nerve|Ophthalmic n.]] (CN V1)
	- Frontal n. → supraorbital n. and supratrochlear n. (skin of forehead and scalp)
	- Nasociliary n. → anterior and posterior ethmoidal n. and infratrochlear n. (tip of nose, conjunctiva, mucous membrane)
		- Long ciliary n. → postsynaptic sympathetic to dilator pulillae, sensory to iris and cornea
	- Lacrimal n. → [[lacrimal gland]], upper [[Eyelids|eyelid]], superolateral orbit

- Ciliary ganglion
	- [[Parasympathetic Nervous System|Parasympathetic]] innervation of eyeball synapse
	- Short ciliary n. → all innervation to [[Ciliary Body]] and sphincter papillae

>[!Clinical]
>-  Accommodation
>	- Parasympathetic through oculomotor makes ciliary muscle contract, stretching lens
>- Pupillary Light Reflex
>	- Sphincter papillae: parasympathetic constriction in light
>	- Dilator papillae: sympathetic dilation in dark

>[!Clinical]
>[[Mydriasis]]: Abnormal dilation from sympathetic response
>- High intracranial pressure
