Unique since it depends on **hyperpolarization**

- In dark, Na depolarizes outside based on cGMP levels
- In light, this Na channel is closed so membrane is more negative
	- Light hits 11-cis retinal eventually causing cGMP to become GMP
	- ↓ cGMP means no Na channels open
	- HYPERPOLARIZATION

1) Sodium closing causes hyperpolarization
2) [[Glutamate]] released from [[Photoreceptors|photoreceptors]] (reduced by hyperpolarization)
3) ↓ [[Glutamate]] → either inhibition or excitation of bipolar and horizontal cells 
#### Response
- Light turns off ionotropic cells (inhibition) and turns on metabotropic cells (excitation)
- Depends on [[visual receptive fields]]
	- On-center = metabotropic → excitation
	- On center = ionotropic → inhibition
	- Off-center = metabotropic → inhibition
	- Off-center = ionotropic → excitation
- Off center (horizontal cells mediate)

#### Output
- M or magnocellular cells
	- Large receptive fields
	- Rod input, no color
	- Gross feature and movement analysis
- P or parvocellular cells
	- Smaller receptive fields
	- Color sensitive
	- Fine detail
