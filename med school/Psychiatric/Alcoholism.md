- AUD = problamatic pattern of [[alcohol]] use
### Etiology
- Genetic, cultural, environmental, biological
- Genetic Risk
	- 50-60% vulnerability
	- 3-4x higher risk in individuals with family members. risk ↑ with closeness, # of family, severity of problems
	- Risk of identical > fraternal
	- High rates in adopted children away from alcoholic parents
	- >>> Risk in sons of alcoholic fathers
- Comorbidity
	- [[Anxiety Disorders|Anxiety disorders]]
### Pathophysiology
- Mechanism of disease
- Relevant pathways (e.g. immune, hormonal)

### Clinical Features
- [[Alcohol Intoxication]]
- Must meet ≥2 of 11 criteria over 12 months (Mild = 2-3, Moderate = 4-5, Severe = 6+)
	- > quantity and longer period of alcohol use than intented
	- desire and unsuccessful efforts to cut down
	- time spent to obtain alcohol and use
	- craving
	- recurrent use = failure to fulfill obligations
	- continued use despite social and interpersonal problems
	- social, occupational activities are given up because of drinking
	- recurrent use in situations when it is hazardous
	- continued use despite knowing of physical or physiological problem
	- tolerance
	- [[alcohol withdrawal]]
- Early remission = none symptoms for 3 months
- Sustained remission = none symptoms for 12+ months
### Diagnosis
- CAGE
	- Have you felt like you should cut down drinking
	- Have ppl annoyed you by criticizing your drinking
	- Have you ever felt guilty about drinking
	- Have you ever had drink as first thing in morning
- Other questions
	- Age when first started
	- How much? Average/day and last time drunk
	- Problems caused
	- Does anyone complain abt drinking
	- Withdrawal symptoms
	- Have you done detox / rehab
- Labs
	- Blood ethanol / hair EtG
	- Phosphatidylethanol (slow drinking over weeks)
	- CDT (heavy / binge drinking) – highly specific but not sensitive (high false negative, low false positive)
	- Biomarkers: GGT, ALT, AST, MCV,

### Treatment
1) Detox – [[Alcohol Withdrawal]]
2) Rehab 
- [[Naltrexone]]
	- [[Opioid antagonist]] ($\mu$) so no rewarding effect
	- ↓ cravings
	- CONTRAINDICATION: liver disease or opioid therapy
- [[Acamprosate]]
	- Weak NMDA antagonist and GABA A agonist
	- Helps reduce cravings
	- Contraindication: renal impairment
- [[Disulfiram]]
	- Inhibits aldehyde dehydrogenase so aldehyde accumulation when alcohol consumed
	- Nausea ↑ to deter drinking
	- AVOID in intoxicated people
		- 12 hr abstinence minimum
- [[Topiramate]]
- [[Gabapentin]]

- Psychosocial – motivational interviewing, 12 step programs, CBT, individual or group therapy

### Complications
- Alcohol poisoning, cardiomyopathy, gastritis, liver cirrhosis, polyneuropathy, psychoses
- Secondary
	- Cancer, diabetes, GI disease, heart disease, liver disease, pancreatitis, pneumonia, tuberculosis
- [[Wernickes Encephalopathy]]
- [[Korsakoff Amnesia]]
