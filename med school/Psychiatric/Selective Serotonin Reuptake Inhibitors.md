Citalopram, escitalopram (more potent form of citalopram), [[fluoxetine]] (long half life, lower risk of withdrawal but higher interactions), fluvoxamine, paroxetine, sertraline
- Most metabolized by [[CYP2D6]]
#### Action
- Inhibit serotonin transporter (SERT) increasing [[serotonin]] levels
- Minimal impact on other neurotransmitters
#### Uses
- First line of treatment for [[Major Depressive Disorder|Depression]], [[General Anxiety Disorder]], [[PTSD]], [[OCD]], [[Panic Disorder]], [[Bulimia Nervosa]]
#### Administration

#### Dosing
#### Benefit
- No additional impact from other receptors (anticholinergic, antihistaminic, and α-adrenergic effects)
#### Adverse Effects
- Nausea
- Anorexia, diarrhea
- Insomnia
- Sexual dysfunction (↓ libido, slow ejaculation)
- [[Serotonin Syndrome]] if combined with other serotonin creating drugs
#### Drug Interactions
- CYP2D6 inhibitors
	- Paroxetine, fluoxetine – can elevate substrate levels like TCAs or antipsychotics
- Fluvoxamien inhibits CYP1A2, CYP2C19, CYP3A4
- Minimal CYP impact
	- Citalopram, escitalopram, sertraline
