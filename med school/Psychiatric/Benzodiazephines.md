Alprazolam, lorazepam, clonazepam, diazepam, oxazepam

>[!Warning] NOT for long term use
#### Pharmacokinetics
- High lipophilic so high GI absorption and brain impact
- Metabolism
	- [[CYP3A4]] and [[CYP2C19]] but don’t induce these enzymes
	- Produce phase I metabolites so action might be longer than half-life

![[Screenshot 2026-01-13 at 1.22.27 PM.png]]
- LOT (lorazepam, oxazepam, temazepam) bypass Phase I and directly do glucuronidation Phase II
#### Action
- Sleeping, hypnosis
	- Shortens time to falling asleep (latency)
	- ↑ Stage 2 non-REM sleep
	- ↓ REM sleep and stage 4 slow wave
- Muscle relaxation ([[Diazepam]])
- Anterograde amnesia
- Anticonvulsant ([[clonazepam]], [[Diazepam]])
- Antiolytic – calming, reduce anxiety
- Respiratory and CV minimal effects

- Additive depressant effect with ethanol, opioids, other sedatives
#### Mechanism
- Bind on [[GABA A Receptors|GABA A receptor]] specific site (NOT binding site)
	- **DO NOT BIND TO GABA B**
- Cl- influx → hyperpolarization → inhibition of neuron
#### Uses
Severe [[Panic Disorder]], [[General Anxiety Disorder]]
- Not first line, only used for acute anxiety or initial management
#### Administration

#### Dosing
- Tolerance: Causes downregulation of [[GABA A Receptors|GABA A receptor]]
- Dependence: taper gradually
	- Withdrawal = anxiety, insomnia
#### Adverse Effects
- Common
	- Lightheadedness, confusion, motor incoordination
	- HIGH risk with alcohol use and other CNS
- Less common
	- Headache, blurred vision, weakness
- Rare
	- Allergy, hepatotoxicity, hematological abnormalities
#### Overdose
- **CNS symptoms with normal vitals**
#### Contraindication
- [[Alcohol]]
- Elderly patients
	- Lower Phase I metabolism so longer half-life
	- Higher risk of amnesia, confusion
- May depress respiration in pt with [[liver disease]], pulmonary disease, or cardiovascular disease
