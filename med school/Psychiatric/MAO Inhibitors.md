selegiline (MAO-B), phenelzine, tranylcypromine

>[!Warning]
>Tyramine buildup warning 
>DO NOT eat aged cheese, wine, cured meat for up to 2 weeks after discontinuing
#### Action
- IRREVERSIBLY inhibit [[MAO]] so no breakdown of monoamines
- No distinction between MAO-A and MAO-B
#### Uses
- [[Major Depressive Disorder|Depression]] that doesn’t respond to anything else
#### Administration

#### Dosing

#### Adverse Effects
- Orthostatic Hypotension – dopamine accumulation
- CNS stimulation
	- Tremor, excitement
	- Insomnia
	- Convulsions
- Weight Gain
- Vitamin B6 deficiency
#### Drug Interactions
- Serotonergic Agents ([[Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors|SSRIs]], [[Serotonin Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors|SNRIs]], [[Tricyclic Antidepressants|TCAs]]) can cause [[Serotonin Syndrome]]
- [[Sympathomimetic Drugs|Sympathomimetics]] can cause hypertensive crisis
- “Cheese Reaction”
	- **[[Tyramine]]** is metabolized by MAO so CANNOT eat aged cheese, wine, cured meats
	- Buildup can cause hypertensive crisis
	- UP TO 2 WEEKS after discontinuing drug
#### Contraindication
