### Etiology
- Risk factors
	- Adolescence, young adults
	- Females and transgender individuals
	- Athletes
### Pathophysiology
- Mechanism of disease
- Relevant pathways (e.g. immune, hormonal)
### Clinical Features
- Pt have intense fear of weight gain, distorted body image, and significant low weight
- Additional symptoms – resolve after weight normal
	- Depression
	- Anxiety
	- OCD
	- PTSD
	- Substance abuse
- Categories
	- Restricting 
	- Binge eating / purging

- Mild: BMI 17-18.49 kg/m²
- Moderate: BMI 16–16.99 kg/m²
- Severe: BMI 15–15.99 kg/m²
- Extreme: BMI <15 kg/m²

### Diagnosis
- Screening (SCOFF)
	- Do you make yourself sick if full
	- Do you think you’ve lost control over your eating habits
	- Loss > 15 lbs in 3 months
	- Do you believe urself to be fat
	- Does food dominate nature life
- Criteria
	- Calorie restriction
	- Intense fear of gaining weight
	- Body image disturbance (denial)

### Treatment
- Psychotherapy
- Outpatient programs
- Hospitalizations
### Complications
- Severe malnutrition
	- Organ failure
- Impaired growth
- ↓ bone density
- Amenorrhea
- Hyponatremia
- Bone marrow suppression
- [[Refeeding Syndrome]]
	- ↑ insulin – ↓ phosphate, magnesium – arrythmia, seizure risk
### Prognosis
- Early treatment = better outcomes
- Adolescents more favorable response
- 70% reach ≥ 85% weight with treatment
- Long term mortality = 10-20%
- **Highest mortality of all psychiatric illnesses**
	- 5-10x suicide rate of normal population

### High-Yield Associations
- 1.4% in women, 0.1% in males
