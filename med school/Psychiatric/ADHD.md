*Persistent patterns of inattention and hyperactivity that impacts functioning*
### Etiology
- Genetic: 75-90% heritability
- Gene links = D4, DAT, MAO A
- Environmental factors: prenatal [[alcohol]] or [[tobacco]] exposure = 9-20% symptoms

### Pathophysiology
- Neurotransmitter dysregulation
	- Dopamine and noradrenergic pathways in prefrontal cortex implicated
- Smaller volume in frontal, basal ganglia, caudate nucleus
- Functional alterations in left prefrontal cortex and left pallidum/putamen

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- Inattention
	- Hyperactivity
	- Daydreaming vs disruptive activity
	- Difficulty focusing
	- Impulsivity
	- Constant motion

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria
	- Persistent symptoms of inattention / hyperactivity for 6+ months
	- Onset before age 12
	- 2+ settings presence of symptoms
	- Impact social academic life significantly
- Test: 
	- Conners Rating Scale

### Treatment
- Behavioral (first line for kids <6 yrs)
	- Parental training (CDC guidelines), classroom intervention, individual learning plan
- Pharmacotherapy
	- ≥6 or adults
	- Stimulants
		- Methlyphenidate Based – **[[Methylphenidate]]** (Ritalin), **Dexmethylphenidate** (Focalin)
		- Amphetamine Based – [[Adderall]], **[[Lisdexamfetamine]]** (**Vyvanse**)
	- Nonstimulants
		- [[Serotonin Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors|SNRIs]] - **[[Atomoxetine]]**, [[Viloxazine]] (2nd line for pt with comorbid anxiety)
		- $\alpha2$ agonists [[Guanfacine]] or [[Clonidine]]
