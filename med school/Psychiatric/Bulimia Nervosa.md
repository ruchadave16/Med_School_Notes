### Etiology
- Primary causes
	- Inheritance: 30-80%
- Risk factors
	- PTSD
- Comorbid
	- Substance use, anxiety, depression, BD, PTSD, and borderline and avoidant personality disorders
### Pathophysiology
- Mechanism of disease
- Relevant pathways (e.g. immune, hormonal)

### Clinical Features
- Binge eating + purging through vomiting or laxatives
- Fatigue and muscle weakness
- Dental erosion
- [[Edema]]
- **Russels Sign** – calluses on knuckles from inducing vomiting

- Mild: 1–3 episodes/week
- Moderate: 4–7 episodes/week
- Severe: 8–13 episodes/week
- Extreme: 14+ episodes/week
### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria
	- Recurrent binge eating
	- Compensation to avoid weight gain
	- Once per week for 3 months
	- Negative body image

### Treatment
- Correction of electrolyte imbalance
- 1st Line: [[Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors|SSRIs]] to reduce binge eating + purging regardless of depression status
	- [[Fluoxetine]] in combination with psychotherapy
	
>[!Warning] [[Bupropion]] is contraindicated because of higher seizure risk

- CBT, family therapy
- Normalize eating routines

### Complications
- Parotid gland [[hypertrophy]]
- Mallory-Weiss syndrome
- Metabolic alkalosis
- Dehydration
- Cardiac complications
- Endocrine complications
- Dental issues
#### Prognosis
- High relapse
- Adolescents recover in 2+ years
### High-Yield Associations
- 1-1.5% of adolescents
- F to M ratio of 3:1
