#### Action
- Chemically similar to sodium
	- Can enter voltage gated sodium channels, cause partial depolarization of cells
	- Selective to brain + kidneys
- Inhibits enzymes in [[inositol]] recycling
	- Depletion of phosphatidylinositol (PI) and PIP2
	- Low signaling through PIP2 pathways
	- Lower signaling thorugh IP3 and DAG pathways
- Limits GSK-3
- Excreted by kidneys fully
#### Uses
- Acute + maintenance for [[Bipolar I]] and [[Bipolar II]]
	- 2/3 pt say good mood stabilization over 6-12 months
	- 1/3 have no recurrence in 3 yrs
#### Administration
- **MUST check renal function before administering**
- Orally as lithium carbonate

>[!Info] Monitor
>- Serum lithium levels
>- Renal function
>- TSH
>- CBC – leukocytosis
#### Dosing
- Slow onset of action, long duration, *narrow therapeutic window*
- **Discontinue gradually so no relapse**
#### Adverse Effects
- Narrow therapeutic index
- Common
	- Polyuria, polydipsia
	- GI effects
	- Fine tremor
	- Weight gain
	- [[Hypothyroidism]]
	- Long term use = renal damage
	- Nephrogenic [[Diabetes Insipidus|diabetes insipidus]] – excessive urination + thirst
	- Benign leukocytosis due to [[neutrophilia]]
- Toxicity
	- Ataxia
	- Coarse Tremor
	- Confusion
	- Slurred speech
	- Seizures 
	- Coma
	- Renal toxicity

WHAT can make it worse
- Dehydration
- ACEIs
- Thiazide diuretics
- NSAIDs
#### Contraindication
- Caution with cardiovascular disease patients
- Associated with [[Epstein Anomaly]]
- Increased risk in 1st trimester
