*Recurrent episodes of eating a lot of food WITHOUT compensatory activities*
### Etiology
- Primary causes
- Risk factors
	- Obesity
	- Dieting history
	- Mood and anxiety disorders
	- Genetic predisposition
	- Childhood abuse

### Pathophysiology
- Mechanism of disease
- Relevant pathways (e.g. immune, hormonal)

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- GI discomfort
	- Shame and social withdrawal
- Signs
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria
	- Recurrent binge eating
	- 1x a week for 3 months with 3+ of following
		- Eating more rapidly than normal
		- Until uncomfortably full
		- Large amounts when not hungry
		- Alone due to embarrassment
		- Feeling guilty or disgusted after
	- Distress
	- No regulatory behaviors
### Treatment
- Psychotherapy – CBT
- [[Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors|SSRIs]] and lisdexamfetamine
- Lifestyle change: meal planning, emotional triggers address

### Complications
- Higher risk of metabolic syndrome + cardiovascular disease

### High-Yield Associations
- Most common in US
- 3.5% women, 2% men
