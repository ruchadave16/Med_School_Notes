#### Mechanism
- ↑ [[GABA]] activity at [[GABA A Receptors|GABA A receptor]] – sedation, amnesia, motor impairment
- ↓ [[glutamate]] at [[NMDA Receptors|NMDA receptors]] – depressant
- ↑ [[dopamine]] – pleasure
- ↑ [[Serotonin|5 HT]]3 receptors – pleasure, nausea
- ↑ [[Norepinephrine|norepinephrine]] – activating
- ↑ [[Endogenous Opioid Peptides|endogenous opioids]] – analgesis, stress relief, euphoria
#### Effects: [[Alcohol Intoxication]]
- Acute:
	- Euphoria, disinhibition
	- Neuro: **slurred speech**, impaired judgement, perception, **ataxia**, incoordination
	- Behavior changes: combative and destructive
	- Sedative: somnolence
	- **Hypoglycemia**
	- **Nystagmus**
- Give [[Benzodiazephines]] if seizure
	
>[!Warning]
>88,000 deaths a year in US
>- 77% related to binge drinking (4+ drinks in women, 5+ in men in 1 occasion)
>- 8.5% (12 month), 20% lifetime prevalence
#### Alcohol Dependence
- GABA A downregulation – lowers seizure threshold
- NMDA upregulation – excitatory neurotransmission

#### Treatment
- [[Naltrexone]]
	- Blocks opioid receptors so no rewarding effect
	- ↓ cravings
- [[Acamprosate]]
	- Weak NMDA antagonist and GABA A activator
	- Helps withdrawal symptoms
- [[Disulfiram]]
	- Inhibits aldehyde dehydrogenase so aldehyde accumulation when alcohol consumed
	- Nausea ↑ to deter drinking
	- AVOID in intoxicated people
		- 12 hr abstinence minimum
