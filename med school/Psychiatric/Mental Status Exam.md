- Appearance:
	- Grooming, hygiene, eye contact, unusual physical features
	
>[!Example] 
>- Young white female dressed in multiple layers of brightly colored mismatched blouses with excessive makeup and lipstick that is twice as large as her lips with at least 20 bracelets, rings and necklaces with multicolored unkept hair
>- Middle aged African American male unshaven for many days and matted gray hair dressed only in old ill-fitting plain cotton pants and a dirty old white undershirt with multiple holes and stains without any shoes covering his feet that have scaly dry skin

- Behavior
	- Psychomotor, abnormal movements, cooperation level
	
>[!Example]
>- The patient was very agitated as evidenced by his loud angry tone, taking my notes out of my hand and telling me to leave before he attacks me
>- The patient sits very quietly staring at the floor and almost unresponsive to questions
>- The patient was constantly moving from chair to chair and stands frequently, interrupting me constantly and saying inappropriate sexual comments

- Speech:
	- Rate of speech
	- Volume/tone
	- Rhythm
	- Fluency

>[!Example]
>shouting, pressured, rapid, loud/soft or barely audible, thick accent, improper use of words, switching between two languages, child like, robotic, monotonal, seductive, broken pattern

- Mood (stated emotional state) & affect (interpretation of mood):
	- Affect: happy, sad, or angry
	- Range: restricted, full, expanded
	- Intensity: low, flat, blunted, constricted, restricted, normal, exaggerated, dramatic
	- Congruency

>[!Example] 
>His mood is distressed and affect (facial/body expression) are congruent

- Sensorium (alert + oriented to person, place, time):
	- Concentration: Serial 7’s, spelling reversal, count backwards from 20 by 2

>[!Example] He is alert and orientated to person, place, and time, and situation.

- Intellectual Functioning: → Normal? What problems?
	- Memory: 3 objects immediate and delayed
	- Judgement: Pt understanding and plan to deal with illness
	- Insight: Pt understanding of illness and situation: 

> [!Example] 
“I will stay in the hospital so the aliens won’t get me” 
“So all I need to do is to take care of my Schizophrenia is take vitamins”
“So even though my medicines help me stabilize I do not have to take them after I leave the hospital”

- Thought Process (are they rational?):
	- Goal directed 
	-  Circumstantial
	- Tangential
	- Flight of Ideas
	- Loosening of Associations
	- Fragmented/Disorganized
	- Thought Blocking, Clanging, Echolalia, Perseveration

- Thought Content:  
	- Presence or absence of [[hallucinations]]: auditory, visual, tactile
	- Presence or absence of other symptoms: grand delusions
	- Suicidal ideations: plan, intent, passive or active thoughts, egosyntonic versus egodystonic, rehearsals, close or distant means
