- Binds to [[GABA A Receptors|GABA A receptor]] at site different than GABA and BDZ
#### Action
- Enhances [[GABA]] impact at low dose → increases Cl- channel opening duration so more hyperpolarization and inhibition
- Directly activates at high dose → PROFOUND CNS DEPRESSION
- Can also impact [[AMPA Receptors|AMPA receptor]]
#### Effects
- Sedative, *no longer used clinically*
- Anesthetic – Ultra short lasting as IV (thiopental)
- Anticonvulsant
	- Phenobarbital for generalized tonic-clonic seizures
#### Uses
- **Phenobarbital**: Anticonvulsant.
- **Thiopental**: Intravenous anesthetic
#### Administration

#### Dosing
Linear dose response curve, high dose causes quick death

#### Adverse Effects
- [[CNS]] depression, additive with other depressants
- Coma + respiratory depression
- Inducer of CYP causing fast metabolism of other drugs
#### Contraindication

>[!Warning] Barbiturate Intoxication
>- Supportive care – ensure ventilation
>- No specific antidote
