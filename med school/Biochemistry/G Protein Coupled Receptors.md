*Most common receptors – integral proteins with 7 membrane spanning alpha helices*

- Use G-protein to relay signal
- Secondary messenger inside cell 
- Can either be Gs, Gi, or Gq
#### cAMP
- G$\alpha$ comes off, $\beta$ and $\gamma$ stay on membrane
- G$\alpha$ exchanges GDP → GTP and gets activated
	- Gs activate **adenylyl cyclase** (ATP → cAMP)
	- Gi inhibits adenylyl cyclase (no cAMP formation)
- cAMP activates protein kinase A
- PKA phosphorylates CREB for [[transcription]]
- G$\alpha$ uses up GTP and is inactivated, joins

>[!Warning] cAMP degraded by [[cAMP phosphodiesterase]] into 5’-AMP

#### DAG (Diacylglycerol) and Inositol Triphosphate IP3
- Gq activates phospholipase C (PLC)
- PLC hydrolyzes PIP2 → DAG and IP3
- DAG stays on membrane and activates protein kinase C → activates downstream
- IP3 diffuses into cytoplasm → [[Endoplasmic Reticulum|ER]] and triggers release of $Ca^{2+}$ into cytoplasm
