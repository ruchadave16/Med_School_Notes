*Monosaccharide made of 6 C atoms*

- Can be covalently linked to form disaccharides and polysaccharides

Most important
 - [[Glucose]]
 - [[Galactose]]
 - [[Fructose]]
 - [[Sucrose]]
 - [[Lactose]]

#### Glucose Transport
Large hydrophilic so need transporters
- GLUTs and SGLTs
- Unitransporters allow into cell if concentration outside > cell
- Bidirectional transporters allow flow in and out

**GLUT1**
- Specific to glucose
- Across body
	- Erythrocytes, liver, pancreas, skeletal muscle, lungs, brain, heart, kidneys, adipose, intestines

**GLUT2**
- Nonspecific
- Pancreatic B-cells, small intestine, kidneys, *liver*
	- **Without GLUT2, liver CANNOT take in fructose**

**GLUT3**
- Glucose and galactose
- Brain and spermatozoa

**GLUT4**
- Indirectly sensitive to [[insulin]] to pull glucose in
- Skeletal muscle, heart, adipose

**GLUT5**
- Fructose
- Mainly intestine, some in kidneys, brain, skeletal muscle, adipose
- NONE in liver
