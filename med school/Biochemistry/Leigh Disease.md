### Etiology
- Nuclear DNA [[Mutations|mutation]] in [[NADH-Ubiquinone Oxidoreductase|Complex I]]
- [[Mitochondrial DNA]] mutation in [[ATP Synthase]]
### Pathophysiology
- Inability to maintain proton gradient of [[Oxidative Phosphorylation|ETC]] because electron mobility is lost

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
- Signs
	- Presents within 3-12 months
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
	- Elevated [[lactate]]
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
- Surgical
- Lifestyle

### Complications
- Fatal
- Developmental delay, progressive cognitive decline

### High-Yield Associations
- Rare disorder
	- 1:40,000 → nuclear [[DNA]] mutation
	- 1:100,000-1:140,000 → [[mitochondrial DNA]] mutation
