### Pathophysiology
- Normally during [[Purines|purine]] degradation, HGPRT combines PRPP with guanine or hypoxanthine to make GMP
	- No HGPRT means loss of [[hypoxanthine]] conversion 
	- Salvage cycle is lost
- Hypoxanthine oxidized 2 times to make uric acid
	- Excess [[uric acid]] produced

- AMP and GMP are inhibitors of GPAT (GPAT is the enzyme catalyzing step 1 of [[Purines|purine]] synthesis 
	- Because we have low AMP and GMP here, amplification of [[nucleotide synthesis]]

### Clinical Features

- High uric acid → crystals in joints (Gouty Arthritis)
- Crystals in kidneys → [[Urolithiasis]]
- Motor dysfunction
- Cognitive deficit
- Self-mutilation (biting lips and fingers)
