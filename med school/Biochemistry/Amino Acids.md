*Building block of [[General/Protein|proteins]]*

- Amino group (NH2)
- Carboxyl (COOH)
- Side chain (R)

![[Screenshot 2025-11-21 at 4.34.46 PM.png]]

- Can be essential or nonessential
#### Electronically Charged
- Charged side group at physiological pH (7.4)
- **Acidic** donate proton if pH > pKa
	- Apsartic Acid (Asp, D)
	- Glutamic Acid (Glu, E)
- **Basic** accept proton if pH < pKa
	- Histidine (His, H)
	- Lysine (Lys, L)
	- Arginine (Arg, R)
#### Polar Uncharged
- Water soluble but NOT charged at physiological pH
- Alcohols
	- Serine (Ser, S)
	- Threonine (Thr, T)
- Amides
	- Asparagine (Asn, N)
	- Glutamine (Gln, Q)
- Thiol
	- Cystine (Cys, C) – some deprotonated
#### Hydrophobic
- Alipathic
	- Alanine (Ala, A)
	- Valine (Val, V)
	- Leucine (Leu, L)
	- Isoleucine (Ile, I)
	- Methionine (Met, M)
- Aromantic
	- Phenylalanine (Phe, F)
	- Tryptophan (Trp, W)
	- Tyrosine (Tyr, Y)

>[!Info] Unique Amino Acids
>- Glycine – no side group, fully soluble
>- Proline – side chain = terminal amine
>- Cysteine and Selenocysteine – can do redox reactions and covalent bridges

#### [[Amino Acid Metabolism]]
Replenishment
- Some can be made by body
- Certain absorbed from diet
- Protein degradation to make AA

Depletion
- Diet: Not consumed at same rate as removal
- Protein synthesis uses up AA
- Excretion by oxidizing and making soluble to water
- Conversion to fat or glycogen
- Protein wasting (kidney can’t retain)
