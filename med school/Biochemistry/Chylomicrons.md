- FFAs, [[cholesterol]], fat-soluble vitamins all form micelles with bile salts that are soluble in intestine lumen
- These transport the lipids to brush border membrane where they’re absorbed

**Chylomicrons** = mixture of lipids absorbed by [[enterocytes]] changed in smooth [[Endoplasmic Reticulum|ER]] to complex lipids which are packed into droplets with layer of phospholipids, cholesterol, and apolipoprotein B-48

- Immature ones pick up apolipoproteins E and C-II when they reach blood
	- Apo C-II ACTIVATES **[[lipoprotein lipase]]** on surface of muscle and adipose
	- LPL hydrolyzes triglyceride and breaks these down
- As TAG content ↓, density ↑ and remnant taken by [[liver]] through [[apoE]] receptor

#### VLDL (Very Low Density Lipoproteins)
- TAG, cholesterol esters, and PL from liver
- Apolipoprotein: [[ApoB-100]]
#### [[LDL]] (Low Density Lipoproteins)
- 60% → liver through apoB-100 receptors
- 40% → extrahepatic tissues for steroid hormone synthesis
- Excess taken up by [[macrophages]] near arteries
	- Inflammation response → [[Atherosclerosis|atherosclerosis]]

>[!Info]
>Normal: <230 mg/dL
>Heterozygote: 300-600 mg/dL
>Homozygote: 600-1200 mg/dL
#### [[HDL]] (High Density Lipoproteins)
- Made in blood by adding lipid to apo A1
- Returns cholesterol to liver, reverse transport
