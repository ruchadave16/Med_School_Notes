- Can exist free as non-esterified (FFAs)
- Make up [[phospholipids]] and [[glycolipids]]
- Stored as esterified – [[Triacylglycerol|triacylglycerols]] (TAGs)
#### FFAs
- Found in all tissues
- Transferred from adipocytes on [[albumin]] in plasma to tissues
- In cell, [[Beta Oxidation]] after binding to CoA in mitochondria to give energy to tissues
- *RBCs do NOT use FFA because no mitochondria, brain also cannot*
#### Structure
- Even number of C atoms, commonly 16, 18, or 20
- Can be saturated or unsaturated
- Double bond almost ALWAYS cis position
#### Essential Fatty Acids
- Human body can’t add double bonds to methyl end
- Linoleic and linolenic **MUST** be gotten from diet (**ESSENTIAL fatty acids**)
- Linoleic acid
	- Nuts, olives, avocados, soybeans, sunflower seeds, corn oils
	- Lowers [[LDL]] but also lowers [[HDL]]
- Linolenic acid
	- Flaxseed, canola oil, walnuts
	- Lower BP, reduce CV mortality

>[!Clinical] 
>Deficiency = [[scaly dermatitis]] and other neuro conditions

#### [[Beta Oxidation|Fatty Acid Oxidation]]
Alternatives
- Peroxisomal $\beta$ oxidation instead of mitochondrial for VERY long chain fatty acids
	- Shortened version then moved to mitochondria

>[!Clinical]
>[[Zellweger Syndrome]] is when these very long chains accumulate in blood, fatal

- Peroxizomal $\alpha$-oxidation for branched fatty acids
- Microsomal $\omega$-oxidation in ER
	- Used when limitation to beta-oxidation
	- Methyl oxidation forms dicarboxylic acid
