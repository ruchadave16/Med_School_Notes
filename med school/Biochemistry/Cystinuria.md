### Etiology
- Genetic disorder (1:7000) for [[Methionine Metabolism|methionine metabolism]] (cysteine)

### Pathophysiology
- Defect of SLC3A1 and SLC7A9 transporters
- Buildup of cystine and other basic amino acids – can’t be reabsorbed to blood
	- Create stones

### Clinical Features
- Symptoms
	- Stones that dissolve in base
- Signs
- Variants

### Diagnosis
- Labs
- Imaging
- Criteria

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
	- Citrate or acetazolamide to make urine more basic to dissolve stones
- Surgical
- Lifestyle
	- Oral hydration
	- Reduce methionine intake

- Long-term
