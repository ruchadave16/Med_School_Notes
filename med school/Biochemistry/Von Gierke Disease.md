### Pathophysiology
- No glucose-6-phosphatase (last enzyme in [[gluconeogenesis]] and [[glycogenolysis]] to convert glucose-6-phosphate → glucose)
	- G6P accumulation → creates glycolytic and PPP productes
	- More glycolysis means ↑ pyruvate → ↑ lactate 
	- High pyruvate → [[Fatty Acid Synthesis]] 
	- High G6P → [[Pentose Phosphate Pathway|PPP]] → high purine created → uric acid degradation
- Glucose cannot be released from liver
- [[Glycogen]] stored in liver → liver issues
### Clinical Features
- VERY low fasting blood sugar
- [[Lactic acidosis]] – ↑ [[lactate]] in blood
- [[Hyperuricemia]] – ↑ [[uric acid]] in blood
- [[Hypertriglyeremia]] – ↑ [[triglycerides]] in blood
- Hepatomegaly
