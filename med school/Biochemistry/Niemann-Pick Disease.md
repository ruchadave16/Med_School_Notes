### Etiology
- Type A: Ashkenazi Jewish population
- Type B: southern Europe and northern African populations

- Defect in enzyme sphingomyelinase 
### Pathophysiology
- [[Sphingomyelin]] cannot be degraded by macrophages so it fills up inside forming globoid bodies and accumulates in CNS
- 
### Clinical Features
**Type A:**
- Onset first few weeks: vomiting, diarrhea
- Hepatosplenomegaly
- Psychomotor retardation
	- Slurred speech
- Loss of motor function
	- Unsteady gait
	- Difficulty swallowing
- Cognitive decline rapid
	- Dementia
	- Seizures
- Death 1.5-3 years
- Cherry red macula, opaque retina

**Type B:** 
- Hepatosplenomegaly
- No neuro components, but can see “cherry red” macula 
- NOT lethal
