#### Components
Embedded in *inner mitochondrial membrane*
ALL are transmembrane except Complex II
All push protons EXCEPT Complex II

- Complex I: [[NADH-Ubiquinone Oxidoreductase|NADH:Ubiquinone Oxidoreductase]]
- Complex II: [[Succinate Dehydrogenase]]
	- Part of [[Citric Acid Cycle|citric acid cycle]], NOT proton pump
- Complex III: Ubiquinone:Cytochrome C Oxidoreductase
	- Oxidizes ubiquinol → ubiquinone
	- Reduces ferricytochrome C → ferrocytochrome C
	- Proton pump
- Complex IV: Cytochrome C Oxidase
	- Oxidizes ferrocytochrome C → ferricytochrome C
	- Reduces oxygen → water **Final e- end**
	- Proton pump
- Complex V: [[ATP Synthase]]

Helpers
- [[Ubiquinone]] – $e^-$ from Complex I and II → III
- [[Cytochrome C]] – $e^-$ between Complex III and IV
#### Mechanism
- Protonmotive gradient orthogonal to electron potential gradient

![[Screenshot 2025-11-20 at 12.59.55 AM.png]]

- Oxidation and reduction uses energy to move protons across membrane outside
- These are allowed back into matrix through Complex V
- Generally $[H^+]$ in intermembrane > matrix – polarized
- We create a concentration gradient
- Complex V uses movement of protons to phosphorylate ADP to ATP
#### Inhibitors
- [[Rotenone]] = fish poison
	- Inhibits Complex I
	- Does not block whole chain
- Antimycin A inhibits Complex III
- [[Azide]], [[cyanide]], [[hydrogen sulfide]] inhibit Complex IV
- [[Oligomycin]] inhibits ATP synthase

- [[Hypoxia]] and [[Pseudohypoxia]]
