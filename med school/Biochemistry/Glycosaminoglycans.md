*Long, unbranched heteropolysaccharides (combination of mono and disaccharides*

- Found attached to proteins as [[proteoglycans]]
- Contain both amino and carboxyl functionalized sugars
- Monomers can be sulfated adding a negative charge

* Most have N-acetylated amino sugars which mask the + charge from amino (**generally negative charge**)
	* VERY hydrophilic
	* Repel each other due to like charges

![[Screenshot 2025-11-22 at 4.48.41 PM.png]]

#### Classifications
1) **Chondroitin 4- and 6-sulfates**
	- GlcUA (glucuronic acid) - GalNAc (N-acetylgalactosamine)
	- MOST common GAG found in body
	- Binds to [[collagen]] and [[hyaluronic acid]]
	- Cartilage, tendons, aorta, ligaments
2) **Keratan Sulfate**
	- Disaccharides mixed with other sugars
	- Found in cornea and loose connective tissue
3) **[[Hyaluronic Acid]]**
	- GlcUA (glucuronic acid) - GlcNAc (N-acetylglucosamine)
	- NOT sulfated
	- Shock absorber and lubricant
	- Found in joints, eyes
4) **Dermatan Sulfate**
	- IdoUA (Iduronic acid) - GalNAc (N-acetylgalactosamine)
	- Skin, blood vessels, heart valves
5) **Heparin**
	- GlcUA (glucuronic acid) - GlcN (Glucosamine)
	- [[Anticoagulant]]
6) **Heparan Sulfate**
	- Like heparin with less sulfates and more acetyls
	- Part of ECM
#### Degradation
- Broken into amino sugars by acid hydrolases in [[Lysosome|lysosomes]] after phagocytosis
	- Multiple need to work together
