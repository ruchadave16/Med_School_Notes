- Ammonium made from deamination of glutamate to make a-ketoglutamate ([[amino acid degradation]])
	- NH4+ in equilibrium with NH3 and H+

To remove NH3, body uses [[urea]]

#### Steps
*Mitochondria*
1) Carbamoyl phosphate made from ammonia, CO2, 2 ATP by **carbamoyl phosphate synthetase 1** (CPS-1)
2) [[Acetyl CoA]] added to glutamate by N-acetylglutamate Synthase (NAGS)
	- N-acetylglutamate is **activator** for CPS-1 
	- Signals to mitochondria that high glutamate exists 
3) Carbamoyl phosphate combined with ornithine by **ornithine transcarbamoylase** to make citrulline

*Cytosol*
1) Citrulline combined with aspartate → argininosuccinate
2) Argininosuccinate cleaved to make fumarate and arginine
3) Head group of arginine removed to make urea and ornithine
4) Ornithine → mitochondria (recycled)

#### Products
- Carbamoyl phosphate – either conjugated to ornithine OR spontaneously becomes orotate (starting material for [[pyrimidine]])

>[!Clinical]
>- Loss of each enzyme makes a new disease
>- Concentrations are a KEY to knowing where in cycle problem is
>
>**Diseases**
>- [[CPS1 Deficiency]]
>	- Most severe, most rare
>	- Hyperammonemia at birth → can’t make carbamoyl phosphate so none is captured
>	- Glutamine (↑), citrulline (↓), arginine (↓)
>	- Orotic acid
>- [[NAGS Deficiency]]
>	- Very rare
>	- NAGS activates CPS1
>	- Glutamine (↑), citrulline (↓), arginine (↓)
>- [[OTC Deficiency]]
>	- Most common urea cycle disorder
>	- X-linked so severe for males, but only 15% of females develop hyperammonemic episodes
>	- Glutamine (↑), citrulline (↓), arginine (↓)
>- [[Argininosuccinic Aciduria]]
>	- Less common than X-Linked but not uncommon
>	- ASS1 Deficiency so no argininosuccinate or parts downstream to this
>	- Glutamine (No change), citrulline (↑↑), arginine (↓)

>[!Info]
>Orotic acid = indicator
>- Diseases related to CPS1 or NAGS don’t show change to orotic acid
>- Diseases related to rest of urea cycle DO show change to orotic acid
>
>Arginine is ALWAYS low in urea cycle diseases except arginase deficiency
>- Arginase will convert back to ornithine
>- In arginase deficiency, VERY high arginine
>  
>  Argininosuccinate
>- in ASS1 deficiency, absent
>- In ASL deficiency, elevated
>- Argininosuccinate lyase removes succinyl group to make arginine, so if ASL deficient, argininosuccinate high
>
>Citrulline
>- Formed immediately after carbamoyl phosphate is made
> - if CPS1 active but downstream down, then citrulline high
> - If CPS1, NAGS, or OTC gone, then citrulline low
>   
>   Glutamine
> - Substrate for glutaminase that makes ammonium
>   - If CSP1, NAGS, or OTC gone, then - feedback (high ammonium or downstream, so glutamine increases)

>[!Info] Treatment
>Low protein diet + [[phenylbutyrate]] – reacts with glutamine to remove from blood
