1) Transamination – NH2 from amino acid to ketoacid
2) Redox Deamination – Elimination of NH3 from [[glutamate]] to make ammonia and ketoacid

![[Pasted image 20251203230852.png]]
#### Transamination

![[Screenshot 2025-11-21 at 5.20.54 PM.png]]

- Alanine aminotransaminase (ALT)
	- Ala-NH2 + aKG → Glu-NH2 + Pyr
- Aspartate aminotransaminase (AST)
	- OxoAc + Glu-NH2 → Asp-NH2 + aKG

- Both enzymes take amino acid and ketoacid, and swap amino group
- **Reversible, used for [[amino acid synthesis]] for nonessential AA**
- Require [[Vitamin B6]] to operate
#### Redox Deamination

![[Screenshot 2025-11-21 at 5.24.24 PM.png]]

- ONLY [[glutamate]] goes under oxidative deamination
- Glutamate dehydrogenase oxidizes glutamate → $\alpha$-ketoglutarate 
- Releases NH3
- Produces [[NADPH]]
- Reversible
#### Glutamate Synthetase

![[Screenshot 2025-11-21 at 5.26.33 PM.png]]

- Glutamine synthetase adds ammonia to glutamate to make glutamine
- Stores NH3
#### [[Citric Acid Cycle]]
- All AA can be broken down into intermediates of citric acid cycle
- Most are either glucogenic or ketogenic 
- ONLY leucine and lysine are ONLY ketogenic
