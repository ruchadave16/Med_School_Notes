#### Regulation
Irreversible enzymes
1) [[Citrate synthase]]
	- Converts oxaloacetate. to citrate in [[Pyruvate Dehydrogenase Complex|pyruvate dehydrogenase complex]]
	- Citrate inhibits this enzyme
	- Citrate ALSO inhibits [[Phosphofructokinase 1|PFK-1]]

2) Isocitrate Dehydrogenase
	- Activators: ADP, Ca2+
	- Inhibitors: ATP, NADH
	- Converts isocitrate → $\alpha$-ketoglutarate

3) a-ketoglutarate Dehydrogenase
	- Activators: Ca2+
	- Inhibitors: NADH, Succinyl CoA
	- Converts $\alpha$-ketoglutarate → succinyl CoA

#### Reactions
- Oxidative Decarboxylation
	- Isocitrate dehydrogenase and $\alpha$-ketoglutarate dehydrogenase make $CO_2$ as a result of rxn
		- Energy from breaking COOH used to convert NAD+ to NADH
	- Source = Acetyl CoA

- Reduction-Oxidation
	- [[Succinate dehydrogenase]] (**Mitochondrial Complex II**)  
		- FAD → FADH2
	- [[Malate dehydrogenase]]
		- NAD+ → NADH

- Phosphorylation
	- [[Succinate Thiokinase]]
		- Produces ATP or GTP
