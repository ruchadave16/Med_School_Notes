*Pathway that makes [[glucose]] from tricarboxylic acid cycle, lactate, or glycerol*

- Main way to maintain sugar during fasting
- Happens in liver and kidneys
#### Inputs
- 2 pyruvate, 4 ATP = 1 glucose
- 2 GTP by PEPCK
- 2 glycerol, 2 ATP = 1 glucose
All inputs must be phosphorylated by ATP: Alanine, lactate, or glycerol

#### Regulation
- [[Pyruvate Kinase|Pyruvate kinase]] (converts PEP to pyruvate)
	- [[Glucagon]] activates protein kinase A which DEACTIVATES protein kinase by phosphorylating it
- [[Pyruvate carboxylase]] (converts pyruvate directly to oxaloacetate)
	- [[Acetyl CoA]] activates (says cell has enough energy so backwards)
	- ADP inhibits (says cell needs more energy so instead makes acetyl CoA
- [[PEPCK]] (converts oxaloacetate → PEP)
	- ADP and insulin inhibit
	- Upregulation: glucagon and cortisol
	- Synthesis: **[[glucagon]]**
- Fructose 1,6 Bisphosphatase (converts fructose 1,6 bisphosphate to fructose-6-phosphate) – opposite of PFK-1
	- Activation: Citrate, glucagon
	- Inhibition: AMP, F-2,6BP
- Glucose-6-phosphatase (converts glucose-6-phosphate to glucose) – opposite of hexokinase
	- Activation: Glucagon
