*Circular [[DNA]] in [[mitochondria]]l matrix*

Codes for [[Oxidative Phosphorylation|ETC]] protein subunits, small and large [[rRNA]], and 22 [[tRNA]]s

**Maternally inherited**

Heteroplasmy – each cell has population of mtDNA, so some cells have have mutations that other cells don’t

#### Pedigree

![[Screenshot 2025-12-07 at 7.06.03 PM.png]]

- Only females can transmit diseases
- Oftentimes incomplete dominance

>[!Clinical]
>[[Leber Hereditary Optic Neuropathy]] (LHON)
>- Bilateral, painless vision failure
>- Mutations in MT-ND1, MT-ND4, MT-ND
>  
>[[Myoclonic Epilepsy with Ragged Red Fibers]] (MERRF)
>- Myoclonus epilepsy, ataxia, muscle weakness, and dementia
>- Mutations in MT-TK, tRNA for lysine. A-to-G transition at nucleotide 8344.
>  
>[[Pearsons Syndrome]]
>- Sideroblastic anemia and exocrine pancrease dysfunction
>- Mitochondrial DNA Deletion
>  
>[[Kearns Sayre Syndrome]]
>- Pigmented retinopathy
>- mDNA deletion
>  
>[[MELAS]] (Mitochondrial Encephalopathy, Lactic Acidosis and Stroke-like Episodes)
>- Mitochondrial encephalopathy
>- Lactic acidosis
>- Stroke-like episodes
>- Headaches, seizures, muscle weakness possible
>- Point mutation in the tRNA leucine gene of mitochondrial DNA A-to-G transition at nucleotide 3243
