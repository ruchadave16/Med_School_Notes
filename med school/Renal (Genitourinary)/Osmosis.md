*Movement of water across semi-permeable membrane due to unequal concentration of solute*

Movement from LOW solute concentration to HIGH solute concentration due to **osmotic pressure**

#### Terminology
- Total Osmotic Pressure – Pressure of solution if separated from pure water by solute impermeable membrane
- Effective Osmotic Pressure – Difference across membrane between 2 solutions
- Isoosmotic – 2 solutions with same osmolarity (reference to plasma)
- Hypo vs Hyper osmotic (lower or higher than [[plasma]])


99% + due to crystalloids
- Crystalloid Osmotic Pressure = 5492 mmHg
Rest due to proteins
- Colloid Osmotic Pressure ([[Oncotic Pressure]])

#### Interstitial vs Plasma
- Because capillaries let crystalloids pass, effective pressure = oncotic pressure between plasma and interstitial fluid
- Generally ↑ movement from ISF → PV (countered by hydrostatic pressure in [[Transcapillary Fluid Exchange|vessels]])

#### Extracellular vs Intercellular
- Changes in [[ECF]] osmolarity due to osmotic pressure change between ECF and ICF
- $\Delta$ in ECF Na+ drives this (Primarily due to [[Vasopressin|ADH]] and [[thirst]])
