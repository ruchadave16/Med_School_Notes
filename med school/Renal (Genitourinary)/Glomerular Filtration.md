- Little filtration of solutes > 70,000 weight (filtrate generally protein free)

Basement membrane and epithelial layer of [[Renal Corpuscle|glomerulus]] are structural barriers for proteins
Fixed negative [[glycoproteins]] in all 3 layers so **repel** negative-proteins in plasma

![[Screenshot 2025-09-15 at 9.05.15 PM.png]]

#### Dynamics
Generally Glomerular Filtration Rate (GFR) = 125 mL/min
- 20% of renal **plasma** flow ([[Renal Plasma Flow|RPF]]) 

GFR and [[RBF]] stay constant due to [[myogenic mechanism]] and [[tubuloglomerular feedback mechanism]]
- Measure through [[Renal Clearance|renal clearance]]

>[!Info] Filtration Fraction = $\frac{GFR}{RPF}$
>
>$GFR = K_f [(P_{GC} - P_{BS}) - (\pi_{GC} - \pi_{BS})]$
>where $K_f$ = permeability
>P and $\pi$ are hydrostatic and oncotic pressure
>
>**Similar to [[Transcapillary Fluid Exchange]] equation

Generally
- Oncotic pressure of Bowman’s Space = 0 because no filtration of proteins into tubule

![[Screenshot 2025-09-15 at 9.07.25 PM.png]]

Net Filtration Pressure (NFP) = sum of afferent and efferent end pressures
- Oncotic pressure increases because ↑↑ protein-free fluid filtered out of capillaries so protein concentration ↑ towards efferent end
- This means filtration afferent > efferent
- *** NEVER reverse absorption, always filtered out

#### Changes to Rate
1) Capillary Hydrostatic Pressure
	- ↑ means GFR ↑
	- Usually ↑ if $\Delta$ in arterial pressure OR resistance of afferent or efferent arterioles

![[Screenshot 2025-09-15 at 9.26.05 PM.png]]

2) Bowman’s Capsule Hydrostatic Pressure
	- ↑ means ↓ GFR (ureteral or tubular obstruction)

3) Plasma Oncotic Pressure
	- ↑ means ↓ GFR – dehydration
	- ↓ means ↑ GFR – malnutrition, cirrhosis (lower protein synthensis or higher excretion of proteins)

4) Glomerular Membrane Permeability
	- ↑ means ↑ GFR – renal disease
	- ↓ means ↓ GFR – inflammation of glomerulus

5) Glomerular SA 
	- ↓ means ↓ GFR – [[mesangial cells]] contract due to [[angiotensin II]] or [[vasopressin]]

6) Renal Blood Flow
	- ↑ means ↑ GFR
	- $\pi$GC increases more slowly so more net filtration
