Interstitial osmolarity of medulla has increasing osmolarity

![[Pasted image 20250918232450.png]]
**Thin Descending Limb**
- Impermeable to NaCl, low permeability of [[urea]], freely permeable to water
- Fluid from [[Proximal Convoluted Tubule|proximal convoluted tubule]] enters increased osmolarity so water flows out of tubule
- Tubular fluid matches interstitial osmolarity
- At tip, 1200 mOsm/L

**Thin Ascending Limb**
- Impermeable to water, moderately permeable by urea, highly permeable to NaCl
- NaCl in tubule > interstitial so NaCl diffuses out
- [[Urea]] interstitial > tubule so urea *secreted* into tubule
- ** NaCl moves faster so net loss of solute

**Thick Ascending Limb**
- Impermeable to water and urea, Transports Na, K, 2Cl out of tubule (active transport)
- Can be inhibited by [[furosemide]]
- Osmolarity at end ~100-120 mOsm/L
