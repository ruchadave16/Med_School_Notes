#### Components
 3 part system
 1) Solvent (water) – 50-70% weight
 2) Solute (ions + non-electrolytes) – sums with solvent = lean body mass
 3) Particulate matter (fat) – about 10% water by weight

Total Body Water (TBW) = 60% body weight
- Intracellular Fluid (ICF) = 40% body weight
- Extracellular Fluid ([[ECF]]) = 20% body weight
	- Interstitial Fluid (ISF) = 15% body weight
	- Plasma Volume (PV) = 5% body weight

Blood Volume = PV + ICF (blood cells) = 8% body weight

- Plasma (High Na, Cl, HCO3) (Low K) (moderate protein)
- ISF – 1/8 protein, same as plasma otherwise
- ICF – (High K, Mg, PO4) (Low Na, Cl, HCO3) (HIGH protein)
#### Measuring Components
[[Dye-Dilution Method]] – measure dye in [[urine]]

- TBW – Deuterium oxide $D_2O$, tritiated water, antipyrine
	- Freely permeable by cells
	- 2 hrs mixing time
- [[ECF]] – No ideal substance (either overestimate by entering cells or don’t distribute and underestimate)
	- Best = inulin, radiolabeled sulfate
	- 30 min mixing time
- ICF – (TBW - ECF = ICF)
- PV – Labeled [[albumin]] or Evans Blue dye (binds to plasma proteins)
	- 10 min mixing time
- ISF – (ISF = ECF - PV)
- BV – Cr-51 labeled RBC distribuiton (10 min) or PV determination and get hemocrit

#### Input and Output
Intake
- 2600 mL/day
- Mainly GI but some oxidative water

Output
- 2600 mL/day
- Insensible water loss (skin + lungs) – solute-free leakage through skin or exhaled with air
- Sweat – glandular secretion
- Feces – Small amount of water loss
- Urine – abt 1500 mL/day with water and solute adjustment
