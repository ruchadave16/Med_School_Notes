*Paired bean-shaped organs in [[Posterior Abdominal Wall|posterior abdominal wall]]*
Located in retroperitoneal space
- R at L1-L3, L at T12-L2
##### Functions
- Maintain volume and composition of [[body fluids]]
- [[Renal Tubular Transport|Renal tubular transport]]
##### Contents

##### External Features
- Covered by dense irregular [[connective tissue]] capsule
- Renal Sinus – Space taken by renal pelvis, vessels, nerves, and fat
- Renal Hilum – Entrance to renal sinus
- Renal Pelvis – Flat superior end of [[Ureters|ureter]]
	- Urine collects here until “pacemaker muscle” initiates peristaltic wave

##### Internal Features
**[[Nephron]]** – functional subunit of kidney

- Outer cortex
	- [[Renal Corpuscle|Renal corpuscles]] (glomeruli)
	- [[Proximal Convoluted Tubule|Proximal convoluted tubules]]
	- [[Distal convoluted tubules]]
	- Medullary rays – straight tubules collect into cortex
- Inner medulla
	- [[Loop of Henle]]
	- [[Collecting ducts]]
		- Merge at renal papillae into [[ducts of Bellini]] → minor calyxes → major calyxes → renal pelvis → [[Ureters|ureter]]
		- [[Collecting Duct Transport|Collecting duct transport]]
	- Renal columns – Cortical tissue (with glomeruli) between pyramids
	- Vasa recta
	- Renal pyramids with bases at cortex and apex (renal papilla) to inside towards renal sinus

##### Blood Supply

![[Pasted image 20250913164307.png]]

- L and R Renal artery (1 from abdominal [[aorta]], posterior to IVC)
- Segmental arteries – anterior and posterior from renal artery before hilum
- Interlobar arteries – in hilum and penetrate medulla before medullary pyramids
- Arcuate arteries – along border between cortex and medulla
- Interlobular arteries – in cortex between medullary rays
- Afferent arterioles – each to [[Renal Corpuscle|glomerulus]]
- Glomerular capillaries – filter blood in [[Renal Corpuscle|glomerulus]]
- Efferent arteriole – carry blood away from glomerulus
	- From cortical glomeruli, form peritubular capillaries bringing away from proximal and distal tubules, converging into veins of peripheral cortex → interlobular veins
	- Endothelial cells of peritubular capillaries make [[erythropoeitin]] to ↓ blood oxygen
	- From juxtamedullary glomeruli, form vasa recta → medulla → interlobular veins → arcuate veins → interlobar → renal vein (anterior to aorta, inferior to SMA) → IVC
