*Reflex that tells bladder to empty*

Reflex causes rise in bladder pressure
- Cystometrogram tells bladder volume

↑ bladder volume → bladder wall stretch receptors → threshold for reflex → afferent pelvic nerves send signals to sacral cord → efferent pelvic nerves stimulated by cord reflex → bladder contracts and internal sphincter dilation → further feeds stretch receptors
- After some time, bladder fatigues and pressure to normal
- If not empty, reflex starts again
#### Innervation
###### [[Urinary Bladder|Bladder]] Filling
- Sympathetic inhibits [[detrusor muscle]] and stimulates internal urethral sphincter to contract
- [[Pudendal Nerve|Pudendal n.]] stimulates external urethral sphincter
###### Bladder Emptying
- Reflex
	- Stretch by pelvic splanchnic (parasymphathetic) → stimulates detrusor and relaxes internal urethral sphincter
- Conscious
	- Brain → spinal cord → [[Pudendal Nerve|pudendal n.]] → inhibits external urethral sphincter

>[!Clinical]
>[[Atonic Bladder]] – sensory fiber destruction of bladder so no information of fullness to brain → no micturition reflex
>- Bladder fills to be too large and urine leaks
>- Can voluntarily empty if push on abdomen to open sphincters
>  
>[[Automatic Bladder]] – Spinal cord section above sacral – micturition can happen but not controlled by CNS so no information on bladder fullness
>- Incontinence at lower bladder volume
>
