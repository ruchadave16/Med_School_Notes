6 major types with respect to water and sodium
- Initial change in water and sodium ALWAYS in [[ECF]]
- Resulting change can change [[osmosis|osmotic equilibrium]] between ECF and ICF (sodium cannot move here so change is water)

Described using [[Darrow-Yannet Diagrams]] (ICF condition, ECF state)
#### Hypertonic Contraction
*Loss of water in excess of sodium* (sweating, ↓ water intake)

![[Pasted image 20250915011124.png]]

- Water lost so same amount of solute in ↓ V increases osmolarity
- To re-establish, water moves into ECF from ICF

#### Isotonic Contraction
*Loss of water and salt* (GI fluid loss – diarrhea)

![[Pasted image 20250915011437.png]]

- ECF contraction is isotonic so no change to osmolarity
- No fluid change

#### Hypotonic Contraction
*Loss of sodium in excess of water* (Adrenal insufficiency)

![[Pasted image 20250915011600.png]]

- Loss of isotonic fluid and replacement with hypotonic (water)
- Results in hypotonic ECF
- Water moves into ICF, causing ECF to still contract and ICF to become hypotonic

#### Hypertonic Expansion
*Excess sodium intake* (Salt poisoning, sea water drinking)

![[Pasted image 20250915011801.png]]

- Intake of Na causes ECF to be hypertonic
- Water moves out of ICF into ECF, causing it to expand

#### Isotonic Expansion
*Excess of sodium and water in isotonic contraction* (edema, saline infusion)

![[Pasted image 20250915011915.png]]

- ECF volume increase but tonicity stays same

#### Hypotonic Expansion
*Excess of water* (water retention, ↑ ADH)

![[Pasted image 20250915012023.png]]

- Increase of water intake means ECF is hypotonic
- Water enters ICF
