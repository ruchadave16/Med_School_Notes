*Carry [[urine]] from [[kidneys]] to [[Urinary Bladder|bladder]]*

Renal pelvis (peristaltic wave) → ureters (in boluses of fluid) → bladder
##### Boundaries
Retroperitoneal
- Travel inferiorly and over pelvic brim along walls of pelvis
- Females: passes medial and then inferior to uterine a. to enter bladder
- Males: passes posterolateral to [[ductus deferens]] to enter bladder above seminal gland
Mainly superior to pelvic pain line

##### Internal Features
Stellate shaped lumen with urothelium
- Inner longitudinal, outer circular [[smooth muscle]] (distal has external longitudinal too)
- Fibrous [[connective tissue]] adventitia with blood vessels + nerves

>[!Clinical] 
>[[Kidney Stones]] can block urine flow to bladder
>Sites = Ureteropelvic junction (renal pelvis + ureter) or pelvic brim (where ureter crosses iliac vessels) or uretovesical junction (ureter + bladder)
>
>- Sign = visceral pain (ipsilateral to lower quadrant or inguinal)
##### Blood Supply
- Ipsilateral from ureteric branches of major blood vessels
##### Innervation
- Sympathetic and parasympathetic + intramural plexus
	- Sympathetic ↓ peristalsis
	- Parasympathetic ↑ peristalsis
- Afferent (pain) follows sympathetic retrograde
- Referred pain to ipsilateral inguinal region
