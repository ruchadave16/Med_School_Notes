Same process as normal up till [[Collecting Duct Transport|collecting duct transport]]
- low [[Vasopressin|ADH]] so water impermeable collecting duct
- Reabsorption of NaCl and tubular osmolarity cannot match interstitial (much higher)

#### [[Urea]] Role in Dilution
1) 1/2 of inner medullary interstitial osmolarity contributor
2) Accounts for gradient needed for NaCl to move out of thin ascending limb

Without [[Vasopressin|ADH]], whole collecting duct is urea-impermeable
- Urea cant diffuse out of duct so washed out of interstitium
- Gradient reduced, BUT duct is not water permeable anyways so water doesn’t flow out and urine stays dilute
With ADH, inner medullary part IS permeable
- Urea concentrates in early + middle collecting duct because water flowing out
- At inner medullary duct, urea diffuses into interstitium
	- Continues to provide gradient for NaCl to diffuse out of [[Loop of Henle Transport|thin ascending limb]]

>[!Clinical]
>In severe [[malnutrition]], plasma urea levels low
>- Urea can’t create osmotic graident
>- Urine WON’T concentrate
