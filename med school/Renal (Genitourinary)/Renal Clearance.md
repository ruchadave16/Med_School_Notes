*Amount of [[plasma]] from which [[Kidneys|kidney]] removes substance per time*

>[!Info] Clearance = $\frac{U_x V}{P_x}$
>$U_x$ = urine concentration of X
>V = urine flow
>$P_x$ = arterial plasma concentration of X
>
>If clearance = 70 mL/min, this means equivalent to complete removal of object from 70 mL of plasma

- If clearance < inulin, net reabsorption
- If clearance > inulin, net secretion

>[!Clinical]
>Inulin – 125 (No transport, all filtered load excreted so equals GFR)
>Glucose – 0 (Complete reabsorption)
>Urea – 70 (Reabsorbed in [[Proximal Convoluted Tubule|proximal convoluted tubule]], secreted in [[Loop of Henle]], reabsorbed at end of [[collecting duct]] so NET reabsorption)
>Creatine – 150 (No reabsorption, some secretion)
>PAH – 700 (No reabsorption, HIGH secretion so matches [[Renal Plasma Flow|RPF]])
#### Measurement of GFR
Substance must meet criteria – [[inulin]]
- Freely filtered from [[Renal Corpuscle|glomerulus]]
- Not reabsorbed by tubule
- Not secreted by tubule
- Not made, degraded, or stored by kidney
- Doesn’t affect GFR
- Nontoxic

>[!Info]
>Inulin filtered = inulin excreted
>Filtered / time = GFR $*$ Pin
>Excreted = Uin $*$ V
>
>GFR $*$ Pin = Uin $*$ V
>GFR = Uin * V / Pin = Cin

**Commonly use [[creatine]] in practice**
- Overestimates a bit since it is secreted in small amounts by tubules
- Pcr = estimate of renal function – shouldn’t be increased unless significant change to GFR (50% or more)

#### Osmolar Clearance and Free Water Clearance
>[!Info]
>V = $C_{osm} + C_{H2O}$
>$C_{osm} = \frac{U_{osm}*V}{P_{osm}}$ – water needed to excrete iso-osmotic urine

Free water clearance tells rate of kidney water reabsorption / excretion
- Negative value means net reabsorption $T_{CH2O}$
