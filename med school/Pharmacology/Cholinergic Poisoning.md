### Etiology
*Medical emergency from ↑ cholinergic excess*
- [[Cholinesterase Inhibitors|AchE inhibitors]] insecticides ([[organophosphates]])
	- FARMERS
- Wild mushroom
- Sarin
### Pathophysiology
- Absorbed through skin, respiratory, GI
- Irreversible inhibition of [[Acetylcholinesterase|AchE]] → ↑ [[Ach]] levels → activation of [[Muscarinic Receptors|muscarinic receptors]] and [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]]
### Clinical Features
- DUMBBELSS
	- Diarrhea
	- Urination
	- **Miosis**, blurred vision
	- Bradycardia
	- Emesis
	- Lacrimation
	- Salivation
	- Sweating
- Parathion has garlic like or petrol like smell
	- Can cause blue saliva or mucosa
- Sarin = odorless and tasteless
- **Cramping, diarrhea, vomiting**
- **↑ sweating, ↑ salivation**
- **Muscle twitching, spasms**

### Treatment
- Patient decontamination
- Secure airways, oxygen therapy, ECG
- Measure erythrocyte cholinesterase activity: Will see ↓ AchE activity
- [[Atropine]]
	- Reverses CNS muscarinic effect
	- Apply repeated dose until effect signs are seen
- [[Pralidoxime|Pralidoxime]], obidoxime
	- Reverse peripheral symptoms of organophosphate poisoning
	- Doesn’t work in CNS or after aging
	- **Only give after atropine**
- Control fever with cooling blankets
- Manage seizures with [[Diazepam]]
- **[[Physostigmine]]** if CNS impact is high
- If poisoning with quaternary, treat with **[[Neostigmine]]** and maintain hypotension with **[[Phenylephrine]]**
### Complications

**Contraindications**
- [[Glaucoma]]: can worsen intraocular pressure
- History of prostatic hyperplasia: can cause urinary retention
