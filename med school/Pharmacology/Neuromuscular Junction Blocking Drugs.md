*Used alongside [[anaesthetics]] for skeletal muscle relaxation*

>[!Note]
>-cur- = NMJ blockers
#### Nondepolarizing Blockers
- Antagonist to [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]] (Nm)
- Stop Ach from binding
- No depolarization, can be reversed by [[Cholinesterase Inhibitors|AchE inhibitors]]
- [[Tubocurarine]]
	- Small dose = competitive inhibition, can be reversed
	- Large dose = blocks ion channel pore, less reversible
	- Highly polar, not absorbed orally, low CNS entry
- Adverse Effect
	- Hypotension from tubocurarine, premedication with antihistamine can attenuate this
	- Hypotension from pancuronium, atracurium, and mivacurium
	- Vecuronium, cisatracurium, and rocuronium have minimal CV effects
- Reversal
	- [[Cholinesterase Inhibitors|AchE inhibitors]]
	- Can prevent bradycardia through atropine or glycopyrrolate
	- [[Sugammadex]] reverses blocking rapidly

>[!Info] Types of Surgeries
>Long Acting: [[D tubocurarine]], [[pancuronium]]
>Intermediate Acting: [[Rocuronium]], [[vecuronium]], [[atracurium]] (metabolized to [[laudanosine]], can enter CNS causing seizures), [[cisatracurium]] (does NOT form laudanosine, is used in clinical use)
>Short Acting: [[Mivacurium]]

#### Depolarizing Blockers
- Agonist to [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]]
- Cause persistent depolarization → desensitization
	- Initially muscle twitches
	- Eventually paralysis
- [[Succinylcholine]]
	- Shortest duration bcz ↑↑ hydrolysis by BuChE
	- Rare genetic variants of BuChE can slow metabolism → diagnostic test = dibucaine number
