>[!Info] **EC50** – $[drug]$ to produce 50% of response
#### Graded Response
- % of maximum response
- Dose related to **intensity** of response

![[Screenshot 2025-11-19 at 2.21.46 PM.png]]
**Common Characteristics**
- X-axis = log scale of concentration of drug
- Y-axis = % response
- Sigmoid shape
- *Threshold* – lowest dose where measurable response seen
- *Slope* – rate of increase of response when increasing dose
- *Maximum asymptote* – highest point on curve showing maximum response

#### Quantal Response
- All or none effect
- Population studies
- % of individuals who respond
- Dose related to **frequency** of response

![[Screenshot 2025-11-19 at 3.42.59 PM.png]]

**Common Characteristics**
- Cumulative vs Normal frequency distribution
- Useful for Analgesic Efficacy – % patients getting pain relief, Lethal dose

#### Therapeutic Effect and Lethal Effect
- Median effective dose ($ED50$) = dose to get specific effect in 50% of population
- Median lethal dose ($LD50$) = dose that causes death in 50% of test population
- Therapeutic index (TI) = ratio of drug safety = LD50 / ED50
- Therapeutic ratio (TR) = TD50 / ED 50 where TD50 = dose causing toxic effect in 50% population
- Margin of Safety: ED99/LD1, or TD1
- Certain Safety Factor: LD1/ED99

![[Screenshot 2025-11-19 at 3.46.44 PM.png]]
#### Therapeutic Window
- Range of drug concentrations that provide desired effect without causing adverse effects
- ↑↑ therapeutic index = wider safety margin so effect with lower risk of toxicity
