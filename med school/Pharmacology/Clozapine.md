#### Action
- [[Second Generation Antipsychotics|Atypical antipsychotic]], first introduced
#### Metabolism
- [[CYP1A2]] converts to inactive
- [[Nicotine]] (smoking) induces CYP1A2 so significantly lowers drug levels
	- Cessation reverses induction in ~8 days
- CYP1A2 inhibitors can increase drug levels in body
#### Uses
- Minimal extrapyramidal side effects
- Effective for treatment resistant [[Schizophrenia|schizophrenia]]

#### Adverse Effects
- [[Agranulocytosis]] (1-2%)
	- Reversible upon discontinuation
	- Onset 6-18 week of therapy
	- NEED regular blood count monitoring
- CV: hypotension, cardiomyopathy
- Seizure risk based on dose
#### Contraindication
- [[Nicotine]]
