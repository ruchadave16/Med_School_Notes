#### Action (Same as [[Tyramine]])
- Increase release of [[dopamine]] and [[Norepinephrine|norepinephrine]]
- Resemble [[Norepinephrine|norepinephrine]], enter terminal through *Uptake 1* transporters
- Enter vesicles through [[VMAT]], displacing NE
- Norepinephrine → cytoplasm
	- Degraded by [[MAO]]
	- Released to synaptic cleft
- Calcium is NOT required to release this
#### Signs
- Strong stimulant of [[CNS]]
	- ↑ release of [[norepinephrine]], [[dopamine]], [[serotonin]]
	- Wakefulness, alertness, confidence ↑
	- ↓ fatigue and appetite
	- **agitation, paranoia**
- **Mydriasis**,
- Peripheral
	- BP ↑, bradycardia but also tachycardia
	- Smooth muscle acts like sympathomimetic drugs

#### Adverse Effects
**Acute Toxicity**
- Rebound fatigue and depression
- CV collapse, ↑ sweating, dry mouth, vomiting, abdominal pain
- Severe: convulsions, coma, cerebral hemorrhage

Long term use can cause tolerance and dependance

**Treatment**
- Acidify urine to increase drug excretion
- Sedatives against agitation
- Manage BP
#### Withdrawal
- Hypersomnia
- ↑ appetite
- Severe depression
#### Contraindication
