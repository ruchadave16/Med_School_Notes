2 main methods:
- Drug Excretion
- [[Drug Metabolism]]
#### Drug Excretion
- [[Kidneys]]
	- [[Glomerular Filtration]]
	- [[Renal Tubular Transport]] – active tubular secretion
	- Passive tubular reabsorption
		- Alkaline urine → excretion of weak acids, lower excretion of weak base
- [[Liver]] / [[Bile]]
	- Glucuronides excreted through bile → intestines where hydrolyzed by microflora
	- Drug is reabsorbed (**enterohepatic recycling**)
	- This prolongs presence and effects of drug
- [[Lungs]]
	- Volatile / gas anaesthetics
###### First-Order Kinetics (Most [[Drug|drugs]])
- Constant proportion eliminated per unit time
- Rate of elimination depends on drug concentration
- Curved concentration-time graph
- Half life = constant
###### Zero-Order Kinetics
- Constant amount eliminated per unit time
- Rate DOES not depend on drug concentration
- Linear concentration-time graph
- 
#### Drug Clearance
Rate of elimination normalized to concentration in biological fluid

CL = rate of elimination / concentration = $Vd * K_{el}$ (mL/min)
- Total Cl = [[Renal Clearance|renal clearance]] + liver clearance + other clearance

**Half Life** = time to decrease amount of drug by half
- $T_{1/2} = 0.693 * Vd / CL$
