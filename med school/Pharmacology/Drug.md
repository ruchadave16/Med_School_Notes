*Agent introduced to body to cause a pharmacological effect*
- Most drugs are lipophilic, unionized at physiological pH
- Most drugs bind to **target**, or receptor *see [[Drug Transport]]*

>[!Info] D + T → DT Complex → altered function

- Some can act through simple mechanisms like [[antacids]] or [[mannitol]]
#### Drug Targets
- Proteins
	- Regulatory proteins: mediate endogenous signals
	- Enzymes: catalyze reactions
	- Transport proteins
	- Ion channels
	- Structural proteins
- [[DNA]]

**Drug-Receptor Interaction**
1) Binding to receptor – determined by affinity
2) Generation of response – determined by efficacy
	- Occupation of receptor might or might not cause activation
	- [[Agonist]] vs antagonist

**Receptor-Occupancy Theory**
- Some have simple and linear (ligand gated ion channels)
	- Current directly proportional to # receptors
- Others have maximum response even when not saturated (cascade)
	- Presence of spare receptors
	- Non-linear response
	- EC50 < Ka so less than 50% of receptors need to be occupied to get 50% of effect
		- Excess receptors
		- Temporal factors

>[!Info]
>$B_{max}$ = maximum binding capacity, depends on receptor density
>$K_d$ = dissociation constant so lower $K_d$ = higher affinity

#### Characteristics
*Affinity* – how tightly drug binds to receptor
*Efficacy* – maximum biological effect from binding to receptor
*Potency* – amount of drug needed to get effect

**BELOW A 2x higher efficacy than B**
**C 10x higher potency than D**

#### Drug Ionization

![[Screenshot 2025-12-03 at 9.19.26 AM.png]]

- Unionized form (HA, B) – lipid soluble, passes membrane
- Ionized form (A-, BH+) – cannot pass membrane

**Ion Trapping: Unionized drug passes membrane and is trapped due to difference in pH**
- Acidic drugs stay in high pH (dissociate here and are trapped), and same for basic in low pH

>[!Info]
>Urinary acidity and basicity affects excretion of basic/acidic drugs
