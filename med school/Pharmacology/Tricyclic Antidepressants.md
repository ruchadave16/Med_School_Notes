[[Amitriptyline]], [[Imipramine]], [[Clomipramine]], Desipramine
- Metabolism = [[CYP2D6]] (polymorphisms can affect)
#### Action
- Block norepinephrine transporter (NET), serotonin transporter (SERT)
- Can affect multiple receptors
	- Muscarinic receptors 
	- H1 receptors
	- $\alpha1$ receptors
	- 5-HT receptors
#### Uses
- Mainly against [[Major Depressive Disorder|depression]] when SNRIs and SSRIs fail
- Useful in psychotic depression
- Neuropathic Pain
- **[[Clomipramine]]** for [[OCD]]
- Nocturnal enuresis – [[imipramine]]
- Amitriptyline for migraines
#### Administration

#### Dosing
- Narrow therapeutic window
- [[TCA Overdose]]
	- CNS: excitement, seizure, coma
	- Respiratory depression
	- Ventricular tachycardia, fibrillation (Na+ channel block)
	- Support, airway protection, activated charcoal, sodium bicarbonate for cardiac
#### Adverse Effects
“*3 C’s – convulsions, coma, cardiotoxicity*”
- CNS
	- Sedation – H1 block
	- Confusion – elderly
- CV
	- Orthostatic hypotension – $\alpha1$ block
	- Dysrhythmias – Risk high in overdose
- [[Anticholinergic]] – Muscarinic block
- Weight gain
- Sexual Dysfunction
#### Contraindication
- Elderly patients because of antimuscarinic, orthostatic hypotension, fall risk
- Monitor for cardiovascular
