“typical”, “classical”

- Chlorpromazine = prototype
- Ex: haloperidol, thioridazine, fluphenazine
#### Action
- Strong D2 ([[dopamine]]) antagonist in [[Reward Pathway|mesolimbic pathway]]
- Reduction of + symptoms
- Not as effective on - symptoms, can worsen them
#### Adverse Effects
- [[Extrapyramidal Symptoms]]
- Endocrine effects: In tuberoinfundibular region, can cause [[hyperprolactinemia]]

>[!Note]
>- Haloperidol: High D2 antagonist, highly potent but EPS risk
>- Chlopromazine: Moderate D2 but strong binding to H1, M1, alpha1 so many side effects (sedation, anticholinergic, orthostatic hypotension)
