Most work by modulating monoamine transmission ([[serotonin]], [[norepinephrine]], [[dopamine]])
- Cause immediate increase in synapse → effect **delayed 2-4 weeks**
- Chronic activation increases BDNF transcription and BDNF levels → increases synapse connectivity and neuroplasticity
- Increase production of glucocorticoid receptors and beta adrenoreceptors
#### Categories
- [[Tricyclic Antidepressants]] (TCAs)
- Selective serotonin norepinephrine reuptake inhibitors ([[Serotonin Norepinephrine Reuptake Inhibitors|SNRIs]])
- Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors ([[Selective Serotonin Reuptake Inhibitors|SSRIs]])
- 5-HT2 receptor modulators
- Tetracyclic and unicyclic antidepressants
	- [[Bupropion]] – use for smoking cessation too
	- [[Mirtazapine]]
- [[MAO inhibitors]]
- NMDA antagonists
	- [[Ketamine]]
	- [[Esketamine]]
- [[GABA A Receptors|GABA A receptor]] allosteric modulators
	- [[Brexanolone]]

>[!Note]
>No class outperforms the other
>- 2/3 patients show symptom improvement
>- 1/3 complete remission

>[!Note]
>FDA Black box warning about suicidal ideation in children and adolescents from antidepressants
