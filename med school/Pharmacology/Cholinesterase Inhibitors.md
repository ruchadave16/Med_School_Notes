**Short Acting**
- Ex: [[Edrophonium]]

- Act on CNS: [[Donepezil]], [[Galantamine]], [[Rivastigmine]] (for Alzheimers)

**Medium Length**
- Carbamates: [[Neostigmine]], [[Pyridostigmine]], [[Physostigmine]]
- Mechanism: Carbamate acid esters transfer carbamoyl to serine OH in catalytic site
	- Hydrolyzed slowly
- Quaternary ammonium compounds DON’T pass blood brain
	- Tertiary amine (physostigmine) CAN pass

**Irreversible**
- Ex: [[Organophosphates]]:
- Toxicity: 
	- SLUDGE (salivation, lacrimation, urination, defecation, GI upset, emesis)

#### Impact on Body
- Lipid soluble (physostigmine) can generally activate CNS at low dose
	- Coma and respiratory arrest at high
- Similar to [[Direct Acting Cholinomimetics|direct cholinomimetics]]
	- Miosis + aqueous humor ↑ 
	- Bronchoconstriction + ↑ secretions in bronchi
	- ↑ Peristalsis + secretions
	- ↑ bladder contraction + sphincter relaxation
- Heart: similar to vagal
	- ↓ Chronotropic – ↓ HR
	- ↓ Inotropic – ↓ contractile strength
- Vessels
	- Minimal impact
- Muscle contraction because ↑ Ach in junction

>[!Info] Clinical use
>[[Glaucoma]] – Not used anymore, replaced by [[beta blockers]]
>[[Bethanechol]] preferred for urinary retention post surgery, but Neostigmine can be used
>[[Myasthenia Gravis]]
>[[Alzheimers Disease|Alzheimers]]
#### Toxicity
- Early Signs (Muscarinic Stimulation)
	- Miosis
	- Salivation, sweating
	- Bronchoconstriction
	- Vomiting, diarrhea
- Later Signs
	- [[CNS]] effect: coma, convulsions
	- Neuromuscular effects: muscle paralysis

>[!Info] Clinical Resolve
>- Ensure respiration
>- **Decontamination**: remove pesticide to prevent further absorption
>- **Medications**
>	- [[Atropine]]: block muscarinic effects of Ach
>	- [[Pralidoxime]]: regenerates AchE
