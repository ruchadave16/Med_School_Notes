**Mechanisms**
- Passive transport
- Carrier-mediated transport
- Pinocytosis
	- For larger molecules with weight > 900 Da

Most perfuse to brain, kidney and liver initially
- [[Blood brain barrier]] limits passing of drugs – must be highly hydrophobic OR have transport mechanism, cannot be substrate of P-gp
- Accumulation in fat → sign of lipophilic drugs
- Placenta = barrier
#### P-Glycoprotein (P-gp)
Efflux transporter
- Limits entry of drugs into tissues
- Contributes to multi-drug resistance in cancer

Inhibition of P-gp can cause ↑ drug levels in cells
Induction of P-gp reduces drug absorption
#### Binding to Plasma Proteins
- Only free drug is **active** in plasma, so binding inhibits
- [[Albumin]] binds to acidic drugs, $\alpha1$-acid glycoprotein binds to basic drugs
