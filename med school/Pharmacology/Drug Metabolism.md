d*Termination of a [[drug]]’s activity but in some cases, product may have activity or even have enhancement in activity*
#### Common Products
- Xenobiotic-metabolizing enzymes convert [[Drug|drugs]] to hydrophilic derivative to be excreted
- Certain converted to toxic or carcinogenic

| Substrate         | Product                                            |
| ----------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| Active drug       | Inactive product                                   |
| Active drug       | Similar mechanism, duration, and potency           |
| Active drug       | Similar mechanism but different action or efficacy |
| Active drug       | Different mechanism                                |
| Active drug       | Toxic or carcinogenic                              |
| Inactive drug     | Active drug                                        |
| Inactive molecule | Inactive product                                   |
#### Phases of Biotransformation
Main sites: [[liver]], [[Small Intestine|small intestine]] epithelium, [[lungs]], [[skin]], [[kidneys]]
- Liver and GI = first pass for oral drugs

**Phase 1**
- Expose functional groups on drug
- Inactivate drugs but activate prodrugs
- Oxidation ([[cytochrome P450s]], amine oxydase, [[Flavin Containing Monooxygenases|flavin-containing monooxygenases]]), reduction (azo, nitro, carbonyl reduction), hydrolysis (ester and amide hydrolysis)
- [[Dehydrogenases]] catalyze oxidation reactions
	- [[Alcohol dehydrogenase]] – primary and secondary alcohol → aldehyde and ketone
		- High genetic variation
	- [[Aldehyde dehydrogenase]] – aldehyde → carboxylic acid
		- Some ppl have deficiency of mitochondrial ALDH → accumulation of acetaldehyde in blood
- Enzymes in [[Endoplasmic Reticulum|ER]]

**Phase 2**
- Reactions in cytoplasm except glucuronidation in luminal ER
- Combine Phase 1 product with highly polar groups 
- [[Glucuronidation]], [[sulfation]], [[glutathione conjugation]], [[acetylation]], [[methylation]]
- Enzymes 
	- [[UDP-glucuronosyltransferases]] (UGT)
	- [[Glutathione-S-transferases]] (GST)
	- [[Sulfotransferases]] (SULT)
	- [[N-acetyltransferases]] (NAT)
	- [[Methyltransferases]] (MT)
- Catalytic rate MUCH faster than CYP
