*[[Enzymes]] essential for metabolizing substances*
- Contain heme, bind to oxygen
- NADPH-cytochrome P450 reductase provides $e^-$ for the reaction
- [[Drug metabolism]]: CYP1, CYP2, CYP3 families
	- CYP2C, CYP2D, and CYP3A are most active
	- **[[CYP3A4]] = 50% drug metabolism**
	- [[CYP2D6]] – decides drug metabolism speed (ultrarapid, extensive, intermediate, poor)
- Steroid and bile acid synthesis: VERY STRICT, no xenobiotic metabolism

**Characteristics**
- Can metabolize single compound at diff positions
- Overlapping substrate specificity
- 1 compound can be metabolized by multiple CYPs at diff rates
- *Slow catalytic rates*

**Nomenclature**
- CYP (family) (subfamily) (gene number – enzyme) (variant)

#### [[CYP Induction]]
- Generally max effect 7-14 days after exposure 
#### [[CYP Inhibition]]
- Faster effect, duration depends on half-life
