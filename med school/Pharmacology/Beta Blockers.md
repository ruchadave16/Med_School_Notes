Most are antagonists but some are partial agonists or inverse agonists
#### Effects
- CV
	- ↓ HR and contractability
	- ↓ BP in hypertensive through suppression of [[Renin-Angiotensin-Aldosterone System|RAAS system]] (B1 block) and ↓ cardiac output
- Respiratory
	- Blocking $\beta2$ can increase airway resistance
- Ocular
	- ↓ intraocular pressure by ↓ humor production
- Metabolic
	- ↓ lipolysis
	- ↓ recovery from hypoglycemia 
	- Reduces HDL/LDL ratio

**Partial B agonists (ISA)**
- Mild activation when no catecholamines, reducing bradycardia risk
- [[Pindolol]], [[acebutolol]], [[celiprolol]] *CAP on top*
- Should be avoided when chronic heart failure

**Local Anesthetic**
- [[Propranolol]], [[pindolol]], [[acebutolol]]
- Contraindication for ophthalmic application

**Vasodilation**
- [[Carvedilol]] and [[labetalol]] block beta and alpha 1
- [[Nebivolol]] causes vasodilation through NO release
#### Adverse Effects
- CV: Bradycardia, AV block, hypertension
	- Severe peripheral vascular disease might worsen from b2 blockage → low vasodilation
- Resp: Bronchospasm for asthma patients from b2 blockage
- Metabolic: Delayed recovery from hypoglycemia, adverse lipid changes
	- Caution for diabetic patients
	- Can mask symptoms of hypoglycemia
- [[CNS]]: Fatigue, depression, dizziness
- Cold extremities
- [[Discontinuation Syndrome]]
	- Rebound hypertension, angina, arrhythmias
#### Classes
1st Gen (Nonselective)
- Blocks B1 and B2 (equal affinity)
- [[Propranolol]], [[timolol]], [[nadolol]]

2nd Gen (B1 selective)
- Cardioselective
- Use for patients with asthma, diabetes, peripheral vascular
- [[Atenolol]], [[metoprolol]], [[esmolol]], [[bisoprolol]], [[nebivolol]]
- *A-BEAM*

3rd Gen
- Vasodilation effects
	- a1 receptor block – [[Carvedilol]] (B + A1), [[labetalol]]
	- ↑ production of NO – [[Celiprolol]], [[nebivolol]] (NO-based vasodilation)
	- b2 agonist – [[Celiprolol]]
	- Ca2+ entry blockage – [[Carvedilol]]
	- Opening of K+ channel – [[Tilisolol]]
	- Antioxidant – [[Carvedilol]] 
- [[Carvediol]] = nonselective, for chronic heart failure
- [[Labetolol]] = nonselective, for [[hypertensive emergency]]
- [[Nebivolol]] = b1 selective + NO vasodilation 
- [[Betaxolol]] = b1 selective, glaucoma treatment
- [[Celipralol]] = b1 blocker, b2 agonist for vasodilation
#### Clinical Use
[[Hypertension]]
- Not first-line treatment
- Used when cardio-protection is desired
- Helpful for heart failure and CAD patients

[[Ischemic Heart Disease]]
- ↓ HR and contractility, useful for managing [[angina]]
- [[Metoprolol]], [[timolol]], and [[atenolol]] increase lifespan for patients post [[Myocardial Infarction|MI]]

[[Cardiac Arrhythmias]]
- Supraventricular and ventricular
- Increases AV node refractory period
- Slows ventricular response rate
- [[Sotalol]] – also K+ blockage so more antiarrhythmia effect
	- nonselective beta blocker, Class III antiarrhythmias

[[Heart Failure]]
- Standard care for chronic heart failure management
- ↑ survival, ↓ hospitalizations
- [[Metoprolol]], [[bisoprolol]], [[carvediol]], [[nebivolol]]

[[Glaucoma]]
- ↓ intraocular pressure by ↓ aqueous humor
- Want blockers WITHOUT anesthesia effects like [[timolol]]

[[Hyperthyroidism]]
- Alleviate symptoms

[[Migraine Prophylaxis]]
- Can reduce frequency and severity 
- [[Propranolol]], [[timolol]], [[metoprolol]]
