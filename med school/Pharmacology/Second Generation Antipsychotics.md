- [[5-HT2A Receptors|5-HT2A]] inverse agonist and weaker antagonism to D2
- Lowers extrapyramidal symptoms, minimal hyperprolactinemia
- Better treatment for - symptoms and cognitive deficits

Ex:
- [[Clozapine]], [[aripiprazole]], [[pimavanserin]]
- Lurasidone – 5-HT2A antagonist with low metabolic risks
- Olanzapine – SIGNIFICANT metabolic impacts (weight gain, hyperglycemia)
- Quetiapine – Sedation because of strong H1 binding, used in [[Bipolar I]] depression
- Risperidone – D2 and 5-HT2A antagonist, risk of [[hyperprolactinemia]] at high dose
- Ziprasidone – Requires food intake for absorption, good metabolic profile

>[!Note]
>- [[Clozapine]]: Minimal D2 and strong H1, M1, alpha1 so many side effects
>- [[Aripiprazole]]: VERY high D2 binding, partial agonist to D2 reducing EPS risk
>- Ziprasidone: Strong 5-HT2A binding, moderate D2 activity so balances efficacy and ↓ side effect risk
