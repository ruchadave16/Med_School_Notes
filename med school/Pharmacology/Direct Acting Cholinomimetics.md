Bind to and activate [[Muscarinic Receptors|muscarinic receptors]] or [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]]
- Some are selective while others are non-selective
- Topical administration can stop broad impacts
#### Classifications
- Choline Esters
	- Like Ach but quaternary N makes hydrophilic 
	- Can break down by [[acetylcholinesterase]]
		- [[Ach]]: rapidly degraded, limited use
		- [[Methacholine]]: resistant to breakdown
		- [[Carbachol]]: highly resistant to breakdown
		- [[Bethanechol]]: bladder and GI motility, resists breakdown
	- Features
		- Poor absorption, doesn’t cross blood-brain
		- Methacholine and bethanechol can NOT act on nicotinic 
	- Clinical Use: GI and Urinary disorders
		- Postoperative Ileus: to restore bowel movement
		- Postoperative Bladder Retention: help bladder empty
			- [[Bethanechol]] directly, but can also indirectly through [[Neostigmine]]
		- Dry Mouth (**Xerostomia**)
- Alkaloids
	- Natural, not degraded by [[acetylcholinesterase]]
		- [[Muscarine]]: in some mushrooms, no clinical use but toxicity
		- [[Nicotine]]: activates [[Nicotinic Receptors|nicotinic receptors]], use for smoking cessation
		- [[Pilocarpine]]: activates [[Muscarinic Receptors|muscarinic receptors]], treat glaucoma and dry mouth
	- Features
		- Lipophilic can penetrate skin (like nicotine)
		- IV injection can cause cortical arousal in animals
		- Effects of muscarine and pilocarpine can be reversed by [[Atropine]]

#### Effects
- Muscarinic stimulation: parasympathetic
- Nicotinic: skeletal muscle or adrenal gland
- Cardiovascular: Muscarinic through M2 receptors
	- SA: ↓ HR
	- Atrial muscle: ↓ contraction
	- AV: ↓ conduction velocity

#### Toxicity of Muscarinic
- Nausea, vomiting, diarrhea
- ↑ Urinary urgency
- ↑ Salivation and sweating
- Skin flushing
- Bronchoconstriction

>[!Info] Antidote = [[Atropine]] (muscarinic receptor blocker)

#### Toxicity of Nicotinic
**Acute**
- CNS Stimulation: Tremors, convulsions *Treatment = anticonvulsants*
- Skeletal muscle: Muscle twitching and paralysis 
- Cardiovascular: Hypertension and arrhythmias
- *Treatment* for respiratory = mechanical ventilation
- *Treatment* for cholinergic = atropine

**Chronic**
- Highly addictive
- ↑ risk of vascular disease and peptic ulcers
