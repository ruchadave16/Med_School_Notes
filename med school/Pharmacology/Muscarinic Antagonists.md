*Prevent [[Ach]] from binding to [[Muscarinic Receptors|muscarinic receptors]]*
- Do NOT block nicotinic receptors

1) Natural Alkaloids
	- [[Atropine]]
		- Competitive antagonist
		- Blocks cholinomimetic effect
		- Non-selective for types
	- [[Scopolamine]]
		- More pronounced CNS effects, useful for motion sickness

2) Semisynthetic Derivatives
	- [[Homatropine]]: shorter duration,  (same as atropine)

3) Synthetic Derivatives
	- **Quaternary Ammonium**: [[Ipratropium]], [[Tiotropium]], [[glycopyrrolate]]
		- ↓ absorption through GI
		- No crossing blood brain
		- Used for peripheral effects
	- **Non-Quaternary**: [[Trihexyphenidyl]], [[Benztropine]]
		- Cross [[blood brain barrier]]
		- Used for [[Parkinsons disease]]

4) Subtype Selective
	- [[Pirenzepine]]: Selective to M1 receptors
	- [[Tolterodine]], [[darifenacin]], [[solifenacin]]: Selective to M3 receptors
	- [[Oxybutynin]]

>[!Info] [[Antihistamines]], [[antipsychotics]], and [[Antidepressants]] can cause similar antimuscarinic effects

#### Drug Effects
- CNS
	- Therapeutic dose: 
		- Atropine: minimal stimulant
		- Scopolamine: drowsiness and amnesia
	- Toxic dose: excitement, hallucination, coma
	- [[Parkinsons disease]]: tremor and rigidity is from excess of cholinergic activity 
		- **[[Trihexyphenidyl]]**
		- **[[Benztropine]]**
	- [[Motion sickness]]: use [[Scopolamine]], [[Dimenhydrinate]]
- Eye
	- Mydriasis, cycloplegia (loss of near vision), ↓ lacrimal secretion
	- Used clinically for eye exams
		- [[Atropine]]
		- [[Tropicamide]]
- Cardiovascular
	- SA: ↑ HR in high dose
	- Blood vessels: can block vasodilation effect of cholinergic agonists
		- Not parasympathetic BUT M3 receptors present that produce NO mediated vasodilation when stimulated
- Respiratory
	- Bronchodilation, ↓ secretion in airways
	- Used to manage [[COPD]] and [[asthma]]
- GI
	- ↓ mobility, causes constipation
	- ↓ gland secretion
	- **[[Pirenzepine]]** and **[[telenzepine]]** block M1 selectively, working to reduce gastric acid with fewer side effects
- Genitourinary Tract
	- ↓ voiding by relaxing detrusor
- Sweat Glands
	- ↓ sweating
