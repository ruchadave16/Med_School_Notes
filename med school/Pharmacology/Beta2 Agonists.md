**Short Acting**
- Fast onset, only 3-4 hr impact
- “Rescue therapy”
- For asthma / COPD
- [[Albuterol]] (salbutamol)
- [[Levalbuterol]]
- [[Metaproterenol]]
- [[Terbutaline]]

**Long Acting**
- > 12 hour duration, more liposoluble
- Used for daily maintenance therapy
- [[Salmeterol]]: 15-20 min onset, >12 hr duration
- [[Formoterol]]: Faster onset

**Ultra-Long Lasting**
- **[[Arformoterol]]** , **[[Olodaterol]]**, **[[Vilanterol]]**
- Used for [[COPD]]
- >24 hr duration
#### Action
- Bronchial smooth muscle relaxation
- Activation of $\beta2$ causes Gs ([[G Protein Coupled Receptors|GPCR]]) to be activated → adenylyl cyclase → cAMP → PKA
#### Uses
- Benefit of these is they don’t activate $\beta1$, which causes cardiac effect
- [[Asthma]]
- [[COPD]] therapy
#### Administration
- Inhalation
	- Metered-dose inhalers / nebulizers
	- Local airway effect and low system toxicity
- Oral: rarely used
	- System side effects
	- Ex: [[Albuterol]], [[Terbutaline]]
- SC
	- [[Terbutaline]]: Used in severe asthma where emergency and inhaled methods don’t work
#### Dosing

#### Adverse Effects
- Tachycardia
	- $\beta1$ and $\beta2$ impact on heart
	- $\beta2$ mediated vasodilation
	- ↑ risk with [[MAO inhibitors]] **NEED 2 WEEK GAP**
- Skeletal Muscle Tremor
	- $\beta2$ receptor stimulation
	- Can also happen from hypokalemia
- [[Hypokalemia]]
- ↑ Fatty acids, insulin, glucose, pyruvate, lactate  

**[[Tolerance]]**
- Non-airway effects build up [[tolerance]] over time
- NO tolerance in asthma patients because of high $\beta2$ receptor reserve in bronchi
#### Contraindication
