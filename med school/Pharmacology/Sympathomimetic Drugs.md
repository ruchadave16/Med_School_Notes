1) Direct Acting
	- [[Adrenergic receptors]] directly act
	- Can be selective for subtypes
		- $\alpha1$: [[Phenylephrine]]
		- $\alpha2$: [[Clonidine]]
		- $\beta1$: [[Dobutamine]]
		- [[Beta2 Agonists|b2 agonists]]
	- Nonselective: [[epinephrine]], [[norepinephrine]], [[isoproterenol]]
	
2) Indirect Acting
	- Increase availability of [[Catecholamines|catecholamines]]
		- Displace [[norepinephrine]] from nerve endings: [[tyramine]], [[amphetamine]]
		- Inhibit reuptake of [[norepinephrine]]: [[cocaine]], [[Tricyclic Antidepressants]], [[Atomoxetine]]
		- Block metabolism of [[catecholamines]]
			- [[MAO inhibitors]]: [[Selegiline]]
			- [[COMT inhibitors]]: [[Entacapone]]

3) Mixed Acting
	- Indirectly release NE and also directly activate [[adrenergic receptors]]
	- Ex: [[Ephedrine]]
#### Effects
- Blood Vessels
	- Constriction = $\alpha1$ and $\alpha2$ in most vascular smooth muscle → ↑ BP → [[baroreceptor reflex]] → ↓ HR
	- Dilation = $\beta2$ in skeletal and D1 (renal, splanchnic, coronary, cerebral)
- Heart
	- ↑ chronotropic and inotropic effect ($\beta1$)
- Renin secretion
	- $\beta1$ stimulates, $\alpha2$ inhibits
	- [[Beta blockers]] reduce renin secretion → antihypertensive
- Eye
	- Alpha agonist – mydriasis (dilation)
- Respiratory
	- $\beta2$ [[Beta2 Agonists|agonists]] = bronchodilation
	- Used for asthma and COPD management
- GI relaxation
	- $\alpha2$ inhibits [[Ach]] secretion so ↓ motility
	- [[Beta Agonist|Beta agonists]] directly relax GI smooth muscle
- Uterus
	- $\beta2$ causes relaxation
	- Alpha receptors minor contractile
- Urinary
	- Alpha receptor = contraction, ↑ urinary continence
	- $\alpha1A$ antagonist for prostate disease treatment to relax prostate and bladder
	- $\beta3$ = detrusor relaxation
		- [[Mirabegron]] is $\beta3$ agonist, used for overactive bladder
- Endocrine
	- Beta receptors ($\beta3$) increase lipolysis in fat cells, $\alpha2$ inhibits
	- Glycogenolysis ↑ by beta and maybe $\alpha1$
	- $\beta$ increase insulin, $\alpha2$ decrease
