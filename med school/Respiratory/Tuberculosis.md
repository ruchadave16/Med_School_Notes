### Etiology
- [[Mycobacterium tuberculosis]]

### Pathophysiology
- Modifies phagosome (for [[Phagocytosis]]) so it can’t fuse with [[lysosome]]
- Allows it to proliferate inside [[Macrophage|macrophages]] and protect from antibodies

- First 3 weeks
	- [[Macrophage]] target
	- Mannose binding receptor
	- Surfactant A binds to M. tuberculosis and phagocytes through alveolar macrophage
	- Avoid degradation by endosome: ↓ acidification, no autophagosome formation
- [[Granuloma]] formation -- [[caseous necrosis]]
	- ↑ TNF-a from macrophages
	- ↓ respiratory burst so no killing of bacteria (NO)
	- Recruitment of healthy macrophages to site -- granuloma
	- [[Th1]] cells activated which further amplify macrophage recruitment
- Post 3 Weeks
	- Ghon complex: Granuloma + ipsilateral mediastinal lymphadenopathy, can spread to other organs
	- Usually ASYMPTOMATIC
- Generally goes dormant
	- Can have seeding in other organs

- **Secondary Tuberculosis**
	- Lung
	- Lymphadenitis -- scrofula
	- Meninges
	- Vertebral bodies  -- Pott Disease
	- Almost all organs
### Clinical Features
- Malaise
- Anorexia
- Weight loss
- Fever (remergent, appears in afternoon and goes away)
- Night sweats
- Blood in cough 
- Pleuritic pain
### Diagnosis
- Sputum culture
- Smear on [[Ziehl-Neelsen Stain|acid fast stain]]
- PCR on sputum
	- Check [[rifampin]] resistance
### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
- Surgical
- Lifestyle

### Complications
- [[Caseous Necrosis|Caseous necrosis]]
