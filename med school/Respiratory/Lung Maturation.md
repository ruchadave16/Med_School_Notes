5 stages by morphology *Every Pulmonologist Can See Alveoli*
1) Embryonic Period (4-7 weeks)
	- [[Lung buds formation]]
	- **Trachea and bronchi** differentiation

2) [[Pseudoglandular Period]] (5-16 weeks)
	- Branching of tree into [[bronchioles]] (full *conducting portion* of [[respiratory system]]) (get all up to terminal bronchioles)
	- Respiration impossible if born at this time

3) Canalicular Period (16-26 weeks)
	- Branching into terminal bronchioles → alveolar ducts (respiratory portion)
	- [[Type I Pneumocytes]] develop at 20 weeks
	- Infants born at this time = mortality about 100%
	- By 24-26 weeks, **primitive alveoli** formed and respiration possible

4) Saccular Period (26 weeks - birth)
	- Thinning of primitive alveolar walls
	- Capillaries protrude into terminal sacs
	- **Alveolar sacs** made
	- [[Pulmonary Surfactant|Pulmonary surfactant]] made by [[Type II Pneumocytes|type II pneumocytes]]
		- Initially made at **24 weeks**
		- Let alveoli stay open and not stick together 

5) Alveolar Period (36 weeks - 8 years)
	- [[Type I Pneumocytes|Type I pneumocytes]] become thin and terminal sacs into [[alveoli]]
	- **Increase** in bronchioles, alveolar ducts, terminal sacs, alveoli

>[!Clinical] [[Pulmonary Hypoplasia]] – small, underdeveloped [[lungs]]
>Comes after:
>- Compression by abnormal positions of abdominal viscera
>- [[Pleural Effusions]]
>- Severe oligohydramnios
