Ventilation and blood perfusion not uniform ← effect of gravity
- Lower in apex of [[lungs]] than at base

![[Pasted image 20250818221719.png]]

Arterial pressure increases from zone 1 → zone 3

**Zone 1**:
- Alveolar pressure is slightly greater than arterial pressure, blood flow lowest (ventilation > perfusion)

**Zone 2**:
- Higher arterial than alveolar so higher blood flow but alveolar greater than venous so caused by difference in arterial and alveolar, not A-V difference

**Zone 3**:
- Highest arterial pressure in lungs, venous > alveolar (perfusion > ventilation)

#### V/Q Abnormalities
*Caused by anything that compromises ventilation*

Issues: 
- Increased dead space – no gas exchange because ventilated but not perfused
- Airway obstruction – low ventilation to perfused areas
- Alveolar membrane thickening – difficult diffusion distance for $O_2$
- Destruction of [[alveoli]] – decrease SA for $O_2$ diffusion (**[[Emphysema]]**)

**Shunts** – blood flow that *may not* be in contact with [[alveoli]]
- Right-to-left: Not oxygenated by alveoli
	- Can be cardiac shunt because of defect in wall between ventricles 
	- Allows right ventricular blood to pass through left ventricle and bypass lungs
	- [[Hypoxemia]] by having patient breath high $O_2$ gas
	- Could also be blood flow in lungs to area not ventilated
- Physiological Shunt: Normal
	- Bronchial blood flow and small amount of coronary blood flow into left ventricle bypassing alveoli
- Left-to-right: Doesn’t bypass alveoli
	- Shunted from left to right side (not delivered to tissues)
	- **No hypoxemia**

##### V/Q Defect Examples
- High V/Q 
	- High ventilation relative to perfusion 
	- Well ventilated areas of lung where blood flow ↓ 
	- NOT absence of blood flow
- Low V/Q
	- Low ventilation relative to perfusion
	- Partial obstruction to ventilation
	- Not a shut – some ventilation
	- Low oxygenation level
