**Pseudostratified columnar** with [[Cilia|cilia]] and [[goblet cells]] 

![[Pasted image 20251108135921.png]]

1) Ciliated columnar
	- Sweeps mucus towards [[pharynx]] to remove
	- Most abundant
2) Goblet cells
	- Secrets mucus to trap bacteria
	- Basal nuclei, apical surface with mucous granules
	- Secretion – Merocrine, eccrine
3) Basal cells
	- Not as common
	- Small, round, mitotically active
	- Stem cells that replace others
4) Brush cells
	- Less common
	- Columnar with [[microvilli]], function as chemosensory
5) Small granule cells [[Kulchitsky Cells]] 
	- Diffuse neuroendocrine cells (DNES)

![[Screenshot 2025-08-16 at 5.34.36 PM.png]]

>[!Clinical] [[Metaplasia]]
>Reversible $\Delta$ in cell type as response to injury → First step in [[cancer]]
>
>For smokers:
>- ↑ [[goblet cells]] in response to pollutant ↑ 
>- cilia immobilization because of toxin accumulation
>  
> Results in mucous not being cleared, ↑ coughing, $\Delta$ of epithelium to **stratified squamous**
