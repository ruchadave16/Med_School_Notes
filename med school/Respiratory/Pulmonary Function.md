#### Lung Volumes
- Tidal Volume ($V_T$) = volume of quiet breath = 500 mL
- Inspiratory Reserve Volume (IRV) = volume inspired above $V_T$ = 3000 mL 
- Expiratory Reserve Volume (ERV) = volume expired at end of tidal volume expiration = 1000 mL 
- Residual Volume (RV) = volume in lungs after expiration = 1200 mL

#### Lung Capacities
- Total Lung Capacity (TLC) = IRV + $V_T$ + ERV + RV = total air in lung after maximum inspiration = 5700 mL 
- Vital Capacity (VC) = IRV + $V_T$ + ERV = volume of max expiration after max inspiration = 4500 mL 
- Inspiratory Capacity (IC) = IRV + $V_T$ = max inspiration from end of tidal expiration = 3500 mL 
- Functional Residual Capacity (FRC) = ERV + RV = volume of air in lung after tidal expiration = 2200 mL 

#### Pulm Function Measurements
- Forced Vital Capacity (FVC) = total volumen exhaled after max inspiration
- $FEV_1$ = volume air exhaled forcibly in first second of FVC
- $FEV_1/FVC$ = fraction of FVC exhaled in 1 second

#### Pulmonary and Alveolar Ventilation
Some air fills conducting airways – **anatomical dead space** (about 150 mL)
- Physiologically dead space if area not perfused by pulm capillaries

Dead space ($V_D$)

Pulmonary Ventilation = $V_T$ * breaths/min
Alveolar Ventilation = $V_T - V_D$ * breaths/min
