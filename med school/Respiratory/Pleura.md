*serous membrane in form of closely invaginated sac*

Covers inside of [[Thoracic Wall|thoracic wall]] (parietal pleura) and surface of [[lung]] (visceral pleura) with space in between (pleural cavity)

1) Parietal pleura (inside of thoracic wall)
	- Costal pleura
	- Diaphragmatic pleura
	- Mediastinal pleura
	- Cervical pleura (above R1)
2) Visceral pleura (surface of lungs)

>[!Clinical] [[Pleuritis]] – inflammation of pleura. Symptoms = peluritic chest pain, friction rub on auscultation, dyspnea

#### Internal Anatomy
##### Pleural Cavity
- Pleural recess: where pleura folds back with on lung ← can accumulate fluid, site of [[thoracentesis]]

>[!Clinical] [[Pleural Effusion]] = fluid accumulation in pleurae space
>Named by type of fluid: 
>- [[Hydrothorax]] = water
>- [[Hemothorax]] = [[blood]]
>- [[Chylothorax]] = [[lymph]]
>- [[Pyothorax]] = pus
>
>Can cause dyspnea (difficulty breathing)

#### Innervation
- Parietal Pleura
	- Somatic from [[intercostal nerves]] (costal pleura) and [[Phrenic Nerve]] (3, 4, 5) (diaphragmatic + mediastinal pleura)
	- Sensitive to pain
- Visceral Pleura
	- Insensitive to pain but sensitive to stretch
	- Visceral somatic autonomic nerves (Vagus)
