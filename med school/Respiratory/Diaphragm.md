*[[Skeletal Muscle|skeletal muscle]] dividing [[thoracic cavity]] and [[abdominal cavity]]*

Moves inferiorly when contracted → increases volume of thoracic cavity ([[inhalation]]) – chief for [[External Respiration|respiration]]
Relaxes when expiration through elastic recoil
##### Boundaries
Right dome: Ascends to 5th rib
Left dome: Level of 5th intercostal space 
##### Contents
- Central tendon (Thin, strong aponeurosis at center)
- Peripheral muscle fibers that attach to sternum, internal ribs, and lumbar vertebra
- Left and right crura – around esophageal hiatus that form suspensory ligament of Treitz (connects to [[duodenum]])
- Medial + lateral arcuate ligaments

![[Pasted image 20250906233529.png]]

>[!Clinical] [[Diaphragmatic Hernia]] – lumbocostal triangle = weak part in posterolateral diaphragm – could be location of abdominal content herniation

>[!Clinical] Hiatal hernia – Esophageal hiatus enlargement means stomach can protrude into thorax through esophageal foramen

##### Internal Contents
- Vena cavan foramen: Through central tendon at T8 vertebral level
	- [[Inferior Vena Cava|IVC]] passes here and branches of R phrenic n.
- Esophageal hiatus: Fibers of R crus at T10 vertebral level
	- Esophagus, vagal trunks, L gastric vessels (esophageal branches) pass here
- Aortic hiatus: Median arcuate ligament covers this at T12 vertebral level
	- Azygous v. and thoracic duct pass here
	- Splanchnic nerves (greater, lesser, less) pass through muscles near this
##### Blood Supply
- Pericardiacophrenic a. and musculophrenic a. ← internal thoracic a.
- Superior phrenic a. and inferior phrenic a. ← descending aorta
##### Innervation
- Motion through [[Phrenic Nerve|phrenic n.]]
- Sensory – Central phrenic, peripheral intercostal T5-T12

>[!Clinical] [[Kehr’s Sign]] – Pain of diaphragm referred to supraclavicular area (C3, 4, 5)

##### Innervation
Motor: [[Phrenic Nerve]] (C3-C5) *C3, 4, 5 keeps diaphragm alive*
Sensory: Phrenic n. minor: Intercostal n. Subcostal n.

>[!Clinical] Referral Pain: Irritation of diaphragm causes C3-C5 [[dermatome]] pain (top of shoulder → lateral arm)

>[!Clinical] Paralysis of Diaphragm
>Damage to [[Phrenic Nerve]], unilateral so only half affected
>- Due to cancer, stroke, surgery
>- Presents with shortness of breath
>  
>  Diagnosis: imaging showing paradoxical movements of affected side **Sniff Test**
>- Inspiration paralyzed moves up, expiration moves down
>
