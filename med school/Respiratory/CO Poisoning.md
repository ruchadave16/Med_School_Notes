### Etiology
- Incomplete combustion (car exhaust, gas furnace, house fire)
- Multiple ppl in one family in winter
- Could be intentional as form of suicide
### Pathophysiology
- CO binds to heme as seen in [[Oxygen Transport|oxygen transport]] forming [[carboxyhemoglobin]]
	- CO has MUCH higher affinity to hemoglobin
	- Shifts to R form
	- Reduced carrying capacity → tissue hypoxia
- CO also binds to [[myoglobin]] → cardiac ischemia

### Clinical Features
- Nonspecific
	- **Headache**
	- Dizziness
	- Fatigue
	- Nausea
- Neurotoxicity
	- Altered mental status
	- Seizures 
	- **loss of consciousness**
- Cardiorespiratory
	- Inhalation injury
	- SOB
	- [[Shock]]
	- **Respiratory failure – Rate = 20-25 / min**
- **Cherry red skin with bulbous lesions** – rare
- **Fundoscopic: bright red retinal veins**
- Mild ↑ temperature
- Elevated pulse 100-120 bpm
	- **PULSE OX = NORMAL**
- Mildly elevated BP

### Diagnosis
- Abnormal COHb on venous/arterial CO oximetry
	- >3-4% in nonsmokers
	- >10-15% in smokers
- Liver positive for CO
### Treatment
- 100% $O_2$ (hyperbaric oxygen) to displace CO from blood
	- **Start IMMEDIATELY if suspicion**

### Complications
- Short-term
- Long-term
