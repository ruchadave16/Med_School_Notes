Dalton’s Law of Partial Pressures: pressure by 1 gas = total * fractional concentration
- Amount of gas depends on partial pressure, in equilibrium with partial pressure of gas in alveolar air after blood passes through lungs
- This difference in partial pressure causes blood to gain $O_2$ and lose $CO_2$
- Capacity of diffusion depends on → SA of [[alveoli]] and thickness of membrane

- Decrease in alveolar $P_{O2}$ relative to air is because of water vapor in air and also loss of $O_2$ since it leaves alveoli to load venous blood with $O_2$. 
	- Opposite is why $CO_2$ 
	- Should show **complete equilibrium** of $P_{O2}$ and $P_{CO2}$ between alveolar air and blood leaving lungs
	- In tissues, opposite movement
