### Etiology
- Rapid ascent above 2000-2500m (6500-8000 ft)

### Pathophysiology
- PiO2 and oxygenation ↓ at high altitudes
- Early change: Hypobaric hypoxia → ventilation → tachypnea + [[Respiratory Alkalosis]] → ↑ [[glycolysis]] → ↑ 2,3 BPG synthesis → right shift of oxygen dissociation curve → ↑ oxygenation
- Later change: Polycythemia and arterial pH to normal because of bicarb excretion (renal compensation)

### Clinical Features
- Onset 4-12 hrs
- **Headache**
- Fatigue, dizziness, 
- Anorexia, nausea, vomiting
- Peripheral edema
- Sleep disturbance

### Diagnosis
- Symptom development post high altitude activity

### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
- Surgical
- Lifestyle

### Complications
- [[High Altitude Cerebral Edema]]
