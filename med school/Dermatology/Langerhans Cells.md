[[Antigen presenting cells]] – bridge between innate and adaptive immunity

- Activate [[T Lymphocytes|T cells]]

Located in spinosum layer of skin and mucosal membrane
- Initially found immature with pattern recognition receptors (PRRs)

**Steps**
1) Antigen Capture: mainly found in tissue and mucosal surfaces where they take antigens in through [[phagocytosis]]
2) Migration: move through lymph
3) Arrival: have peptide loaded on MHC
4) Presentation: present to naive [[T Lymphocytes|T cells]] in [[Lymph Nodes]]

**Categories**
- Receptor mediated endocytosis – extracellular bacteria: MHC-II → CD4
- Macro-pinocytosis – extracellular bacteria, soluble antigens, virus particles: MHC-II → CD4
- Viral infection: MHC-I → CD8
- [[Cross Presentation]] after phagocytosis of virus: MHC-I → CD8
- Transfer between dendritic cells of viruses: MHC-I → CD8
