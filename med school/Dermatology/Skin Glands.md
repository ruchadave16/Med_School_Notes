1) Sebaceous Glands
	- In [[dermis]] over most of body
	- Produces serum to lubricate and waterproof skin
	- **Holocrine** secretion
	- Open into [[hair follicles]] as part of [[pilosebaceous]] unit

![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 2.42.01 PM.png]]

2) Eccrine Sweat Glands
	- Widely distributed, mainly in soles
	- Important for [[thermoregulation]] but also waste excretion
	- Ducts open to [[Epidermis|epidermal]] surface
	
![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 2.44.49 PM.png]]

>[!Clinical]
>[[Cystic Fibrosis]] – Infants often have very salty sweat, which can be indication of CF
>- Caused by defect in [[Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane Regulator|CFTR]], which causes disruptive mucus accumulation and more salt in sweat

3) Apocrine Sweat Glands
	- Skin of axillary and perineal regions
	- Develop during [[puberty]]
	- Require [[sex hormones]]
	- **Merocrine** secretion
	- Apical blebs on lumen surface, scalloped appearance
