##### Function
- [[Connective Tissue|Connective tissue]] layer that supports [[epidermis]] and anchors to [[subcutaneous tissue]]
- Dense vascularity to support avascular epidermis
- Nervous tissue
- Adnexal structures extend into dermis
	- [[Hair follicles]]
	- Secretory glands
- Fibrous tissue
- Immune cells

##### Layers
1) Papillary Layer: 

![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 2.01.48 PM.png]]

-  Superficial layer with irregular surface with projections into [[epidermis]]
- **Dermal papilla** and **epidermal ridges**
- Loose [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]] with type I and II [[collagen]]
- **Meissner’s Corpuscles** in dermis ridges

![[Screenshot 2025-08-09 at 2.08.21 PM.png]]
	
2) Reticular Layer:
	- Dense irregular [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]], mainly Type I [[Collagen|collagen]]
	- [[Elastic Fibers|Elastic fibers]] provide elasticity
	- Fewer cells than papillary but thicker

>[!Warning] This doesn’t have Type III collagen even though it is the reticular layer

##### Components
1) Subpapillary Plexus
	- Vascular network between papillary and reticular layers
	- Provides nutrition and thermoregulation
2) Cutaneous Autonomic Nervous System
	- Regulates [[sweat gland]] function, skin blood flow, and body temperature homeostasis
	- Made of [[sympathetic fibers]]

##### Sensory Receptors
1) Free Nerve Endings: 
	- Papillary dermis + lower epidermis
	- Respond to high and low temp, pain, itching, some tactile function
2) Meissner’s Corpuscles
	- Light touch and low frequency stimuli 
	- Rapidly adapting mechanoreceptors
3) Pacinian Corpuscles
	- Mechanical and vibratory pressure sensors
	- Coarse touch, substained pressure and vibrations
	- Found in reticular dermis or hypodermis
4) Ruffini Receptors
	- Small, simple dermal mechanoreceptors 
	- Common in soles
	- Respond to stretch and torque of collagen
5) Root Hair Plexus
	- Hair follicle receptor
	- Detect movement of hair
6) Krause End Bulbs
	- Cold thermoreceptors in special areas of body
