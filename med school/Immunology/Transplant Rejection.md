*T cells and antibodies produced against graft antigens to destroy graft tissues*

- Main difference is in HLA genes
#### Hyperacute Rejection
- Minutes - hours after transplantation
- **Pre-existing antibodies** for graft antigens on endothelial cells
	- Can be from previous exposure or IgM
- Rare
#### Acute Rejection
- Days to weeks (<6 months)
- **Main cause of graft failure**
- Acute cellular rejection
	- [[T Lymphocytes|T cells]] attack graft – histology = perivascular cellular infiltrates
- Antibody-mediated rejection
	- Antibodies bind to vascular endothelium and activate [[Complement System|complement]] through classical pathway
![[Screenshot 2026-02-02 at 10.54.53 PM.png]]

#### Chronic Rejection
- Months - years
- T cell mediated, proliferation and activation of [[fibroblasts]] and [[Smooth Muscle]] cells causing **interstitial fibrosis and vessel narrowing**
- Resistant to therapy

![[Screenshot 2026-02-02 at 10.56.16 PM.png]]

#### Prevention
- Anti-inflammatories (steroids) and drugs to inhibit lymphocyte proliferation
- Plasmapheresis for antibody-mediated rejection

Complications
- Polyoma virus
- EBV virus
- HPV
- HHV-8
