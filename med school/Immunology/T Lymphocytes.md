White blood cells that help [[B Lymphocytes|B cells]] → helper T
Others directly kill → killer T

**Need [[Antigen Presenting Cells|APC]] → can’t recognize antigen by self**
- Express [[T cell receptors]] on surface
- Development: initially in [[bone marrow]] but education in [[thymus]]
	- Mature once they encounter antigen – **Only recognize peptides**
#### Histology
Round nucleus, Scant cytoplasm (Whole cell looks like nucleus)
#### Selection
- Positive Selection: ensures T-cells recognize MHC. Only those with MCH-I or MHC-II get survival signal
	- [[Thymus]] cortex
- Negative Selection: ensure they don’t bind to self-antigens. If too strong, [[Apoptosis|apoptosis]] signal
	- *Also called [[central tolerance]]*
	- [[Thymus]] medulla
#### Types of Cells

![[Screenshot 2025-08-17 at 4.45.25 PM.png]]

1) [[Helper T Cells]] (CD4+) 
	- Subset: [[Th17 Cells]]

2) [[Cytotoxic T Cells]] (CTL) / Killer T Cells (CD8+)

3) Regulatory T Cells (Tregs, suppressor T cells) (CD4+)
	- Suppress CD4 and CD8
	- All express [[CD25]], also have [[CD4]] and [[CD3]]
	- Regulate against extra immune response
	- Recognize antigens on MHC-II
	- Produce [[IL-10]] and TGF-$beta$

4) Memory T Cells (CD4+ and CD8+)
	- Memory of acquired immune response
	- 2 categories – effector and central memory
	- [[IL-7]]
	- Effector
		- Terminally differentiated
		- Immediate effector function
		- CCR7-
	- Central
		- Not terminal
		- Not immediate effector
		- CCR7+

>[!Clinical] PPD Test
>- Inject TB under skin
>- Memory Th1 cells activate and secrete IFN-$\gamma$
>- Activate skin macrophages
>- Local swelling if previous TB exposure
