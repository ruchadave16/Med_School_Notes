### Pathophysiology
- Autoantibodies, especially antinuclear antibodies (ANAs) – group of antibodies that target normal proteins in nucleus like DNA, histones, non-histone proteins, and centromeres
	- Do NOT cross membranes to attack nuclear proteins
	- Exposed to nuclear proteins *when* cell is damaged and nuclear proteins are exposed
	- UV radiation thus worsens condition
	- **anti-dsDNA and anti-Sm antibodies** are highly specific to lupus
- Lesions due to [[Type III hypersensitivity]] – immune complex deposition
- Autoantibodies against blood cells opsonize these cells and cause phagocytosis causing cytopenias – [[Type II hypersensitivity]]
##### Pathological Findings
- Presence of anti-dsDNA or anti-Sm antibodies
- Presence of antiphospholipid antibodies
- Acute necrotizing vasculitis → [[fibrinoid necrosis]] of vessel walls
- Glomerulonephritis (glomerular deposits of immune complexes)
- Nonerosive synovitis
- Pericarditis
- Libman-Sacks endocarditis
### Clinical Features
**Presentation**
- Chronic, remitting, and relapsing febril illness
- Injury to **kidneys, skin, joints, serosal membranes**

**Criteria**
- Butterfly rash on face – malar rash
- ![[Screenshot 2026-02-02 at 10.41.59 PM.png]]
- Photosensitivity
- Discoid rash – erythematous patches with keratotic scaling
-![[Screenshot 2026-02-02 at 10.42.36 PM.png]]
- Non-scarring alopecia
- Oral or nasal ulcers
- Joint disease
- Serositis
- Renal involvement
- Neuro symptoms
- Hematologic manifestations 
### Diagnosis
- Presence of anti-dsDNA or anti-Sm antibodies
- Presence of antiphospholipid antibodies
- Can have cytopenias, thrombocytopenia (10%)
### Treatment
- Pharmacologic
	- Flares treated by [[Synthetic Corticosteroids|synthetic corticosteroids]] and immunosuppressants
- Surgical
- Lifestyle
	- Avoiding UV radiation – can be a trigger

### Complications
- Common cause of death = renal failure
-
>[!Warning]
> [[Hydralazine]] can cause SLE-type response

### High-Yield Associations
- 1 in 2500 people in certain populations
- Primarily affects women, higher prevalence in black and hispanic populations
- Presents 20-30 yrs
