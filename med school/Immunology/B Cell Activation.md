2 steps – [[BCR]] binds to [[antigen]] and then signal 2 from [[T Lymphocytes|T cell]]

1) Naive B cells → haven’t seen antigen exit [[bone marrow]] into circulation

**T Cell Dependent**
2) Humoral immunity starts: B-cell receptor on naive interacts with antigen
	- Binds on multiple receptors and the more cross-linking the stronger
		- CD19, CD21, CD81 strengthen signal transduction
	- B cell internalizes antigen
	- Presents antigen on [[MHC-II]] to [[helper T cell]] or [[Langerhans Cells|dendritic cell]]
		- [[CD40]]  presented to CD40 ligand (for [[class switching]]) → activates AID
		- [[B7]] → CD28 (for cytokine production from T cell)

	- Variation: Macrophage presents to T cell but B cell still has the connection through CD40

**T Cell Independent**
2) Some can cluster a large number of B-cell receptors and activate directly
	- Important for non-protein antigens
	- Weaker response, mainly [[IgM]], no memory
#### Fate of B Cells 
B cell activated → migrates to secondary lymphoid tissues to proliferate
-  Differentiates into [[Plasma Cells|plasma cell]] (produce antibodies against specific antigen in spleen or bone marrow) or memory B cell (stays to increase response)
