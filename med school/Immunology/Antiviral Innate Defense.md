#### Recognition
- TLRs (3, 7, 8) for viral nucleic acids
#### Response
##### Initial Response
- Type 1 Interferons: 
	- Cause for **flu-like symptoms**
	- Produced by infected cells to block replication in normal cells by turning on antiviral genes
	- Also increase [[MHC-I]] so infected cells visible to CD8 cells
	- IFN-a = dendritic cells, macrophages by detecting viral nucleic acids
	- IFN-b = virally infected cells like fibroblasts and epithelial cells
- Type 2 Interferons (IFN-y):
	- Produced by [[Natural Killer Cells|NK cells]] early on (24-48 hrs)
	- [[Cytotoxic T Cells|CD8]] recognize and make IFN-y to kill
	- CD4 also make for antigen visibility
- Inflammation
	- Bring [[Natural Killer Cells|NK cells]] (early killing), [[Cytotoxic T Cells|CD8]] (specific killing), [[Macrophage|Macrophages]] (phagocytosis and antigen presentation), [[Langerhans Cells|dendritic cells]] (activate T cells in lymph nodes)
##### Antigen Specific Response
- Takes longer to start
- Humoral 
	- Neutralizing (stop replication process of virus) and non-neutralizing [[antibodies]](increase phagocytosis)
- Cellular immunity
	- CD8 
	- Lymphokines and monokines regulate
#### Impact
- Immunopathology (persistent viral infection) and Immunosuppression ()
