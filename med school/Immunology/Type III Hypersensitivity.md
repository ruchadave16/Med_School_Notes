Antibody-antigen (IgG) complexes form and activate [[Complement System|complement]] → tissue damage

#### Generalized: Serum Sickness
- IC in plasma → systemic disease
- Deposit in skin, kidneys, joints
- Trigger immune response – complement activation and activation of macrophages / neutrophils
- Historical Description:
	- Horse plasma for passive immunization
	- **Fever, rash, arthalgias** 5-10 days later
	
*Presentation*
- Urticaria or palpable purpura
- ↓ complement
- ↑ sedimentation rate
- Diffuse lymphadenopathy
- Acute glomerulonephritis

*Causes*
- [[Rabies]] or [[tetanus]] anti-toxin
- Rarely for penicillin
- Monoclonal antibodies (rituximab, infliximab)
- Post-strep glomerulonephritis
- [[Systemic Lupus Erythematosus|SLE]]
- Polyarteritis nodosa
#### Localized: Arthus Reaction
- Local reaction usually in skin from injection of antigen
- Preformed antibodies in plasma or skin
- Formation of immune complexes 4-10 hrs after injection
	- Complement activation, edema, necrosis
- Immunofluorescent staining

*Causes*
- Tetanus, diphtheria, hep B vaccines
- Insulin
- Swelling, redness at site of injection
- Hypersensitivity pneuomonitis
	- Farmer’s lung = reaction from environmental antigen
