*[[Antigen]]-specific immune system*

Response must be generated
Distinguishes self from non-self, memory, diversity, specificity
Made of [[B Lymphocytes|B-lymphocytes]] and [[T lymphocytes]] and [[Antigen Presenting Cells]]
- [[Cluster of Differentiation|CD]] cell surface markers, [[cytokines]], [[Antibodies]] ← [[plasma cells]]
Slow acting

**Primary Response**: 1st exposure
- [[IgM]] before IgG
- Long lag phase before antibodies are made
- Titers are low
**Secondary Response**: With memory (more rapid and powerful)
- Requires [[T Lymphocytes|T cells]] → helper T secrete cytokines to more quickly activate B cells
- Faster, longer antibody response
- Higher titers
- Class switching so main = [[IgG]]
#### Humoral
Depends on action of [[Antibodies]], mainly [[B Lymphocytes|B cells]]

#### Cell-Mediated
Foreign cells destroyed by [[T Lymphocytes|T cells]] directly
