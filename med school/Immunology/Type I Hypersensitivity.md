Immediate reaction to antigen (**minutes**)
- [[IgE]] mediated from class switching

#### Mechanism
Pre-formed IgE antibodies from 1st exposure
- Antibodies bound to [[Mast Cells]] 
- Bind and cross-link with IgE
- Mast cell degranulation (releases vasoactive amines and other mediators)

**Atopy**: genetic predisposition to hypersensitivity

![[Screenshot 2026-02-03 at 6.21.19 PM.png]]
#### Mediators
- [[Histamine]]: vasodilation, ↑ permeability venules, SM contraction (bronchospasm)
- Eicosanoids
	- PGE2 – redness (vasodilation), edema (permeability), fever (hypothalamus), pain (nerves)
	- PGD2 – bronchoconstriction, [[Eosinophils|eosinophil]] infiltration
	- LTC4 / LTD4 – vasoconstriction, bronchoconstriction
	- [[LTB4]] – neutrophil, eosinophil chemotaxis
- ECF-A
	- [[Eosinophils|Eosinophil]] chemotactic factor of [[anaphylaxis]]
	- Preformed in [[Mast Cells|mast cells]]
- [[Serotonin]]: Preformed in [[Mast Cells|mast cells]] for vasodilation
- Platelet activating factor: bronchoconstriction
- Neutral proteases
- [[Heparin]]: anticoagulant
#### Symptoms
- Skin: hives (urticaria)
- Respiratory tract: rhinitis and wheezing
- Eyes: conjunctivitis
- GI: Diarrhea

**Early** (minutes)
- Degranulation → release of [[histamine]], leukotrienes, prostaglandins
- Edema, redness, irritation

**Late** (6 hours)
- Release of cytokines
- Inflammatory response (neutrophils, eosinophils)
- Induration

>[!Warning] [[Anaphylaxis]]
>Type I reaction
#### Examples
- [[Asthma]]
- Penicillin allergy
- Seasonal allergies
- Allergic conjunctivitis
- Peanut allergy
- Shellfish allergy
#### Desensitization
- Gradual increase of levels of allergens
- Change from IgE → IgG to block mediator release
- Modified [[Th2]] response
