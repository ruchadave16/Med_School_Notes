1) Diffusion: small, nonpolar molecules move directly through bilayer down gradient
	- [[Simple Diffusion|Simple diffusion]], [[facilitated diffusion]]
	- No energy
	- Lipophilic can diffuse slowly, water diffuses very slowly
2) Channels: pores so ions and small molecules pass selectively 
	- Water has [[aquaporins]]
	- Ex: [[Cystic Fibrosis Transmembrane Regulator|CFTR]]
3) Carriers: transmembrane proteins that bind to molecules and transport them
	- [[Active Transport|Active]] vs. passive transport
	- Ex: [[ABC Transporter]]
4) [[Vesicles]]: endocytosis 
	- Phagocytosis: ingestion of particles (bacteria, dead cell parts)
	- Pinocytosis: trap extracellular fluid 
	- Receptor-mediated endocytosis: ligands bind to receptors which aggregate and pinch in
	
	
			Cell-surface receptor associates w cytoplasmic proteins and starts entering as a coated pit
			
			Electron-rich coating has many polypeptides including clatharin
			This marks location of endocytosis and forms cage-like scaffolding during invagination
