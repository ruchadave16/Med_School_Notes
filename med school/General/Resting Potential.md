Unequal distribution of ions across [[Cell Membrane|membrane]] results in equilibrium potential

$Na^+$ is present in a higher concentration outside the cell so the concentration gradient is pulling it towards the cell. 
But the inside of the cell is +, so the electrical gradient is keeping it outside the cell

![[Screenshot 2025-08-07 at 11.07.55 AM.png]]

The point where these two equal one another is the **equilibrium potential**

**Nernst Equation**:

![[Screenshot 2025-08-07 at 11.12.56 AM.png]]


When differential permeability is taken into account, we get the resting potential of the ions
