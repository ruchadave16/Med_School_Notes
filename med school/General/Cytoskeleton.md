*”provide structural support and cell and organelle motility”*
##### Microtubules
- Made of $\alpha$ and $\beta$ **[[tubulin]]** subunits put in cylindrical tube
- Leading edge where polymerization and tubule lengthening happens
- Can be structurally cross-linked by microtubule-associated proteins
- Can be randomly distributed or in structures
	- [[Centrioles]]: center for microtubule formation in cells
		- 9 sets of 3 microtubules in wheel with no central (9+0)
	- [[Cilia]] & [[Flagella]]: motile with 9 sets of 2 microtubules in wheel around 2 microtubules (9+2)![[Screenshot 2025-07-13 at 10.25.55 PM.png]]
	- Basal Bodies: microtubule anchor at base of [[cilia]] and [[flagella]]
	- Mitotic Spindles: centrioles organizing [[mitosis]]
	
>[!Clinical Considerations]
>
>Cancer chemotherapy uses inhibitory compounds to block mitotic spindle in neoplastic cells
##### Intermediate Filaments
- Smaller diameter than microtubules that provide structural support
- Specific to cell type
	- Cytokeratin - [[Epithelial Tissue|epithelial]]
	- Desmin - muscle
	- Glial-fibrillary acidic protein - astrocytes
	- Lamin - nuclei
	- Peripherin; Neurofilaments - neuron
	- Vimentin - mesenchymal (connective tissue)

>[!Clinical Considerations]
>
>Immunocytochemical identification of intermediate filaments used for cancer diagnosis

##### Microfilaments
- Smallest diameter filaments, made of **actin**
- Cell contraction, motility usage
