Derived from [[bone marrow]] cells, become fixed resident in connective tissue
##### Appearance
Nucleus: oval, spherical, centrally-located. No obvious nucleolus
Cytoplasm with **basophilic** secretory granules with mediators of inflammatory response
Look like basophils but a bit larger, found in tissue

![[Screenshot 2025-08-04 at 12.41.56 AM.png]]
##### Function
Respond to [[antigen]]s by releasing granule content, most commonly **histamine**
- [[Blood Flow|Vasodilation]] and increased permeability of vessels
- Mucus production
- Smooth muscle cell contraction

Release factors to attract [[Neutrophils|neutrophil]]s and [[Eosinophils|eosinophil]]
- Activated by [[IgE]]
- ECF-A = eosinophil chemotactic factor
- [[Serotonin]] for vasodilation
