*Matrix that gives support and connects other tissues and cells to form organs*

**Origin is almost always [[mesoderm]]**

>[!Note] Primitive connective tissue called **[[mesenchyme]]**
##### Components
###### Cellular → Fewer cells than other tissue types
1) Fixed cells / Resident cells
	- Little movement, includes cells that make [[Extracellular Matrix|ECM]]
	- Mainly [[fibroblasts]]
	- Others include:
		- [[Osteocyte]] → Bone
		- [[Chondrocyte]] → [[Cartilage]]
		- [[Adipocytes]]
		- [[Macrophage]]
		- [[Pericytes]]
		- [[Mast cells]]
		- [[Plasma cells]]
2) Wandering cells
	- Perform function then die by [[Apoptosis|apoptosis]] or leave
	- Associated with immune or repair functions
	- Called in by some stimuli

###### [[Extracellular Matrix|ECM]]
Made of fibers and ground substance 

##### Function 
- Physical support and protection
- Biochemical support
- Immune function
- Energy storage (storage of fat)
- Damage repair

##### Classification
Connective tissue **proper**: 3 types differentiable based on appearance
1) Loose connective tissue (areolar)
	- Lots of ground substance
	- Hypercellular
	- Least fibers that lack organization
	- Looks delicate and watery
	- Found right below epithelium often	
- ![[Screenshot 2025-08-04 at 12.02.14 AM.png]]
2) Dense irregular connective tissue 
	- Much less ground substance
	- Fewer cells ([[fibroblasts]]) b t still obvious
	- More fibers in bands, but disorganized
	- Looks solid
	- Found in organ capsule usually under loose connective
		- ![[Screenshot 2025-08-04 at 12.04.49 AM.png]]
3)  Dense regular connective tissue
	- Mainly [[collagen]] fibers with scattered [[fibroblasts]]
	- Fibers in parallel form, organized and looks smooth
	- Very little ground substance
	- Looks strong, firm
		- ![[Screenshot 2025-08-04 at 12.06.04 AM.png]]
