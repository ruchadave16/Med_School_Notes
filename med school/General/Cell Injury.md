Reversible and irreversible, can cause cell death through [[necrosis]] and [[apoptosis]]
- Response to stimulus depends on type, duration, severity of injury
	- Ex: skeletal muscle can survive ischemia 2-3 hrs but cardiac only 30 min
#### Common Causes
- [[Hypoxia]] and [[ischemia]]
- Toxins
- Infectious Agents
- Immunologic reactions
- Genetic abnormalities, nutrition abnormalities, physical agents, aging
- [[Chemical cell injury]]

**Common Targets**
- [[Mitochondria]]
- [[Cell Membrane]]
- [[DNA]]
- [[Protein Synthesis]] – ER swells so ribosomes lost, protein synthesis impaired
#### Reversible
- Cellular swelling – Organs appear *pale and enlarged*
	- Small, clear cytoplasmic vacuoles
	- Happens from **failure of ATP-dependent ion pumps** so influx of water
- Fatty change – triglyceride containing lipid vacuoles in cytoplasm
	- Seen in organs involved with lipid metabolism (mainly **[[liver]]**)
	- Disruption of metabolism so accumulation 
- Other changes
	- Blebbing of plasma membrane
	- Mitochondrial swelling
	- Dilation of ER
	- Nuclear chromatic clumping
	- Cytoplasmic myelin figures
