*Major phagocytic cell in [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]], main cell in [[chronic inflammation]]*

**Dominate within 1 week**

Derived from blood-born [[monocytes]] in [[bone marrow]]
- [[Kupffer cell]] (liver)
- [[Microglia]] (brain)
- [[Osteoclasts]] (bone)
- [[Langerhans cell]] (skin)
#### Functions
- Guardians of [[Innate Immunity|innate immunity]]
	- [[Phagocytosis]] of [[Extracellular Matrix|ECM]]
	- [[Tissue repair]] – [[fibrosis]]
	- Antigen-presenting cells needed to activate [[leukocytes]]
	- Secrete GF and [[cytokines]]: **[[IL-1]], [[TNF-a]]**, [[IL-6]], [[IL-8]], [[IL-12]]
- [[Chronic inflammation]] – secrete IL-1, TNF
- [[Tissue repair]] 

**Resting State**: Debris Removal
**Activated State**: More effective killers

#### Activation
**Classic Pathway**
 M1: inflammation and [[Neutrophils|neutrophil]] recruitment
- Induced by 
	- Microbial endotoxins
	- IFN-y from [[Th1]], NKC
	- [[LPS]] from bacteria
	- [[Peptidoglycan]]
	- Bacterial DNA (no methylation)
	- [[C5a]] (complement system)
- Produce
	- NO, lysozymes
	- [[Cytokines]] – increase inflammation
	
**Alternative Pathway**
M2: wound healing and [[collagen]] deposition
- Induced by [[IL-4]] or [[IL-13]] NOT IFN-y
- NOT microbial 
- **TERMINATE INFLAMMATION** and cause tissue repair
- Produce [[IL-10]], TGFb, GF
#### Appearance
Large cells, irregular shaped euchromatic [[nucleus]]
- Commonly kidney-shaped 
Large amount of **eusinophilic** cytoplasm
