*thin, extracellular sheet of macromolecules where basal surface of all epithelia rests*

Some are thick enough that visible with H&E

![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 1.52.55 PM.png]]

Others are thin and need special stains to visualize
- Here a is H&E stain but only see membrane on PAS stain (b)

![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 1.55.42 PM.png]]

##### Composition
1) Basal Lamina: Layer closest to [[Epithelial Tissue|epithelial]] cells
	- Electron dense
	- Composed of
		- **Type IV [[Collagen]]**: structural support
		- **[[Laminin]]**: [[Glycoproteins|glycoprotein]] that attaches to transmembrane integrin proteins in basal cell membrane
2) Recticular Lamina: Layer created by underlying [[Connective Tissue]]
	- Looser, more diffused
	- Composed of
		- **Type III [[Collagen]]**: mainly used to anchor BM to connective tissue

![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 1.58.06 PM.png]]

##### Function
- Anchoring of overlying epithelial and underlying connective
- Filtration: semipermeable filter for substances going to epithelial from below
- Structure: allow quick epithelial repair and regeneration
