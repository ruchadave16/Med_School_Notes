### Etiology
Exposure
- Battery manufacturing, metallurgy
- Gun ranges
- Tableware with lead
- Drinking water
- Lead containing paint – homes before 1978
- Moonshine (illegally made alcohol)
### Pathophysiology
- Inhibition of aminolevulinate dehydratase and ferrochelate → [[heme synthesis]] disrupted → ↑ protoporphyrin and ↑ aminolevulinic acid  in [[Erythrocytes|RBCs]]
### Clinical Features

>[!Note] Symptoms
>LEAD
>- Lead Lines
>- Encephalopathy and erythrocyte stippling
>- Abdominal colic and sideroblastic anemia
>- Drops – wrist and foot drop

- Nervous system
	- Polyneuropathy, encephalopathy, headache, fatigue, muscle weakness, delirium, seizures
	- Peripheral neuropathy → paralysis of radial n. / peroneal nerve **FOOT DROP**
- Kidneys: nephropathy
- Anemia
- *Blue line on gums*
- *SEVERE abdominal pain*
- *Constipation*
- Hypertension
- Infertility
- *Irritability*
### Diagnosis
- Peripheral blood smear: **Basophilic stippling of [[Erythrocytes|erythrocytes]]** on peripheral blood smear
