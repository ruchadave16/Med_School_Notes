##### Rough Endoplasmic Reticulum 
*”Granular endoplasmic reticulum”*

- Made of tubular system of membranes with [[Ribosome|ribosomes]] 
- Continuous with [[nucleus]]
- Synthesis and modification of proteins 
	- Secreted from cell
	- Form other organelles
	- Integral membrane proteins
- Modifications
	- Glycosylation 
	- Sulfation, phosphorylation
	- Cleavage
	- Protein assembly
- Proteins isolated from cytoplasm
- Packaged to [[Golgi Apparatus]]

>[!Info]
>Glucosidases remove 3 glucose on oligosaccharides
>- If properly folded, leaves ER
>- If improper, glucosyl transferase recognizes and adds 1 glucose
>- Calnexin and calreticulin bind to proteins with this 1 glucose terminal
>	- Stops it from moving out of ER
>- ER Retention of misfolded proteins
>	- Mannose trimming and subsequent targeting for [[ERAD]]
>	- ↑ ER folding capacity
>	- ↓ global protein synthesis

##### Smooth Endoplasmic Reticulum
*”Agranular endoplasmic reticulum”*
Abundant in [[liver]]

- Forms network of membranes in cell
- Detoxification
- [[Steroid Hormones|Steroid hormone]] synthesis 
- [[Cholesterol]] metabolism
- Intracellular calcium buffering
- [[Glycogen]] breakdown (in liver)
- Lipid synthesis

>[!Clinical Consideration] [[Jaundice]]: 
> Yellowish skin discoloration caused by accumulation in extracellular fluid of bilirubin 
> 
> → Normally bilirubin metabolized by smooth ER in liver and excreted as bile
> → In newborn: underdeveloped smooth ER so bilirubin cannot be converted to water-soluble form
