Mainly made of integral membrane [[glycoproteins]] → 3 domains: extracellular, transmembrane, intracellular
##### Functions
- Control [[Cell Membrane|plasma membrane]] permeability
- Regulate [[Membrane Transport|entry of molecules]] into cells
- Bind [[Extracellular Matrix|ECM]] to [[Cytoskeleton|cytoskeleton]] through [[integrins]]
- Act as transducer to translate extracellular activity into intracellular response through secondary messenger system

>[!Clinical]
>**[[Familial hypercholesterolemia]]** → associated with low ability of cells to take in cholesterol which is normally ingested through endocytosis of LDLs
>	— Inherited genetic defect leads to inability to make LDL receptors or synthesis of defective receptors that cannot bind to LDLs or clatharin-coated pits
>	— Characterized by high cholesterol in bloodstream → leads to early atherosclerosis
>		
>**[[Venoms]]** → inactivate Ach receptors of skeletal muscle sarcolemma at neuromuscular junctions
>
>**Autoimmune** → autoantibodies made that bind to and activate certain cell membrane receptors
>
