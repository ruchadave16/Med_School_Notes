*Connections between cells to each other and [[Extracellular Matrix|ECM]]*

![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 2.29.56 PM.png]]


1) Tight Junction 
	- Names: Occluding Junction, Zonula Occludens
	- Location: Lateral domain towards apical side, makes band around cell
	- Composition: 2 transmembrane proteins attach to same protein in adjacent cell membrane **claudin and occludin**.
		- Connect to [[cytoskeleton]] via actin
	- Function: paracellular diffusion barrier
		- Prevent flow of water, ions, macromolecules
		- Maintain **cell polarity** 
![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 2.32.45 PM.png]]

2) Adherent Junction 
	- Names: Adherens Junction, Zonula Adherens
	- Location: Lateral domain towards apical side, right below tight junctions
		- Forms band around entire cell
	- Composition: **cadherins** → bind to same protein in adjacent cell and **catenins** → link between cadherin and actin
	- Function: anchor to neighbor cells
		- Resist separation due to mechanical stress
![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 2.38.09 PM.png]]

3) Desmosome 
	- Names: Macula Adherens
	- Location: Lateral domain in localized sites (NOT continuous)
	- Composition:
		- **Desmoglein and desmocollin**: bind to same molecules in neighbor cells
		- **Desmoplakin**: bind to intermediate filaments
	- Function: Like a spot weld
		- Strong cell adhesion and strength

![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 2.40.37 PM.png]]

4) Hemidesmosome
	- Location: Basal domain connecting cytoskeleton to [[basement membrane]]
		- Found in epithelia subject to shearing forces that would separate from connective tissue
	- Composition:
		- **Integrin**: transmembrane proteins
		- Bind to **Laminins** in [[basement membrane]]
		- Connect to intermediate filaments in cytoskeleton
	- Function:
		- Maintain integrity of epithelium-connective tissue interface
		- Strong connection at locations where likely to disrupt

![[Screenshot 2025-08-03 at 2.44.01 PM.png]]

5) Gap Junction
	- Name: Nexus, Communicating Junction
	- Location: Lateral domain in circular patches
	- Composition: **Connexin proteins** (6 subunits) make a channel which allows cytoplasm to cytoplasm connection
	- Function: Direct cell to cell signaling through small molecule transfer
