- Network of intrinsically disordered polypeptides
	- Permeability barrier
	- Makes up the central pore
	- Binding site for nuclear transport receptors (FG repeats)
#### Transport
- Bidirectional 
- Cytoplasm → nucleus:
	- histones, polymerases, transcription factors & RNA processing proteins, ribosomal proteins
- Nucleus → cytoplasm
	- tRNA, mRNA, pre-ribosomal subunits
- Both directions:
	- [[tumor suppressor genes]], transcription factors (transport is part of the signal transduction pathway), nuclear transport receptors (NTRs)

>[!Clinical]
>- Autoimmune Diseases: autoantibodies to Nups
>	- Primary biliary cirrhosis
>	- Mixed connective tissue diseases
>- Viral Infections
>	- Misregulation of NPC proteins
>	- Nuclear entry of viruses
>- Neurological Diseases
>	- [[Triple A syndrome]]
>	- Motor neuron degenerative disorders
>- Cancer
>	- Up or down regulation of NPC proteins and receptors
>- Cardiovascular Disorders
>	- Heart failure linked to change in Nup distribution
>- [[Oxidative Stress]]
>	- ROS can cause mislocalization of nuclear transport factors (NTFs)
