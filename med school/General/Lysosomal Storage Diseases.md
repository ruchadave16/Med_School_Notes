[[Glycosphingolipid]] storage disorders because of [[mutations]] in [[glycohydrolases]] that breakdown these glycosphingolipids in [[Lysosome|lysosomes]]

![[Screenshot 2026-01-10 at 5.32.53 PM.png]]

1) [[I Cell Disease]] – most severe
	- Defective GlcNAc phosphotransferase → catalyzes N-linked [[glycosylation]] of asparagine with M6P (mannose 6 phosphate) → secretion lysosome proteins

2) [[Tay-Sachs]]
	- [[Mutations|Mutation]] of B-hexosaminidase A so no catalytic activity (hydrolysis of **GalNAc**)
	- Accumulation of GM2
	- Kids between 3-6 months show disease
		- Commonly see “startle response”
	- Ashkenazi Jewish descent
	- Terminal between 3-4 age
	- Neuro symptoms – degeneration, seizures, blindness, muscle weakness
	- “cherry red” macula because retina accumulates GM2

3) [[Niemann-Pick Disease|Niemman-Pick Disease]]
4) [[Sandhoff Disease]] – can’t distinguish from Tay-Sachs often
5) [[Fabry Disease]] 
	- Most common death cause = cardiovascular disease
	- Male live ~60 yrs, Female 75 yrs

6) [[Gaucher Disease]]
	- Accumulation of glucocerebrosides (all removed but glucose) because defective B-glucosidase (Step 5 can’t happen)
	- Macrophages appear like “crumbled paper”
	- Most common disease
	- Hepatosplenomegaly, osteoporosis of long bones
	- Enzyme replacement therapy
	- Survival till 2 up to adulthood
