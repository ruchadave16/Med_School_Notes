[[Acidemia]] – blood pH ↓, depresses [[CNS]] (lowest = 6.8 for survival)
[[Alkalemia]] – blood pH ↑, excites [[CNS]] (highest = 7.7 for survival)

[[Acidosis]] – net gain of $H^+$ in fluids
[[Alkalosis]] – net loss of $H^+$ in fluids

Acids produced through metabolism **NORMAL pH = 7.4**
1) volatile $H_2CO_3$ from $CO_2$ = 15,000 mmol/day
2) nonvolatile acids from sulfur containing + phosphorylated AA
#### Regulation
1) [[Buffers]] : systems regulate immediately to minimize pH change
2) [[Lungs]] ([[Respiratory Regulation of Acid-Base|respiratory regulation]]): min-to-min regulation by changing $CO_2$ removal rate
3) [[Kidneys]] ([[Renal Regulation of Acid-Base|renal regulation]]): hours-to-days regulation that changes
	- Rate of renal tubular $H^+$ transport from tubular cell to renal tubule
	- Rate of $HCO_3^-$ reabsorption from tubule into tubular cell and $HCO_3^-$ formation by renal cell
