7 - 10 nm thick, visible through [[Microscopy|electron microscopy]]
	** Some tissues have “distinct cell borders” but this is not because of a thicker plasma membrane, but addition of cellular constituents

![[Pasted image 20250712173515.png]]


Fluid mosaic model: proteins clustered in lipid bilayer but move around
- Membrane asymmetry → proteins stay on their respective sides of membrane
- Membrane motility → move rapidly around membrane or can be held stable by intracellular structures

**FLUIDITY** determined by [[cholesterol]] and unsaturated [[Fatty Acids|fatty acid]] content and chain length
- As saturation increases, chains get longer, cholesterol increases, fluidity decreases
##### Components
- [[Lipids]]
	- [[Cholesterol]] (evenly distributed in both leaflets of bilayer)
	- Phospholipids (asymmetrical distribution)
	
			phosphatidyl-chlorine and sphingomyelin on extracellular surface
			phosphatidyl-serine and phosphatidyl-ethanolamine on intracellular surface
			
	- Glycolipids (only on outer leaflet of bilayer)
- [[Proteins]]
	- Integral proteins (traverse membrane)
	- Peripheral proteins (either extracellular or intracellular face)
- [[Carbohydrates]]
	- Linked to proteins and lipids
	- Glycolipids have oligosaccharide chains that form **glycocalyx** - surface coat that helps with cell adhesion and recognition

**Functions**: [[Membrane Transport|Transport]] and Cell-to-Cell Communication through [[Membrane Receptors|membrane receptors]]
#### Damage
- Causes: 
	- Bacterial toxin
	- Viral protein
	- Lytic complement components
- Damage mechanism
	- [[ROS]]
	- ↓ phospholipid synthesis
	- Cytoskeletal issues
- Consequence
	- Mitochondrial membrane = ↓ ATP, activation of [[caspade cascade]]
	- Plasma membrane = influx of sodium and water, leaking of contents
	- Lysosomal membrane = leaking of enzyme into cytoplasm → [[necrosis]]
