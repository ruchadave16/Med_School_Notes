*type of [[Epithelial Tissue|epithelium]] specialized in production and secretion of substances*
### Classification by Secretion Method
##### [[Endocrine System|Endocrine]] Glands
Secrete into [[Connective Tissue|connective tissue]] and then reach through bloodstream
- **Paracrine** Signaling: Directly diffuse into targets without entering bloodstream
- **Autocrine** Signaling: Signal acts on self
##### Exocrine Glands
Secrete onto surface directly or through epithelial ducts/tubes
- Merocrine
	- Delivered in membrane-bound vesicle to apical surface where it fuses to surface and releases product
	- Free outside cell (no membrane)
	- **Most common**
- Apocrine
	- Released in envelope of plasma membrane along with some cytoplasm
	- Found in **lactating [[Mammary Glands|mammary glands]]**
- Holocrine
	- Cell “blows up” releasing product
	- Form of [[Apoptosis|apoptosis]]
	- Seen in [[sebaceous glands]] of skin, [[Tarsal Glands]] of eyelid

### Classification by Cell Type
- Unicellular → Generally no ducts
	- **[[Goblet Cell]]**: 
		- Mucus-secreting cell amongst other columnar cells
		- Found in lining and glands of intestines and certain passages of respiratory tract
		- Shaped like goblet

### Classification by Quality of Material Secreted
##### [[Mucous Glands]]
- Slimy and viscous products with heavily glycosylated proteins and oligosaccharides
- Look “bubbly” on H&E
- Nucleus flattened against basal surface
- Examples
	- Goblet cells
	- Secretory cells of sublingual salivary glands
	- Surface cells of stomach
##### [[Serous Glands]]
- Watery secretions with poorly glycosylated proteins
- Look darker on H&E with eosinic staining at apical surface
- Can see secretory granules
- Nucleus is rounder at basal surface
- Examples
	- Parotid gland
	- Pancreatic exocrine gland
